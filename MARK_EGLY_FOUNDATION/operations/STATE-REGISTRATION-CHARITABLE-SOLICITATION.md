## 1. Overview & Legal Framework

### Why State Registration Matters

**Legal Requirement**: 41 states + DC require nonprofits to register before soliciting charitable contributions from residents. Solicitation = asking for donations (direct mail, email, social media, events, website donate button, peer-to-peer campaigns).

**Consequences of Non-Compliance**:

- **Fines & Penalties**: $500-$25,000 per violation (varies by state‚Äîsome daily penalties accumulate)
- **Cease & Desist Orders**: State Attorney General can prohibit fundraising until registered
- **Lawsuits**: Donors can sue for fraud/misrepresentation if not properly registered
- **Reputational Damage**: News coverage of compliance failures erodes donor trust
- **Loss of Tax-Exempt Status**: Egregious violations can trigger IRS review of 501(c)(3) status

**Foundation Context**: Mark Egly Foundation solicits nationwide (website, social media, national awareness campaigns, research partnerships across states) = must register in most states.

---

### Federal vs. State Authority

**IRS (Federal)**:

- Grants 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status (Form 1023 already completed)
- Requires annual Form 990 filing (public disclosure of financials, governance, programs)
- Does NOT regulate charitable solicitation‚Äîthat's state jurisdiction

**State Attorneys General & Secretaries of State**:

- Regulate charitable solicitation within their borders
- Require registration, annual reports, financial disclosures
- Enforce consumer protection laws (donors = consumers of charitable services)

## 2. Registration vs. Solicitation Licensing

### Two Types of State Compliance

**1. Foreign Qualification / Entity Registration**:

- Purpose: Authorize nonprofit to "do business" in state (open office, hire employees, hold property)
- Filed with: Secretary of State (usually Business Services division)
- Requirement: If Foundation establishes physical presence (office, employees) in state beyond home state (Ohio)
- Cost: $50-$200/state filing fee + $50-$150 annual report
- Example: If Foundation opens satellite office in California, must foreign qualify in California

**2. Charitable Solicitation Registration**:

- Purpose: Authorize soliciting donations from state residents
- Filed with: Attorney General or Secretary of State (Charities division)
- Requirement: If solicit donations from state residents (even remotely via website, mail, social media)
- Cost: $0-$400/state initial + annual renewal (see state matrix)
- Example: Foundation website has "Donate" button accessible to New York residents = must register in NY

**Key Distinction**: Can solicit without physical presence (charitable registration), but physical presence may trigger both registrations.

## 4. Unified Registration Statement (URS)

### What is URS?

**URS (Unified Registration Statement)**: Standardized multi-state registration form developed by National Association of State Charity Officials (NASCO) and National Association of Attorneys General (NAAG). Accepted by 41 states‚Äîfile once, cover most states.

**Benefits**:

- Single form = efficiency (don't complete 41 different state forms)
- Covers: organization info, mission, governance, programs, financials, fundraising activities
- Attachments: IRS determination letter, Form 990, financial statements, board list

---

### URS Sections

**Part A: Organization Info** (legal name, EIN, address, registration states, entity type)

**Part B: Purpose, Programs, Activities** (mission statement, program descriptions, beneficiaries served)

**Part C: Directors, Officers, Key Personnel** (names, addresses, compensation‚Äîtransparency for state regulators)

**Part D: Fundraising Activity** (revenue breakdown‚Äîcontributions, grants, events, other; expenses‚Äîprogram, management, fundraising)

**Part E: Financial Information** (balance sheet summary, revenue/expense summary‚Äîextracted from Form 990)

**Part F: Professional Fundraisers** (if use paid solicitors or consultants‚Äîlist name, address, compensation)

**Part G: Signature & Certification** (ED or Board Chair signs under penalty of perjury)

**Attachments**:

- IRS Form 1023 determination letter (501c3 approval)
- Most recent IRS Form 990 (public tax return)
- Audited financial statements (if required by state‚Äîtypically if >$500K-$1M revenue)
- State-specific supplements (some states require additional forms despite accepting URS)

---

### Where to File URS

**Multi-State Portal Options**:

1. **HarbourCompliance.com** ($500-$2,500/year for multi-state filing service‚Äîrecommended Year 1-2 for simplicity)
2. **CharityNet USA** (filing service + compliance calendar)
3. **Direct Filing**: Each state's Attorney General or Secretary of State Charities Division website (time-consuming‚Äî50+ hours/year for 41 states)

**Foundation Approach Year 1**: Use HarbourCompliance for initial 15-20 priority states (highest donor volume), expand to all 41 states Year 2-3.

## 5. Initial Registration Process

### Phase 1: Determine Where to Register

**Step 1: Analyze Donor Geography**:

- Review CRM (Salesforce): Where do donors live? (export mailing addresses, group by state)
- Review online donations: Google Analytics‚Äîvisitor geography for markeglyfoundation.org/donate
- Review event registrations: Alpha-1 Awareness Month participants by state
- Review grants: Foundation/corporate grants by funder state

**Prioritization** (Year 1):

- **Tier 1** (Must register immediately): Home state (Ohio) + top 5 donor states (e.g., CA, NY, FL, TX, PA)
- **Tier 2** (Register by Month 6): States 6-15 by donor volume
- **Tier 3** (Register by Year 2): Remaining states with any donor activity
- **Skip** (Year 1-2): States with zero donors and no planned solicitation campaigns

---

### Phase 2: Prepare URS Package

**Gather Documents** (one-time prep, reuse annually):

- [ ] IRS determination letter (501c3 approval‚Äîreceived after Form 1023)
- [ ] Certificate of Incorporation (Ohio Secretary of State‚ÄîArticles of Incorporation)
- [ ] Bylaws (current version, Board-approved)
- [ ] Form 990 (most recent filed‚ÄîYear 1 may file 990-EZ if <$200K revenue)
- [ ] Financial statements (balance sheet, income statement‚ÄîCPA-reviewed if budget allows, else internally prepared)
- [ ] Board of Directors list (names, addresses, affiliations‚Äîpublic info, demonstrates governance)
- [ ] Mission statement (1-2 paragraphs‚Äîfrom strategic plan or website About page)
- [ ] Program descriptions (Alpha-1 patient registry, patient navigation, research support, awareness campaigns‚Äî3-5 sentences each)

**Complete URS Form**:

- Use online URS tool (HarbourCompliance portal or CharityNet) or PDF fillable form
- Answer all questions accurately (states cross-check with Form 990‚Äîdiscrepancies trigger audit)
- ED and Board Chair review before signature

---

### Phase 3: File State-by-State

**Filing Methods**:

- **Online Portal** (preferred): Most states accept electronic filing (faster, confirmation receipt)
- **Mail**: Some states require paper + check (Florida, North Carolina‚Äîslower, 4-8 weeks processing)
- **Service Provider**: HarbourCompliance handles filing logistics (submit once, they distribute to states)

**Processing Time**: 2-8 weeks per state (plan ahead‚Äîdon't wait until solicitation campaign launch)

**Confirmation**: Save registration certificates/confirmation emails (audit trail‚Äîstates may not send physical certificates)

## 6. Annual Renewal & Reporting

### Renewal Requirements

**All 41 states require annual renewal** (even if no fundraising activity‚Äîmust file or formally withdraw registration)

**Renewal Components**:

1. **Updated URS** (if financials changed significantly) OR state-specific renewal form
2. **Updated Form 990** (year just filed with IRS)
3. **Renewal Fee** (varies by state‚Äîsee matrix)
4. **Financial Statements** (if revenue threshold met‚Äîe.g., CA requires audit if >$2M)

**Deadlines**: Vary by state (see matrix)‚Äîmost common:

- **4.5 months after FYE** (fiscal year-end): Many states (CA, MA, OR, PA, TN, VA)
- **6 months after FYE**: Many states (AK, CT, HI, ME, NH, NJ, NY, WI)
- **Anniversary of registration**: Some states (AL, CO, IL, KS, LA, MS, MO, ND, OK, UT)

**Example** (Foundation FYE = December 31):

- **May 15**: Renewal due in 4.5-month states (CA, MA, OR, PA, TN, VA)
- **June 30**: Renewal due in 6-month states (AK, CT, HI, ME, NH, NJ, NY, WI)
- **Various**: Anniversary states based on initial registration date

**COO Responsibilities** (Annual Cycle):

## 7. Exemptions & Thresholds

### Common Exemptions

**Revenue-Based Exemptions** (varies by state):

- **Alabama**: <$25K gross revenue ‚Üí exempt
- **Arizona**: <$50K gross revenue OR <$25K from AZ residents ‚Üí exempt
- **Colorado**: <$25K solicited ‚Üí register anyway (low threshold)
- **Pennsylvania**: <$25K revenue AND all volunteers (no paid staff/fundraisers) ‚Üí exempt

**Religious Organization Exemption**: Many states exempt churches, synagogues, mosques‚Äîbut NOT religious nonprofits like foundations (Foundation = not exempt under religious exception)

**Membership Organizations**: Some states exempt if solicit only members (not applicable‚ÄîFoundation solicits general public)

---

### Claiming Exemptions

**Risks**:

- Revenue can spike unpredictably (major donor gift, viral campaign pushes over threshold mid-year)
- "Solicitation" defined broadly (website donate button = solicitation in all states website accessible from)
- States may challenge exemption claims (audit, demand proof)

**Foundation Strategy**: Register proactively (don't rely on exemptions)‚Äîcompliance easier than defending exemption + penalties more expensive than registration fees

## 8. Online Fundraising Considerations

### Charleston Principles (NASCO Guidance)

**Issue**: Internet has no borders‚Äîdonate button on Foundation website accessible to all 50 states. Must register in all 41 states that require registration?

**Charleston Principles** (2001, updated 2019):

- **Passive Website** (general info, no solicitation): May not trigger registration
- **Active Solicitation** (donate button, specific appeals, online campaigns): Triggers registration in states where:
  - Foundation specifically targets state (geo-targeted ads, state-specific appeals)
  - Foundation receives donations from state repeatedly/regularly
  - Foundation has other contacts in state (events, volunteers, board members, grant relationships)

**Practical Standard**: If receive donations from state ‚â•2 times/year OR plan solicitation campaign targeting state ‚Üí register

## 9. Professional Fundraisers & Consultants

### State Requirements for Paid Fundraisers

**If Foundation hires professional fundraisers** (paid firms to solicit donations‚Äîtelemarketing, direct mail firms, online fundraising platforms with fees), many states require:

- Fundraiser registers separately in each state (not Foundation's responsibility but Foundation must verify)
- Written contract between Foundation and fundraiser filed with state
- Disclosure to donors (some states require fundraiser disclose % of donation retained as fee)

**Foundation Year 1-2**: Unlikely to use professional fundraisers (staff-driven, volunteer-driven fundraising)‚Äîbut if hire Grant Writer on commission or Fundraising Consultant who solicits directly, may trigger requirements

---

### Fundraising Counsel (Consultants)

**Distinction**:

- **Professional Fundraiser** (Solicitor): Directly asks for donations (telemarketer, paid canvasser)‚Äîheavily regulated
- **Fundraising Counsel** (Consultant): Advises on strategy, writes materials, but doesn't solicit‚Äîless regulated, often exempt

**Foundation Context**: Hiring Grant Writer or Development Consultant = fundraising counsel (exempt in most states)‚Äîbut verify if consultant will solicit directly (calls to donors, asks at events = solicitor, not counsel)

## 11. Penalties & Enforcement

### State Enforcement Actions

**Common Violations**:

1. **Failure to Register**: Soliciting without registration (most common‚Äî$1K-$5K fine per state)
2. **Failure to Renew**: Missed renewal deadline (late fees $100-$500 + reinstatement fee)
3. **Misrepresentation**: Inaccurate URS or Form 990 (fraud investigation‚Äîrare but severe)
4. **Failure to File Financial Reports**: Missing audited financials when required (suspension of registration)

**Penalties**:

- **Civil Fines**: $500-$25,000 per violation (some states per-day penalties‚Äîcan accumulate fast)
- **Cease & Desist**: State AG orders Foundation stop fundraising until compliant (immediate revenue loss)
- **Criminal Charges**: Rare‚Äîonly if intentional fraud, embezzlement (not inadvertent compliance lapse)

---

### Notable State Enforcement Examples

**New York**: Aggressive enforcement‚Äîfrequent audits, steep penalties ($10K+), publicizes enforcement actions (reputational harm)

**California**: High registration fees ($50) but strict compliance‚Äîrequires audit if >$2M revenue, scrutinizes Form 990 discrepancies

**Florida**: High initial registration fee ($400) as barrier to non-serious orgs‚Äîaudits common, quick to suspend non-compliant charities

**North Carolina**: Highest fees ($200/year)‚Äîvigorous enforcement, partners with IRS for data sharing

---

### Responding to State Inquiries

**If Receive Letter from State AG**:

1. **Don't Ignore**: Response required within 30 days (typically)‚Äîignoring escalates to enforcement
2. **Consult Legal Counsel**: Especially if fraud allegation, penalty notice, or subpoena
3. **Gather Documents**: State requesting proof of registration, financials, or donor records‚Äîassemble promptly
4. **Respond in Writing**: Professional tone, factual, attach supporting docs (registration confirmation, Form 990, bank statements if requested)
5. **Remediate**: If out of compliance, register immediately, pay back fees, submit overdue reports

**COO Responsibility**: Monitor Foundation email and physical mail (state notices may go to registered agent address‚Äîensure current)

## 14. Audits & Investigations

### Types of State Audits

**Routine Compliance Audit**:

- State AG reviews filed reports (Form 990, URS, financials) for accuracy
- May request additional info (bank statements, donor records, board meeting minutes)
- Typically resolved with document submission (no penalty if minor discrepancies explained)

**Investigative Audit**:

- Triggered by complaint (donor alleges fraud, funds misused, misleading solicitation)
- State subpoenas records, interviews staff, may involve forensic accountant
- Can result in penalties, restitution orders, or consent decrees

**Random Audit**:

- Some states (NY, CA, NC) randomly select % of registered charities for detailed review annually
- Ensures systemic compliance (not complaint-driven)

---

### Preparing for Audit

**Best Practices**:

1. **Maintain Organized Records**: Financial records, board minutes, donor correspondence, policies‚Äîreadily accessible
2. **Respond Promptly**: State auditors expect response within 10-30 days‚Äîdon't delay
3. **Be Cooperative**: Professional tone, provide requested documents, don't obstruct
4. **Engage Counsel if Serious**: If criminal investigation or fraud allegation, hire attorney immediately (don't speak to auditors without counsel)
5. **Remediate Findings**: If audit identifies deficiencies (missing policies, inadequate board oversight), fix immediately and document corrective actions

## 15. Best Practices & Risk Mitigation

### Proactive Compliance Strategies

**1. Register Early & Broadly**:

- Don't wait until state contacts Foundation‚Äîregister proactively in top donor states
- Avoid relying on exemptions (revenue thresholds unpredictable‚Äîregister anyway)

**2. Use Compliance Service**:

- HarbourCompliance or CharityNet‚Äîworth cost for time savings and deadline tracking
- Frees COO to focus on mission, not compliance paperwork

**3. Centralize Donor Data**:

- CRM (Salesforce) tracks donor state, donation frequency‚Äîenables geographic analysis for registration planning
- Annual donor report by state (January‚Äîinform registration decisions)

**4. Monitor State Law Changes**:

- States periodically change fees, deadlines, thresholds‚Äîsubscribe to NASCO newsletter, HarbourCompliance alerts
- Legal counsel monitors significant changes (e.g., CA increased audit threshold 2024)

**5. Document Everything**:

- Save registration confirmations, renewal receipts, state correspondence‚Äîaudit trail
- If state claims non-compliance, can prove filed timely

**6. Train Staff on Solicitation Policies**:

- All staff understand "solicitation" definition (asking for donations = solicitation‚Äîincludes social media posts, email appeals, event invites)
