## Retirement Plan (401k) Administration

### Plan Design

**Plan Type**: Traditional 401(k) + Roth 401(k) option (employee chooses pre-tax or after-tax contributions)

**Contribution Limits** (2025 IRS limits):

- **Employee**: $23,000/year (under age 50), $30,500/year (age 50+)

### Loans & Hardship Withdrawals

**Loans**:

- **Allowed**: Yes (common 401k featureâ€”employee borrows from own account, repays with interest to self)
- **Amount**: Up to 50% of vested balance (max $50,000)
- **Repayment**: 5 years (deducted from paycheckâ€”e.g., $10K loan = $175/month for 60 months at 5% interest)
- **Consequence**: If leave Foundation, loan due within 60 days or treated as taxable distribution (10% penalty if under age 59.5)

**Hardship Withdrawals**:

- **Allowed**: Yes (IRS-approved hardships onlyâ€”medical, home purchase, tuition, eviction/foreclosure prevention, funeral)
- **Amount**: Minimum necessary (must exhaust other resources firstâ€”savings, loans)
- **Tax**: Taxable income + 10% penalty (no penalty if age 59.5+, disabled, or death)
- **Not Repaid**: Permanent withdrawal (reduces retirement savings)

**Foundation Policy**: Discourage hardships (offer EAP financial counseling, emergency loans from emergency fund if established Year 2-3â€”small $500-$2K loans at 0% interest, repaid via payroll)

### Long-Term Disability (LTD)

**Coverage**: 60% of salary after 90 days disability, continuing until age 65 or recovery (whichever earlier)

**Cost**: Foundation-paid (~$0.50 per $100 of payrollâ€”e.g., $50K salary = $250/year)

## Flexible Spending Accounts (FSA)

### Healthcare FSA

**Purpose**: Pre-tax savings for out-of-pocket medical expenses (copays, deductibles, prescriptions, glasses, dental, orthodontia)

**Contribution Limit**: $3,200/year (2025 IRS limitâ€”employee elects annual amount, divided by pay periods)

**Eligible Expenses** (IRS Publication 502):

- Medical/dental copays, deductibles, coinsurance
- Prescription drugs (not over-the-counter without prescription)
- Glasses, contacts, LASIK
- Orthodontia (bracesâ€”can claim full cost in year paid, even if treatment spans 2 years)
- Chiropractic, acupuncture, mental health (if not covered by insurance)

**Ineligible Expenses**:

- Health insurance premiums (already pre-tax via Section 125 cafeteria plan)
- Over-the-counter drugs (unless prescribedâ€”aspirin, vitamins)
- Cosmetic procedures (Botox, teeth whitening)

**Use-It-Or-Lose-It**:

- **Rule**: Funds must be used by December 31 (+ grace period 2.5 months = March 15 following year)
- **Carryover**: $640 carryover allowed (2025 IRS limitâ€”e.g., contribute $3,200, spend $2,600, carry over $600 to next year)
- **Forfeit**: Remaining funds after March 15 forfeited to Foundation (IRS ruleâ€”Foundation may use for plan admin costs or refund prorated to participants)

---

### Dependent Care FSA

**Purpose**: Pre-tax savings for childcare, eldercare (daycare, preschool, after-school programs, adult daycare for disabled parent)

**Contribution Limit**: $5,000/year (married filing jointly or single), $2,500 (married filing separately)

**Eligible Expenses**:

- Daycare (children under 13)
- Preschool (not kindergarten tuitionâ€”IRS considers that education, not care)
- Summer day camp (not overnight camp)
- After-school care
- Adult daycare (disabled spouse or parent)

**Ineligible Expenses**:

- Kindergarten through 12th grade tuition
- Overnight camp
- Care provided by dependent (e.g., 16-year-old sibling watching younger siblingâ€”not eligible)

**Reimbursement Timing**: Claims paid as incurred (no front-loading like Healthcare FSAâ€”employee must incur expense before reimbursement)

---

### FSA Administration

**Third-Party Administrator** (TPA): WageWorks, HealthEquity, Payflex (carrier handles claims, debit cards, customer serviceâ€”Foundation pays admin fee $5-$10/employee/month)

**Employee Claims Process**:

1. Incur eligible expense (pay doctor, pharmacy, daycare)
2. Submit claim (online portal or mobile appâ€”upload receipt, explain expense)
3. TPA reviews (1-3 business daysâ€”verify eligible per IRS rules)

## COBRA Administration

### What is COBRA?

**Law**: Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act (1985)â€”requires employers with â‰¥20 employees offer continuation of group health insurance to former employees/dependents

**Applicability**: Foundation subject to COBRA once reaches 20 employees (projected Year 2-3)

**Qualifying Events**:

- **Employee**: Termination (voluntary or involuntary except gross misconduct), reduction in hours (<30/week, loses eligibility)
- **Spouse**: Divorce, legal separation, employee death, employee Medicare-eligible
- **Child**: Loses dependent status (age 26), parent's divorce

---

### COBRA Process

**Employer Responsibilities** (COO):

1. **Initial Notice** (within 90 days of plan coverage): Provide COBRA rights notice to all covered employees (one-time, typically at enrollment)
2. **Qualifying Event Notice** (within 30 days of event): When employee terminates, COO sends COBRA election notice to employee + covered dependents
3. **Election Period** (60 days): Former employee decides whether to elect COBRA (no response = waived)
4. **Coverage Continuation** (if elected): 18 months (employee/dependent), 36 months (spouse/child if divorce/death)

**Employee Responsibilities**:

- Pay premium (102% of full costâ€”100% premium + 2% admin feeâ€”e.g., $800/month employer-paid becomes $816/month employee-paid)
- Pay on time (45 days for first payment, 30 days for subsequentâ€”late = coverage terminated, no reinstatement)

---

### COBRA Cost Example

**Active Employee**:

- Premium: $800/month
- Foundation pays: 80% = $640
- Employee pays: 20% = $160 (deducted from paycheck)

**COBRA (Former Employee)**:

- Premium: $800/month Ã— 102% = $816/month
- Employee pays: 100% = $816 (no employer contribution, must mail check or ACH)

**Why Expensive**: COBRA is often unaffordable ($816/month = $9,792/year for single coverage), causing many to decline and instead enroll in ACA marketplace (healthcare.govâ€”may qualify for subsidies if income <400% FPL)

---

### COBRA Vendor

**Third-Party Administrator** (TPA): WageWorks COBRA, Payflex, Conexis (handles notices, election tracking, premium collection, IRS complianceâ€”Foundation pays $5-$15/qualified beneficiary/month)

**Why Outsource**: COBRA compliance is complex (strict timelines, penalty $100/day per person for notice failuresâ€”$36,500/year for single missed notice). TPA ensures compliance, Foundation avoids DOL audits/penalties.

## Life Event Changes

### Mid-Year Election Changes (Special Enrollment)

**Normally**: Benefits elections locked until next open enrollment (November)

**Exception**: IRS-approved "life events" allow mid-year changes (must change within 30 days of event)

**Qualifying Life Events**:

1. **Marriage**: Add spouse to health/dental/vision
2. **Divorce**: Remove ex-spouse (COBRA offered to ex-spouse)
3. **Birth/Adoption**: Add new child
