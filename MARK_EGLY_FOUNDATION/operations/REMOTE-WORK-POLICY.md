## Remote Work Philosophy

### Foundation's Approach to Flexible Work

**Mission-First Flexibility**: Remote work is a tool to support the mission‚Äîattract top talent regardless of location, reduce overhead costs, and accommodate employee needs (childcare, health conditions, geographic preferences)‚Äînot an entitlement, but a privilege contingent on performance and business needs.

**Trust & Accountability**: Foundation trusts employees to manage their time and deliver results. Remote work succeeds when employees demonstrate self-discipline, communication, and ownership of outcomes.

**Hybrid-First** (Year 1-3): Most roles hybrid (2-3 days in office, 2-3 days remote)‚Äîbalances collaboration with flexibility

**Remote-First** (Year 4+): As Foundation matures, consider remote-first (office optional, fully distributed team)‚Äîexpands talent pool nationwide, reduces real estate costs

---

### Benefits of Remote Work

**For Employees**:

- ‚úÖ **Work-Life Balance**: Eliminate commute (average 30 min each way = 5 hours/week saved), flexibility for childcare/eldercare, doctor appointments
- ‚úÖ **Productivity**: Fewer office distractions (open-plan noise, drop-by interruptions), deep work time
- ‚úÖ **Health**: Reduce exposure to illness (flu, COVID), better ergonomics (customize home setup), mental health (less commute stress)
- ‚úÖ **Cost Savings**: Gas, parking, professional wardrobe, lunch out ($200-$400/month saved)

**For Foundation**:

- ‚úÖ **Talent Acquisition**: Recruit nationwide (not limited to Columbus, OH‚Äîaccess rare skills like Alpha-1 clinical expertise, rare disease research)
- ‚úÖ **Retention**: 54% of employees would leave job for remote option (FlexJobs 2024)‚Äîoffering remote reduces turnover 10-15%
- ‚úÖ **Cost Reduction**: Smaller office footprint (hot-desking, not dedicated desks‚Äîsave $5K-$10K/employee/year on rent)
- ‚úÖ **Business Continuity**: Office closure (snow, fire, pandemic) doesn't halt operations‚Äîremote infrastructure already in place

---

### Limitations & Challenges

**Not Suitable for All Roles**:

- ‚ùå **In-Person Patient Support**: Patient navigators meeting newly diagnosed patients (emotional support, trust-building‚Äîhard to replicate via Zoom)
- ‚ùå **Lab Work**: Biobank sample processing (requires physical presence in lab‚ÄîYear 3+ if Foundation operates biobank)
- ‚ùå **Event Management**: Conferences, fundraising galas (on-site coordination required)

**Potential Downsides**:

- **Isolation**: Remote employees may feel disconnected from team, mission (mitigate: regular video check-ins, quarterly in-person all-staff meetings)
- **Communication Lag**: Asynchronous communication slower than tapping colleague's shoulder (mitigate: establish response time expectations, use Slack for quick questions)
- **Security Risk**: Home networks less secure than office (mitigate: VPN, encrypted devices, security training)

## Eligibility & Approval Process

### Eligibility Criteria

**Position Requirements** (All must be met):

- [ ] **Job Function**: Role can be performed remotely (primarily computer-based‚Äîgrant writing, data analysis, fundraising, research coordination)
- [ ] **Performance**: Meets or exceeds expectations (not on PIP, no attendance/productivity issues)
- [ ] **Tenure**: Completed 90-day probation (new hires work in office for onboarding, relationship-building)
- [ ] **Self-Management**: Demonstrates self-discipline, time management, communication (can work independently without constant supervision)
- [ ] **Technology**: Reliable home internet (‚â•25 Mbps download, ‚â•5 Mbps upload‚Äîverify via speedtest.net), dedicated workspace (not couch or kitchen table‚Äîergonomic setup)

**Ineligible Roles** (Year 1-2):

- Executive Director (in-office presence required for Board liaison, donor meetings, media interviews‚Äîmay consider 1 day/week remote Year 3+)
- Roles requiring in-person patient interaction (unless accommodation under ADA)
- Roles handling physical documents (if Foundation receives paper mail, donor checks‚ÄîYear 1 may have administrative role that scans/processes)

---

### Approval Process

**Step 1: Employee Request** (Submit to Supervisor):

- **Form**: Remote Work Request (Google Form‚Äîincludes work arrangement, rationale, proposed schedule, home office setup description)
- **Lead Time**: 2 weeks minimum (supervisor evaluates, discusses with COO, prepares approval/denial)

**Step 2: Supervisor Evaluation**:

- Review request against eligibility criteria
- Consider business needs (team coverage, collaboration requirements, meeting schedules)
- Discuss with COO (consistency across Foundation‚Äîensure similar roles treated equally)

**Step 3: Trial Period** (Optional, 30-90 days):

- Supervisor may approve trial (temporary remote work‚Äîassess productivity, communication, technology reliability)
- If successful, convert to permanent; if issues, return to in-office or modify arrangement

**Step 4: Approval/Denial**:

- Approved: Document in personnel file, update Google Calendar availability, inform team
- Denied: Explain rationale (role not suitable, performance concerns, business needs), offer alternatives (compressed workweek, flex hours)

**Revocation**: Foundation may revoke remote work approval at any time (business needs change, performance declines, security breach)‚Äîtypically 30 days notice unless immediate (e.g., termination, gross misconduct)

## Work Arrangements

### Hybrid Schedule (Most Common Year 1-3)

**Definition**: Split time between office and home (typically 2-3 days office, 2-3 days remote)

**Example Schedules**:

- **Monday, Wednesday, Friday**: In office (team collaboration, meetings, whiteboarding)
- **Tuesday, Thursday**: Remote (deep work‚Äîwriting grants, data analysis, report creation)

**Core In-Office Days** (Foundation-Wide):

- **Wednesday**: All staff in office (all-hands meetings, team lunches, collaboration‚Äîbuild culture)
- **Flexibility**: Other days employee chooses (coordinate with team‚Äîensure adequate coverage)

**Process**:

1. Employee proposes schedule (days in office vs. remote)
2. Supervisor approves (considers team needs‚Äîat least one team member in office each day for phones, mail)
3. Update Google Calendar (mark "In Office" vs. "Remote" daily‚Äîtransparency for meeting scheduling)

---

### Fully Remote (Rare Year 1-2, More Common Year 3+)

**Definition**: Work from home full-time (office visits occasional‚Äîmonthly, quarterly, or as needed)

**Eligibility**:

- **Geographic**: Live >50 miles from office (daily commute unreasonable‚Äîe.g., employee lives Cleveland, office Columbus = 140 miles)
- **Accommodation**: ADA accommodation (immunocompromised, disability prevents commute)
- **Business Need**: Rare skill not available locally (e.g., Alpha-1 researcher lives Boston, willing to work remotely‚Äîhire anyway rather than lose talent)

**Requirements**:

- Quarterly in-person attendance (all-staff meetings, annual planning, team-building)
- Foundation covers travel costs (flight, hotel, meals‚Äîsee Expense Management System)

---

### Occasional Remote (Default for In-Office Roles)

**Definition**: Primarily in-office, occasional remote days (1-2 days/month)

**Use Cases**:

- **Sick Child**: Stay home to care for child (not sick enough for PTO, but can't come to office)
- **Inclement Weather**: Snow, ice, flooding (safety concern‚Äîwork from home rather than risk commute)
- **Appointments**: Morning doctor appointment (work from home morning, come to office afternoon)
- **Deep Work**: Grant deadline (minimize distractions‚Äîwork from home to focus)

**Approval**: Supervisor approval same-day (email or Slack: "Working from home today due to snow‚Äîavailable on Slack/email")

---

### Digital Nomad / Temporary Relocation (Year 3+)

**Definition**: Work remotely from different location temporarily (2 weeks‚Äì3 months‚Äîe.g., spend winter in Florida, summer in Michigan, visit family in California)

**Requirements**:

- Pre-approval required (2 weeks notice minimum‚Äîsupervisor ensures no business disruption)
- US-only (Year 1-3‚Äîinternational remote work complicates tax, legal, security; consider Year 4+ with attorney guidance)
- Maintain work hours in Eastern Time Zone (if relocate to California, work 6am-2pm Pacific = 9am-5pm Eastern‚Äîattend meetings)
- Reliable internet (verify before relocation‚ÄîAirbnb with fiber, not rural cabin with satellite)

## Home Office Setup & Equipment

### Workspace Requirements

**Dedicated Workspace**:

- **Minimum**: Desk, chair, quiet space (door preferred‚Äîminimize distractions from family, pets, roommates)
- **Not Acceptable**: Couch, bed, kitchen table (short-term okay‚Äîe.g., occasional remote day‚Äîbut not sustainable for hybrid/full-remote)
- **Ergonomics**: Monitor at eye level, keyboard/mouse ergonomic, chair adjustable (prevent repetitive strain injuries)

**Environment**:

- **Quiet**: Minimize background noise (no barking dogs, crying babies on calls‚Äîuse headset, mute when not speaking)
- **Professional**: Video call background neutral (bookshelf, plain wall‚Äînot messy bedroom, laundry visible; virtual background okay if doesn't glitch)
- **Lighting**: Adequate lighting for video calls (face visible, not backlit by window)

---

### Foundation-Provided Equipment

**Standard Remote Work Kit** (Provided to all hybrid/remote employees):

- [ ] **Laptop**: MacBook Air or Dell XPS (Foundation-issued, encrypted, configured with VPN, 1Password, security software)
- [ ] **Monitor**: 24" external monitor ($150-$200‚Äîreduces neck strain, dual-screen productivity)
- [ ] **Keyboard & Mouse**: Wireless ergonomic ($50-$100‚ÄîLogitech, Microsoft)

**Optional Equipment** (Available upon request):

## Technology & Security Requirements

### Internet & Connectivity

**Minimum Speed**:

- **Download**: 25 Mbps (video calls, cloud apps)
- **Upload**: 5 Mbps (video calls‚Äîupload = your face/voice to others)
- **Latency**: <50 ms (responsive apps‚Äîhigh latency = laggy Zoom, slow Salesforce)

**Test Before Approval**:

- Visit speedtest.net ‚Üí Run test ‚Üí Screenshot results ‚Üí Submit with remote work request
- If below minimums: Upgrade internet (Foundation reimburses $20/month increase‚Äîsee Reimbursement section), or use mobile hotspot, or ineligible for remote work

**Backup Internet**:

- If home internet fails during workday, employee must:
  1. Switch to mobile hotspot (tethering from phone‚Äîadequate for email, light browsing, Zoom if LTE/5G)
  2. Notify supervisor (expected to restore connectivity within 2 hours or use PTO if can't work)
  3. If chronic issues (>2 outages/month), remote work privilege may be revoked

---

### VPN & Secure Access

**VPN Requirement**: ALL remote work must use Foundation VPN (Virtual Private Network‚Äîencrypts internet traffic, masks IP address, protects from coffee shop eavesdropping)

**VPN Provider**: Tailscale ($10/user/month) or Cloudflare Zero Trust (free tier adequate Year 1-2)

**How to Use**:

1. Open VPN app (installed by IT on Foundation laptop)
2. Log in (SSO via Google‚Äîauthenticate identity)
3. Connect (click "Connect"‚Äîtraffic now routed through Foundation VPN, encrypted)
4. Work as normal (access Salesforce, REDCap, Google‚Äîsystems see Foundation IP, not home IP)

**When Required**:

- **Always**: If accessing PHI (REDCap, Salesforce patient records)
- **Recommended**: All work (even non-PHI‚Äîemail, Asana‚Äîprotects against home network compromise)

---

### Device Security

**Laptop Security Checklist** (IT enforces, employee verifies):

- [x] **Full-Disk Encryption**: FileVault (Mac) or BitLocker (Windows)‚Äîif laptop stolen, data unreadable
- [x] **Antivirus**: Microsoft Defender (Windows) or Malwarebytes (Mac)‚Äîscans daily, auto-updates
- [x] **Firewall**: Enabled (blocks unauthorized inbound connections)
- [x] **Screen Lock**: Auto-lock after 5 minutes idle (password or biometric required‚Äîprevents family member accessing work if walk away)
- [x] **Automatic Updates**: OS and apps auto-update (security patches installed within 7 days of release)
- [x] **No Shared Accounts**: Laptop = employee only (spouse, kids don't use‚Äîmalware risk, accidental deletion)

**Mobile Device Security** (if using personal phone for work‚Äî2FA, Foundation apps):

- [x] **Passcode**: 6+ digits (not 1234, birthday)
- [x] **Biometric**: Face ID or fingerprint (faster, more secure than passcode alone)
- [x] **Find My Device**: Enabled (remote wipe if lost/stolen)
- [x] **No Jailbreaking/Rooting** (disables security‚Äîif detected, revoke work phone access)

---

### Data Protection

**Prohibited on Remote Devices**:

- ‚ùå **Saving PHI Locally**: Patient data must stay in REDCap, Salesforce (cloud‚Äîbacked up, encrypted)‚Äînot downloaded to laptop Desktop or Documents folder
- ‚ùå **Personal Cloud Storage**: No uploading Foundation data to personal Dropbox, Google Drive, iCloud (data leak risk)‚Äîuse Foundation Google Drive only
- ‚ùå **USB Drives**: No copying Foundation data to USB (loss risk, no encryption)‚Äîunless encrypted USB approved by COO

**If Device Lost/Stolen**:

1. **Immediately** notify IT Manager + COO (call, text, email‚Äî24/7 response)
2. IT remotely wipes device (all data erased‚ÄîFind My iPhone, Windows/Mac remote wipe)
3. File police report (theft = crime, need report for insurance claim)
4. Foundation investigates (was data accessed? PHI exposed? ‚Üí HIPAA breach report if yes)

## Performance & Productivity

### Performance Measurement

**Output-Based** (Not Hours-Based):

- Remote employees evaluated on deliverables, not time spent online (finish grant proposal by Friday = success, whether worked 20 hours or 40)
- Exception: Non-exempt hourly employees must track hours (FLSA compliance‚Äîsubmit timesheet daily via Asana)

**Key Performance Indicators** (Role-Dependent):

- **Grant Writer**: # grants submitted, success rate, $ raised
- **Patient Navigator**: # patients served, satisfaction scores, response time
- **Research Coordinator**: Registry enrollment rate, data quality, publication contributions

**Red Flags** (Supervisor monitors):

- **Missed Deadlines**: Repeatedly late (if pattern, discuss‚Äîtechnical issues? overwhelmed? time management?)
- **Low Responsiveness**: Don't reply to Slack, emails within expected timeframes (isolated? disengaged?)
- **Quality Decline**: Work errors increase (distracted at home? need support?)

---

### Productivity Tools & Monitoring

**Time Tracking** (Non-Exempt Only):

- Use Asana time tracking or Toggl (clock in/out, log hours by task)
- Supervisor reviews weekly (ensure 40 hours worked, overtime pre-approved)

**Activity Monitoring** (Not Implemented Year 1-2, Consider Year 3+ if Productivity Concerns):

- **Avoid**: Invasive monitoring (keystroke logging, webcam screenshots‚Äîkills trust, demotivates)
- **If Necessary**: Light monitoring (Slack active status, email activity‚Äîbut only if performance issues, not blanket surveillance)

**Trust-But-Verify**:

- Assume employees working until evidence otherwise (innocent until proven guilty)
- If suspicion (not delivering, unreachable during core hours), supervisor documents, counsels, may require in-office return or termination

## Legal & Compliance

### Workers' Compensation

**Coverage**: Remote work injuries covered (trip over laptop cable, carpal tunnel from poor ergonomics‚Äîif during work hours, performing work duties)

**Reporting**: Employee must report immediately (COO files workers' comp claim, same as office injury)

**Prevention**: Foundation provides ergonomic equipment (monitor, keyboard, chair‚Äîreduces injury risk)

---

### Tax Implications

**State Income Tax**:

- **Work in Same State as Office** (Ohio): No issue (employee pays Ohio income tax, Foundation withholds Ohio)
- **Work in Different State** (e.g., remote employee lives Michigan): Complex (Michigan income tax on days worked in Michigan, Ohio tax on days in Ohio office‚Äîemployer may need to register in multiple states, withhold accordingly)
- **Foundation Policy Year 1-2**: Hire only Ohio residents (avoid multi-state tax complexity‚ÄîYear 3+ consult CPA, payroll service can handle)

**Home Office Deduction** (Employee Personal Tax Return):

- IRS allows home office deduction IF:
  - Dedicated space (room or portion of room used exclusively for work‚Äînot kitchen table)
  - Regular and exclusive use (not guest bedroom used occasionally for work)
- Deduction = % of home expenses (rent/mortgage, utilities, insurance‚Äîe.g., 10% of home = office ‚Üí deduct 10% of $1,200/month rent = $1,440/year)
- **Note**: Foundation reimbursements reduce deduction (can't double-dip‚Äîif Foundation pays $40/month internet, employee can't deduct that $40)

---

### Liability & Insurance

**Homeowner's/Renter's Insurance**: Employee should notify insurance (using home for business‚Äîsome policies exclude business use, may need rider‚Äî$50-$100/year)

**Foundation Insurance**: General liability covers remote employees (if patient visits employee's home for meeting‚Äîrare‚ÄîFoundation covered if injury/property damage)

---

### Out-of-State Remote Work (Year 3+)

**Considerations Before Hiring Out-of-State**:

- [ ] **State Registration**: Register Foundation as foreign corporation (if employee = nexus, must register to do business in state‚Äîfiling fee $50-$500, annual report $50-$200/year)
- [ ] **Payroll Tax**: Register with state unemployment, withholding tax (complexity = 50 states √ó different rules)
- [ ] **Workers' Comp**: Purchase coverage in employee's state (premium varies by state‚ÄîCalifornia expensive, Texas cheaper)
- [ ] **Employment Law**: Comply with state laws (California = stricter meal breaks, paid sick leave, than Ohio‚ÄîHR must track state-specific rules)

**Foundation Decision**: Evaluate cost vs. benefit (hire rare talent in California = worth $5K/year compliance cost; hire interchangeable skill in Indiana = not worth it, hire local Ohio instead)
