### Volunteer Program Principles

**1. Mission-Aligned**

- Every volunteer role advances our mission

- Impact is measured and celebrated

- Volunteers gain skills, connections, and fulfillment
- Foundation gains capacity and expertise

**4. Inclusive & Accessible**

- Opportunities for diverse skills and time commitments

- Accommodations for disabilities

**5. Recognized & Appreciated**

- Regular thank-yous and recognition
- Annual volunteer appreciation event
- Volunteer awards and milestones celebrated

## Volunteer Opportunities

### Volunteer Role Categories

#### 1. Patient Support Volunteers

**Support Group Facilitators**

- **Commitment**: 2-4 hours/month
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Facilitate monthly support group meetings (virtual or in-person)
  - Create welcoming, safe space for patients
  - Share resources and information
  - Connect patients to foundation services
- **Qualifications**:
  - AATD patient, family member, or caregiver strongly preferred
  - Empathetic listener
  - Comfortable facilitating groups
  - Completed facilitator training
- **Year 1 Need**: 25 facilitators
- **Training**: 4-hour facilitator training + monthly supervisor check-ins

**Patient Navigation Assistants**

- **Commitment**: 5-10 hours/month
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Support paid patient navigators with administrative tasks
  - Help patients find resources
  - Make reminder calls for appointments
  - Follow up on assistance applications
- **Qualifications**:
  - Healthcare or social services background helpful
  - Organized, detail-oriented
  - Compassionate and patient
  - HIPAA training required
- **Year 1 Need**: 5 assistants
- **Training**: 8-hour orientation + HIPAA certification

**Alpha1Life.com Community Moderators**

- **Commitment**: 3-5 hours/week
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Monitor discussion forums daily
  - Welcome new members
  - Answer questions or escalate to staff
  - Enforce community guidelines
  - Flag inappropriate content
- **Qualifications**:
  - AATD patient or family member preferred
  - Active on social media/online communities
  - Good judgment and communication skills
  - Completed moderator training
- **Year 1 Need**: 5 moderators
- **Training**: 2-hour moderator training + ongoing support

---

#### 2. Education & Outreach Volunteers

**Physician Outreach Ambassadors**

- **Commitment**: 5-10 hours/month
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Contact physicians to promote CME course
  - Share educational materials with medical offices
  - Follow up on leads for AATD Aware certification
  - Report activity in CRM
- **Qualifications**:
  - Sales or outreach experience helpful
  - Comfortable making cold calls
  - Knowledgeable about AATD
  - Completed training on CME program
- **Year 1 Need**: 10 ambassadors
- **Training**: 3-hour training on outreach scripts and CRM

**Public Awareness Volunteers**

- **Commitment**: Flexible (event-based)
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Staff awareness booths at health fairs, races, events
  - Distribute educational materials
  - Engage public and answer questions
  - Collect contact information for follow-up
- **Qualifications**:
  - Outgoing, friendly demeanor
  - Knowledgeable about AATD
  - Comfortable speaking with strangers
- **Year 1 Need**: 20 volunteers (for multiple events)
- **Training**: 1-hour event volunteer orientation

**Patient Storytellers**

- **Commitment**: 1-2 stories/year
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Share personal AATD diagnostic journey
  - Participate in video interviews or write testimonials
  - Speak at events or to media (optional)
  - Allow story to be used in fundraising/advocacy
- **Qualifications**:
  - AATD patient or family member
  - Willing to share personal story publicly
  - Comfortable with video/written format
- **Year 1 Need**: 20 storytellers
- **Training**: Storytelling workshop (2 hours)

---

#### 3. Advocacy Volunteers

**Grassroots Advocates**

- **Commitment**: 3-5 hours/month
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Contact legislators (calls, emails, meetings)
  - Mobilize others to take action
  - Attend legislative events and rallies
  - Share advocacy alerts on social media
  - Testify at hearings (if comfortable)
- **Qualifications**:
  - Passionate about policy change
  - Willing to engage with elected officials
  - AATD patient or family member strongly preferred
  - Completed advocacy training
- **Year 1 Need**: 100 advocates (across 10 target states)
- **Training**: 2-hour advocacy training + ongoing issue briefs

**Legislative Champions**

- **Commitment**: 5-10 hours/month
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Lead advocacy efforts in their state
  - Build relationships with key legislators
  - Coordinate local advocacy events
  - Recruit and mentor other advocates
  - Represent foundation at state capitol
- **Qualifications**:
  - Strong advocacy experience
  - Existing legislative connections a plus
  - Leadership and organizing skills
  - Strategic thinker
- **Year 1 Need**: 10 champions (1 per target state)
- **Training**: 4-hour champion training + monthly strategy calls

---

#### 4. Fundraising Volunteers

**Event Volunteers**

- **Commitment**: 1-2 events/year (4-8 hours each)
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Setup and breakdown for fundraising events
  - Registration and check-in

  - Recruit attendees and donors
  - Manage event logistics
  - Serve as day-of event leads
- **Qualifications**:
  - Event planning experience
  - Strong networks for sponsorship/attendance
  - Project management skills
  - Fundraising experience a plus
- **Year 1 Need**: 8-10 committee members
- **Training**: Committee orientation + monthly planning meetings

**Corporate Engagement Volunteers**

- **Commitment**: 5-10 hours/month
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Leverage corporate connections for partnerships
  - Introduce foundation to corporate giving programs
  - Promote employee giving campaigns

- **Qualifications**:
  - Corporate connections (current or former employee)
  - Understands corporate giving landscape
  - Comfortable making introductions
- **Year 1 Need**: 10 volunteers
- **Training**: Corporate partnerships overview (1 hour)

### Volunteer Application Process

**Step 1: Online Application** (5-10 minutes)

- Basic contact information
- Areas of interest (check boxes for role types)
- Skills and experience
- Availability (hours/week, days/times)
- How they heard about us
- Why they want to volunteer

**Step 2: Application Review** (within 1 week)

- Volunteer Coordinator reviews applications
- Matches volunteers to appropriate roles
- Contacts volunteer to schedule interview

**Step 3: Volunteer Interview** (30 minutes, phone or video)

- Learn about volunteer's background and interests
- Explain volunteer opportunities in detail
- Assess fit for specific roles
- Answer questions
- Discuss next steps

**Step 4: Background Check** (for certain roles)

- Patient-facing roles (support, navigation)
- Roles with access to sensitive data
- Roles involving minors (if applicable)

**Step 5: Orientation & Training** (see Training section)

**Step 6: Placement & First Assignment**

- Volunteer matched to specific role
- Introduced to staff supervisor or volunteer lead
- Assigned first project or shift

## Volunteer Management

### Volunteer Coordinator Role

**Position**: Volunteer Coordinator (0.5 FTE, Year 1)

**Reports To**: Executive Director (Year 1), HR Manager (Year 3+)

**Responsibilities**:

- Recruit, screen, and onboard volunteers
- Match volunteers to appropriate roles
- Provide orientation and training
- Supervise and support volunteers
- Track volunteer hours and impact
- Recognize and appreciate volunteers
- Maintain volunteer database

---

### Volunteer Supervision Structure

**Model**: Each volunteer has a designated **staff or volunteer supervisor**

**Supervisor Responsibilities**:

- Orient volunteer to specific role
- Assign tasks and projects
- Provide ongoing guidance and support
- Check in regularly (weekly or monthly depending on role)
- Troubleshoot issues
- Provide feedback and recognition
- Report volunteer hours to Volunteer Coordinator

**Example Supervision Assignments**:

- Support Group Facilitators ‚Üí Patient Services Director
- Physician Outreach Ambassadors ‚Üí Medical Education Director
- Grassroots Advocates ‚Üí Advocacy Director
- Event Volunteers ‚Üí Development Director
- Professional Skills Volunteers ‚Üí Relevant staff (e.g., Communications Manager for marketing volunteers)

## Policies & Procedures

### Volunteer Agreement

**All volunteers sign Volunteer Agreement covering**:

- Roles and responsibilities
- Time commitment expectations
- Confidentiality and privacy (HIPAA if applicable)
- Code of conduct
- Photography/media release
- Conflict of interest disclosure
- At-will volunteer relationship (can end anytime)

---

### Confidentiality & HIPAA

**All Volunteers**:

- Sign confidentiality agreement
- Do not share internal foundation information
- Do not share donor information publicly

**Patient-Facing Volunteers**:

- Complete HIPAA training
- Sign HIPAA compliance agreement
- Understand penalties for violations
- Report any breaches immediately

---

### Code of Conduct

**Expected Behaviors**:

- Treat everyone with respect and dignity
- Represent foundation professionally
- Follow all policies and procedures
- Communicate openly and honestly
- Ask for help when needed

**Prohibited Behaviors**:

- Harassment, discrimination, or bullying
- Misrepresentation of foundation
- Solicitation for personal gain
- Political activity on behalf of foundation
- Violation of confidentiality

**Consequences**: Volunteers who violate code of conduct may be removed from program

---

### Volunteer Safety

**Foundation Responsibility**:

- Provide safe volunteer environment
- Clear instructions and proper equipment
- Workers' compensation insurance (if applicable)
- Background checks for appropriate roles

**Volunteer Responsibility**:

- Follow safety guidelines
- Report hazards or injuries immediately
- Use common sense and good judgment

---

### Volunteer Expenses

**Reimbursable**:

- Mileage for approved volunteer activities (IRS rate)
- Parking and tolls
- Pre-approved supplies or materials

**Non-Reimbursable**:

- Time spent volunteering (volunteers donate time)
- Meals (unless pre-approved for multi-day event)

**Process**:

- Submit expense reimbursement form with receipts
- Approval by volunteer supervisor
- Reimbursement within 30 days

---

### Volunteer Tax Deductions

**Volunteers CAN Deduct** (consult tax professional):

- Mileage for volunteer activities
- Out-of-pocket expenses (if not reimbursed)
- Travel expenses for volunteer trips

**Volunteers CANNOT Deduct**:

- Value of time donated
- Professional services at usual rate

**Foundation Provides**:

- Volunteer hour tracking for records
- Mileage logs
- Tax-exempt status letter (EIN)

## Impact Measurement

### Volunteer Program Metrics

**Quantitative Metrics**:

- **Number of Active Volunteers**: Volunteered in last 90 days
  - Year 1 Target: 50
  - Year 5 Target: 250

- **Total Volunteer Hours**:
  - Year 1 Target: 2,000 hours
  - Year 5 Target: 15,000 hours

- **Volunteer Hour Value** (Independent Sector rate: ~$30/hour):
  - Year 1 Target: $60,000 in-kind value
  - Year 5 Target: $450,000 in-kind value

- **Volunteer Retention Rate**: % of volunteers active year-over-year
  - Target: 70%+

- **Volunteer Satisfaction (NPS)**: Net Promoter Score from annual survey

**Qualitative Metrics**:

- Volunteer testimonials and stories
- Staff feedback on volunteer contributions
- Impact stories (e.g., advocate who helped pass legislation)
