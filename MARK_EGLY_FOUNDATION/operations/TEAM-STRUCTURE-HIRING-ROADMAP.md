## Organizational Structure Overview

### Guiding Principles

**1. Mission-Driven Team**

- Every role directly advances our mission to eliminate AATD diagnostic delays
- Patient outcomes drive all staffing decisions
- Lean team maximizes resources for programs

**2. Expertise & Passion**

- Hire people with relevant expertise (medical, nonprofit, advocacy)
- Passion for rare disease advocacy essential
- Lived experience (AATD patients/families) valued

**3. Sustainable Growth**

- Scale team in line with revenue growth
- Build leadership capacity for future growth
- Invest in staff development

**4. Culture of Excellence**

- High-performing, collaborative team
- Commitment to learning and improvement
- Transparent, accountable, supportive environment

## Year-by-Year Hiring Roadmap

### Year 1 (Nov 2025 - Oct 2026): Core Team

**Goal**: Establish foundation infrastructure and launch pilot programs

**Budget**: $500K total / ~$300K personnel (~60%)

**Hires** (4 positions, 4.0 FTE):

**Q1 (Nov-Jan)**:

**1. Executive Director** (FTE 1.0, Salary: $85K + benefits)

- **Start Date**: December 1, 2025
- **Reports to**: Board of Directors
- **Key Responsibilities**:
  - Overall strategic leadership and execution
  - Staff management and development
  - Board liaison and support
  - Partnership development
  - Spokesperson (secondary to Mark)
- **Qualifications**:
  - 10+ years nonprofit leadership experience
  - Healthcare or rare disease background preferred
  - Proven fundraising track record
  - Strategic thinker and relationship builder
- **Hiring Priority**: #1 (critical for launch)

**2. Development Director** (FTE 1.0, Salary: $65K + benefits)

- **Start Date**: January 15, 2026
- **Reports to**: Executive Director
- **Key Responsibilities**:
  - Lead all fundraising efforts ($500K Year 1 goal)
  - Major donor cultivation and solicitation
  - Grant writing and reporting
  - Fundraising event planning
  - Donor database management
- **Qualifications**:
  - 5+ years fundraising experience
  - Track record of securing major gifts
  - Grant writing expertise
  - Relationship-oriented, entrepreneurial
- **Hiring Priority**: #2 (funding is critical)

**Q2 (Feb-Apr)**:

**3. Program Manager** (FTE 1.0, Salary: $55K + benefits)

- **Start Date**: March 1, 2026
- **Reports to**: Executive Director
- **Key Responsibilities**:
  - Coordinate all program activities (Education, Support, Research, Advocacy)
  - Website and Alpha1Life.com project management
  - Event coordination
  - Program data tracking
  - Vendor/contractor management
- **Qualifications**:
  - 3-5 years program management experience
  - Healthcare or nonprofit background
  - Project management skills (PMP a plus)
  - Detail-oriented, organized, tech-savvy
- **Hiring Priority**: #3

**4. Administrative Coordinator** (FTE 1.0, Salary: $42K + benefits)

- **Start Date**: April 1, 2026
- **Reports to**: Executive Director
- **Key Responsibilities**:
  - Office management and operations
  - Bookkeeping support (QuickBooks)
  - Board meeting coordination
  - Donor acknowledgment letters
  - General administrative support
- **Qualifications**:
  - 2-3 years administrative experience
  - Nonprofit experience preferred
  - QuickBooks knowledge
  - Excellent communication skills
  - Self-starter, adaptable
- **Hiring Priority**: #4

**Contractors/Part-Time** (Year 1):

- Communications Manager (0.5 FTE contractor, $30K)
- Finance Manager / Bookkeeper (0.25 FTE contractor, $15K)
- IT Support (as needed, $5K)
- Web Developer (project-based, $20K)

**Total Year 1 Personnel**: $317K (63% of budget)

### Application Review

**Process**:

1. Applications reviewed by hiring manager + ED
2. Phone screens with top 5-8 candidates (30 min)
3. First interviews with top 3-5 candidates (1 hour)
4. Second interviews with top 2-3 candidates (panel)
5. Reference checks (3 references)
6. Background check (if applicable)
7. Offer extended

**Timeline**: 4-6 weeks from posting to offer

---

### Interview Process

**Phone Screen** (30 minutes):

- Review resume and experience
- Assess basic qualifications and fit
- Discuss salary expectations
- Gauge interest level

**First Interview** (1 hour):

- Behavioral questions (STAR method)
- Mission alignment and passion
- Technical skills and experience
- Cultural fit

**Second Interview** (1-2 hours):

- Panel interview (ED + 2-3 staff/board)
- Deeper dive on experience
- Scenario/case study questions
- Meet potential colleagues

**Sample Interview Questions**:

- "Tell me about a time you had to overcome a significant challenge in your work."
- "Why are you passionate about rare disease advocacy?"
- "How do you prioritize when you have multiple urgent deadlines?"
- "Describe your experience with [specific skill for role]."
- "What questions do you have for us?"

---

### Reference Checks

**Process**:

- Request 3 professional references (supervisors preferred)
- Call references to verify employment and performance
- Ask about strengths, areas for growth, and fit

**Sample Questions**:

- "How did [candidate] perform in their role?"
- "What are their greatest strengths?"
- "What areas could they develop further?"
- "Would you hire them again? Why or why not?"
- "Is there anything else we should know?"

### Goal Setting

- Adjusted as needed based on priorities

**Example Employee OKRs**:

**Development Director**:

### Performance Conversations

**Quarterly Check-Ins** (informal):

- Progress on OKRs
- Challenges and support needed
- Feedback (both directions)
- Adjust priorities as needed

**Annual Performance Review** (formal):

- Self-assessment submitted by employee
- Manager assessment and rating
- Discussion meeting (1-2 hours)
- Performance rating assigned
- Goals set for next year
- Salary adjustment discussed

**Performance Ratings**:

- **Exceeds Expectations** (20%): Outstanding performance, exceeds all goals
- **Meets Expectations** (70%): Strong performance, meets or exceeds most goals
- **Needs Improvement** (10%): Some goals not met, improvement plan required

---

### Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)

**When Used**: Performance consistently below expectations

**Process**:

1. Manager documents performance concerns
2. Meeting with employee to discuss concerns
3. Written PIP created (specific goals, timeline, support)
4. Regular check-ins during PIP period (weekly or bi-weekly)
5. Final review: Performance improved OR termination

**PIP Duration**: Typically 60-90 days
