## Governance Overview

### Mission & Purpose

The Mark Egly Foundation is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization dedicated to eliminating diagnostic delays for Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency (AATD) through education, support, research, and advocacy.

The Board of Directors provides **governance, strategic direction, and fiduciary oversight** to ensure the foundation achieves its mission effectively, ethically, and sustainably.

---

### Governance Principles

**1. Mission-Driven**

- Every decision prioritizes advancing our mission
- Patient outcomes guide all strategic choices
- Impact measurement drives accountability

**2. Fiduciary Responsibility**

- Prudent financial management
- Asset protection and sustainability
- Transparent reporting to stakeholders

**3. Strategic Leadership**

- Set long-term vision and strategy
- Monitor progress toward goals
- Adapt to changing landscape

**4. Ethical Operation**

- Highest standards of integrity
- Conflicts of interest disclosed and managed
- Compliance with all laws and regulations

**5. Diversity & Inclusion**

- Board composition reflects communities we serve
- Multiple perspectives strengthen decision-making
- Inclusive culture welcomes all voices

---

### Board Terms

**Term Length**: 3 years

**Term Limits**: Two consecutive 3-year terms (6 years max)

- After serving 6 years, members must rotate off for at least 1 year
- Can be re-elected after 1 year break

**Rationale**:

- 3-year terms allow deep engagement and strategic thinking
- Term limits ensure fresh perspectives and prevent stagnation
- Re-election option retains valuable institutional knowledge

**Staggered Terms**:

- Approximately 1/3 of board rotates each year
- Ensures continuity while allowing for renewal

## Board Meetings

### Meeting Frequency

**Quarterly Board Meetings**: 4 meetings per year

- February, May, August, November (2-3 hours each)
- In-person preferred (virtual option available)

**Annual Board Retreat**: 1 full-day retreat per year

- Strategic planning and deep-dive discussions
- Team building and board development
- Typically held in Q4 to plan next year

**Special Meetings**: Called as needed by Chair or majority of board

---

### Meeting Structure

**Typical Agenda** (2.5 hours):

**1. Opening** (15 min)

- Welcome and mission moment (patient story)
- Approve minutes from last meeting
- Conflicts of interest declaration

**2. Executive Director Report** (30 min)

- Progress on strategic goals (OKRs)
- Program updates (Education, Support, Research, Advocacy)
- Wins, challenges, learnings
- Q&A from board

**3. Financial Report** (20 min)

- Treasurer presents financial statements
- Budget vs. actual
- Fundraising update
- Q&A

**4. Committee Reports** (30 min)

- Finance & Audit Committee
- Development Committee
- Program Committee
- Governance Committee

**5. Strategic Discussion** (40 min)

- Deep dive on one strategic topic (rotates quarterly)
- Examples: CME program effectiveness, legislative strategy, research partnerships, major gift cultivation

**6. Action Items** (10 min)

- Votes on matters requiring board approval
- Budget amendments, policies, major expenditures, etc.

**7. Executive Session** (15 min, if needed)

- Board-only discussion (no staff)
- Executive Director evaluation, compensation, sensitive matters

**8. Closing** (5 min)

- Recap action items and next steps
- Set next meeting date

---

### Meeting Materials

**Distributed 1 week before meeting**:

- Agenda
- Previous meeting minutes
- Executive Director report
- Financial statements
- Committee reports
- Any materials requiring board action

**Board members expected to**:

- Read materials before meeting
- Come prepared with questions
- Engage actively in discussion

---

### Decision-Making

**Quorum**: Majority of board members (e.g., 3 of 5, or 5 of 9)

**Voting**:

- Majority vote required for approval
- Voice vote for routine matters
- Roll call vote for significant matters (budget, policies, etc.)
- Abstentions recorded but don't count toward majority

**Matters Requiring Board Approval**:

- Strategic plan and annual goals
- Annual budget (and amendments >10%)
- Expenditures >$50K not in approved budget
- New programs or significant program changes
- Hiring/firing Executive Director
- Executive Director compensation
- Governance policies (bylaws, conflict of interest, etc.)
- Real estate transactions
- Debt incurrence
- Acceptance of gifts with restrictions

## Bylaws

### Article I: Name and Purpose

**Section 1.1 - Name**
The name of this organization shall be the **Mark Egly Foundation**.

**Section 1.2 - Purpose**
The foundation is organized exclusively for charitable, scientific, and educational purposes under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, specifically to eliminate diagnostic delays for Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency (AATD) through education, support, research, and advocacy.

---

### Article II: Board of Directors

**Section 2.1 - Governing Body**
The foundation shall be governed by a Board of Directors consisting of not fewer than 5 and not more than 9 members.

**Section 2.2 - Powers and Duties**
The board shall have full power and authority to manage the affairs of the foundation, including but not limited to:

- Adopting policies and procedures
- Approving budgets and financial plans
- Hiring and evaluating the Executive Director
- Amending these bylaws

**Section 2.3 - Qualifications**
Board members shall be individuals committed to the mission of the foundation and willing to fulfill the responsibilities outlined in the Board Member Agreement.

**Section 2.4 - Terms**
Board members shall serve three-year terms and may serve up to two consecutive terms. After two consecutive terms, a member must rotate off for at least one year before being eligible for re-election.

**Section 2.5 - Election**
Board members shall be elected by majority vote of the existing board upon recommendation of the Governance Committee.

**Section 2.6 - Resignation and Removal**

- Board members may resign by written notice to the Board Chair
- A member may be removed for cause by two-thirds vote of the board
- Cause includes failure to fulfill board responsibilities, unresolved conflicts of interest, or conduct detrimental to the foundation

**Section 2.7 - Vacancies**
Vacancies on the board shall be filled by majority vote of the remaining board members. A member elected to fill a vacancy shall serve the remainder of the term.

---

### Article III: Officers

**Section 3.1 - Officers**
The officers of the foundation shall be: Chair, Vice Chair, Treasurer, and Secretary.

**Section 3.2 - Election and Term**
Officers shall be elected by the board from among its members for one-year terms. Officers may be re-elected annually.

**Section 3.3 - Duties**
Officer duties are as defined in Section "Board Roles & Responsibilities" of this document.

**Section 3.4 - Removal**
Officers may be removed by two-thirds vote of the board.

---

### Article IV: Meetings

**Section 4.1 - Regular Meetings**
The board shall hold at least four regular meetings per year, scheduled quarterly.

**Section 4.2 - Special Meetings**
Special meetings may be called by the Chair or by written request of one-third of board members, with at least 7 days' notice.

**Section 4.3 - Notice**
Notice of meetings shall be provided at least 7 days in advance (14 days for annual meeting) via email or written communication.

**Section 4.4 - Quorum**
A majority of board members shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.

**Section 4.5 - Voting**
Each board member shall have one vote. Decisions shall be made by majority vote of those present at a meeting where quorum is met, except where these bylaws require a supermajority.

**Section 4.6 - Remote Participation**
Board members may participate in meetings via telephone or video conference, which constitutes presence for quorum and voting purposes.

**Section 4.7 - Action Without Meeting**
The board may take action without a meeting if all members consent in writing (including email).

---

### Article V: Committees

**Section 5.1 - Standing Committees**
The board may establish standing committees as needed to conduct the foundation's business.

**Section 5.2 - Committee Authority**
Committees shall have authority delegated by the board but cannot take final action on matters requiring board approval.

**Section 5.3 - Committee Composition**
Committees shall be composed of board members and may include non-board members as advisors (non-voting).

---

### Article VI: Executive Director

**Section 6.1 - Appointment**
The board shall appoint an Executive Director who serves at the pleasure of the board.

**Section 6.2 - Duties**
The Executive Director shall be responsible for day-to-day management of the foundation, implementation of policies and programs approved by the board, and supervision of staff.

**Section 6.3 - Authority**
The Executive Director shall have authority to make operational decisions and expenditures within the approved budget, subject to limitations established by board policy.

**Section 6.4 - Evaluation**
The Executive Director shall be evaluated annually by the board based on agreed-upon performance goals.

---

### Article VII: Conflicts of Interest

**Section 7.1 - Policy**
The foundation shall maintain a Conflict of Interest Policy requiring board members, officers, and key staff to disclose potential conflicts and recuse themselves from decisions where conflicts exist.

**Section 7.2 - Annual Disclosure**
All board members shall complete an annual Conflict of Interest Disclosure Statement.

---

### Article VIII: Finances

**Section 8.1 - Fiscal Year**
The fiscal year shall be November 1 through October 31.

**Section 8.2 - Budget**
The board shall approve an annual budget before the start of each fiscal year.

**Section 8.3 - Financial Controls**
The board shall ensure appropriate financial controls, including:

- Segregation of duties
- Regular financial reporting
- Annual independent audit (when budget exceeds $250K)

**Section 8.4 - Checks and Contracts**
Checks and contracts shall be signed according to authority limits established by board policy.

---

### Article IX: Indemnification

**Section 9.1 - Indemnification**
The foundation shall indemnify board members, officers, and staff to the fullest extent permitted by law for actions taken in good faith in their official capacity.

**Section 9.2 - Insurance**
The foundation shall obtain Directors & Officers (D&O) liability insurance.

---

### Article X: Amendments

**Section 10.1 - Amendment Process**
These bylaws may be amended by two-thirds vote of the board, provided that notice of the proposed amendment is included in the meeting notice at least 14 days in advance.

---

### Article XI: Dissolution

**Section 11.1 - Dissolution**
In the event of dissolution, assets shall be distributed to one or more organizations exempt under Section 501(c)(3) with similar missions, as determined by the board.

## Conflict of Interest Policy

### Purpose

To protect the foundation's interests when considering transactions or arrangements that might benefit the private interest of a board member, officer, or key employee.

---

### Definitions

**Conflict of Interest**: A conflict exists when a person in a position of authority has a financial or personal interest that could influence their judgment regarding the foundation's best interests.

**Financial Interest**: A person has a financial interest if they have, directly or indirectly, through business, investment, or family:

- An ownership or investment interest in any entity doing business with the foundation
- A compensation arrangement with any entity doing business with the foundation
- A potential ownership or investment interest or compensation arrangement

---

### Procedures

**1. Annual Disclosure**
All board members shall annually disclose:

- Business affiliations
- Board service on other organizations
- Family relationships that might create conflicts
- Financial interests in entities that do business with foundation

**2. Transaction Review**
Before any transaction involving a potential conflict:

- Interested person discloses the conflict
- Interested person provides all material facts
- Interested person leaves the meeting during discussion and vote
- Remaining board determines if conflict exists and whether transaction is in foundation's best interest

**3. Documentation**
All conflict disclosures and decisions shall be documented in board minutes.

---

### Examples of Potential Conflicts

**Transactions Requiring Disclosure**:

- Board member's company provides services to foundation
- Foundation makes grant to organization where board member serves
- Board member's family member is candidate for staff position
- Board member has financial interest in research partnership

**Permitted Relationships** (with disclosure and approval):

- Board member volunteers professional services at reduced rates
- Board member's employer provides matching gift to foundation
- Board member introduces foundation to potential partner (no personal benefit)

## Board Development

### Board Recruitment

**Process**:

1. Governance Committee assesses board composition gaps
2. Committee develops target board profile
3. Committee identifies candidates (recommendations from board, staff, partners)
4. Chair and/or committee members meet with candidates
5. Candidate attends board meeting as observer
6. Committee recommends candidate to full board
7. Board votes on new member

**Candidate Evaluation Criteria**:

- Mission alignment and passion
- Needed expertise or perspective
- Ability to fulfill board responsibilities (time, giving, service)
- Character and integrity
- Cultural fit and collaborative approach

---
