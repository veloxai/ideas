## Crisis Management Philosophy

### Foundation's Crisis Response Principles

**Principle 1: Patient Safety First**
Any crisis response prioritizes patient well-being above organizational reputation or financial considerations.

**Principle 2: Transparency & Honesty**
Foundation communicates honestly about crisis scope, impact, and responseâ€”no cover-ups, no minimizing, no spin (except legally privileged).

**Principle 3: Rapid Response**
Act within 1-4 hours of crisis identificationâ€”silence fuels speculation, swift action demonstrates control.

**Principle 4: Unified Voice**
Only designated spokespersons communicate externallyâ€”prevents contradictory messages, rumors, unauthorized statements.

**Principle 5: Preserve Mission Continuity**
Crisis response protects Foundation's ability to serve AATD patients long-termâ€”short-term pain acceptable if preserves mission.

---

### Crisis vs. Issue vs. Incident

**Crisis**: Event requiring immediate, coordinated response across organizationâ€”threatens reputation, operations, or financial viability
- Examples: Data breach exposing 5,000 patient records, Executive Director accused of fraud, major donor publicly withdraws support alleging mismanagement

**Issue**: Ongoing challenge requiring management attention but not immediate crisis response
- Examples: Staff turnover, budget deficit, declining donor retention

**Incident**: One-time problem handled through normal operations
- Examples: Support group leader sick (find substitute), website down 2 hours (IT fixes), grant application rejected (apply elsewhere)

**Decision Rule**: If you're asking "Is this a crisis?"â€”convene Crisis Management Team to decide. Better to mobilize unnecessarily than delay response to actual crisis.

## Crisis Types & Definitions

### Category 1: Public Relations Crises

**Definition**: Events damaging Foundation's reputation, stakeholder trust, or public perception

**Examples**:
- **Board/Staff Misconduct**: Executive Director arrested for DUI, Board member accused of sexual harassment
- **Financial Scandal**: Allegations of misappropriated donor funds, excessive executive compensation
- **Patient Harm**: Foundation's advice alleged to have harmed patient (e.g., delayed diagnosis due to bad referral)
- **Discrimination Claims**: Lawsuit alleging Foundation discriminated against employee or patient based on protected class
- **Controversial Statement**: Board member or staff makes offensive public statement, Foundation associated with backlash
- **Partnership Scandal**: Pharmaceutical partner accused of price gouging, Foundation criticized for accepting funding

**Impact**: Loss of donor trust, media scrutiny, Board resignations, patient community distrust

---

### Category 2: Operational Disruptions

**Definition**: Events preventing Foundation from delivering services or conducting normal operations

**Examples**:
- **Natural Disaster**: Earthquake, flood destroys office, staff unable to work
- **Pandemic/Public Health Emergency**: COVID-like event forces office closure, event cancellations
- **IT System Failure**: Website, patient portal, database down for 72+ hours
- **Vendor Failure**: Critical vendor (payroll, CRM, fundraising platform) goes bankrupt or breaches contract
- **Facility Loss**: Office lease terminated suddenly, forced to relocate
- **Key Staff Loss**: Executive Director dies suddenly, multiple staff quit simultaneously

**Impact**: Inability to serve patients, missed grant deadlines, lost revenue, stakeholder frustration

---

### Category 3: Financial Emergencies

**Definition**: Events threatening Foundation's financial stability or solvency

**Examples**:
- **Major Donor Withdrawal**: Largest donor ($500K+/year) withdraws support, creating budget crisis
- **Grant Loss**: Federal grant ($1M+) not renewed, 30% budget shortfall
- **Fraud/Embezzlement**: Staff member steals $50K+, requiring emergency audit
- **Investment Loss**: Market crash decimates endowment value (if Foundation has endowment)
- **Unexpected Liability**: Lawsuit judgment exceeds insurance coverage, Foundation must pay $500K+
- **Fundraising Shortfall**: Annual gala nets $50K (expected $200K), creating cash flow crisis

**Impact**: Layoffs, program cuts, inability to pay vendors, potential insolvency

---

### Category 4: Cybersecurity Incidents

**Definition**: Breaches, hacks, or cyber attacks compromising data, systems, or operations

**Examples**:
- **Data Breach**: Hacker accesses patient registry, 5,000 PHI records exposed
- **Ransomware**: Systems locked, attackers demand $100K Bitcoin payment
- **Phishing Success**: Staff member tricked into wiring $50K to scammer
- **Website Defacement**: Attacker replaces Foundation website with offensive content
- **Insider Threat**: Employee downloads patient database before quitting, threatens to sell data
- **DDoS Attack**: Website unavailable for 48 hours due to distributed denial-of-service attack

**Impact**: HIPAA violations, patient trust loss, regulatory fines, operational disruption, reputational harm

---

### Category 5: Leadership Transitions (Unplanned)

**Definition**: Sudden loss or unavailability of key leadership requiring emergency succession

**Examples**:
- **Executive Director Death/Serious Illness**: ED has heart attack, hospitalized indefinitely
- **Executive Director Termination**: Board fires ED for cause, immediate departure
- **Board Chair Resignation**: Chair resigns mid-crisis or mid-year, leadership vacuum
- **Mass Resignations**: 3+ Board members resign simultaneously (e.g., after scandal)
- **Key Staff Departure**: Development Director, Research Director quit without notice mid-project

**Impact**: Leadership vacuum, strategic direction uncertainty, stakeholder anxiety, operational disruption

---

### Category 6: Legal & Regulatory Crises

**Definition**: Lawsuits, investigations, or regulatory actions threatening Foundation

**Examples**:
- **Employment Lawsuit**: Employee sues for wrongful termination, sexual harassment, discrimination
- **IRS Investigation**: 501(c)(3) status challenged, audit of political activity
- **State Attorney General Investigation**: Charitable solicitation violations, misuse of funds
- **HIPAA Investigation**: HHS Office for Civil Rights investigates data breach, potential $1.5M fine
- **Qui Tam (Whistleblower) Lawsuit**: Former employee alleges grant fraud, government joins lawsuit
- **Defamation Lawsuit**: Foundation accused of defaming competitor organization or individual

**Impact**: Legal fees ($100K-$500K+), reputational damage, potential loss of 501(c)(3) status, fines

- Data breach affecting >100 patients

**Discretionary CMT Activation** (CMT Leader + 1 other member agree):
- Potentially damaging media story in development
- Staff misconduct requiring investigation
- Significant financial shortfall
- Key partnership at risk
- Internal conflict threatening operations

**Decision Rule**: When in doubt, activateâ€”1 hour CMT meeting costs $500, missed crisis costs $500K.

## Crisis Severity Levels

### Level 1: Minor Crisis (Localized Impact)

**Definition**: Crisis affecting single program, department, or limited stakeholder groupâ€”manageable within normal operations with CMT oversight

**Examples**:
- Support group leader misconduct (one group affected)
- Small data breach (<50 patients)
- Single negative media story (local outlet)
- Staff member terminated for cause, no legal threat
- Event cancellation due to low attendance

**Response Time**: 4-24 hours
**CMT Involvement**: CMT informed, Operations Lead handles with oversight
**Communication**: Limited (affected parties only, internal memo)
**Budget Impact**: <$10,000
**Duration**: Resolved within 1 week

---

### Level 2: Moderate Crisis (Organizational Impact)

**Definition**: Crisis affecting multiple stakeholder groups or threatening Foundation's reputation/operationsâ€”requires coordinated CMT response

**Examples**:
- Data breach (50-500 patients)
- Staff misconduct requiring external investigation
- Major donor withdraws support ($50K-$100K)
- Negative media coverage (regional/national outlet)
- Key staff departure mid-project
- Cybersecurity incident (systems down 24-72 hours)
- Employment lawsuit filed

**Response Time**: 1-4 hours
**CMT Involvement**: Full CMT convened within 4 hours, meets daily
**Communication**: Stakeholder notifications (affected parties, Board, key donors), public statement if media inquiry
**Budget Impact**: $10,000-$100,000
**Duration**: 2-4 weeks to resolve

---

### Level 3: Major Crisis (Existential Threat)

**Definition**: Crisis threatening Foundation's survival, mission continuity, or requiring fundamental changesâ€”full CMT mobilization, external experts, Board oversight

**Examples**:
- Data breach (>500 patients), class action lawsuit likely
- Executive Director scandal (fraud, embezzlement, sexual misconduct)
- Multiple Board resignations
- Loss of 501(c)(3) status
- Natural disaster destroying office, records
- Ransomware with systems down >72 hours
- Major grant loss ($500K+), budget crisis
- Patient death attributed to Foundation's negligence
- Federal investigation (IRS, DOJ, HHS)

**Response Time**: <1 hour (CMT mobilizes immediately)
**CMT Involvement**: Full CMT + Board Executive Committee, meets twice daily, 24/7 on-call
**Communication**: Full stakeholder notifications (all donors, patients, partners, media), holding statement within 2 hours, press conference if warranted
**Budget Impact**: $100,000-$1,000,000+
**Duration**: 1-6 months to resolve, long-term reputation repair

## Crisis Response Protocols

### Phase 1: Crisis Identification & Activation (Hour 0-1)

**Step 1: Crisis Identified** (Any staff member can identify potential crisis)

**How Crises Are Discovered**:
- Staff observes incident (data breach alert, patient complaint, media inquiry)
- External party notifies Foundation (attorney, insurance carrier, media, donor)
- Monitoring systems detect (cybersecurity alerts, social media monitoring)
- Whistleblower report (internal hotline, Board member notification)

**Immediate Action**: Staff member notifies CMT Leader (Executive Director) within 15 minutes via:
- Phone call (preferredâ€”fastest)
- Text message (if ED unreachable by phone)
- Email marked "URGENT: POTENTIAL CRISIS"

---

**Step 2: CMT Leader Assessment** (15-30 minutes)

**CMT Leader Determines**:
- Is this a crisis, issue, or incident? (Use definitions above)
- What severity level? (1, 2, or 3)
- Who needs to be involved? (Core CMT + extended members)

**If Crisis**: Activate CMT (send alert to all CMT members)

**CMT Activation Message Template**:
> **SUBJECT: CMT ACTIVATION - [Crisis Type] - [Date/Time]**
>
> Crisis Management Team is activated as of [TIME].
>
> **Crisis**: [1-sentence description]
> **Severity**: Level [1/2/3]
> **First Meeting**: [Location/Zoom link], [Date/Time - within 1-4 hours]
> **What to Bring**: [Relevant documents, data, expertise]
>
> Until meeting:
> - **No external communication** (media, social media, donors) without CMT approval
> - **Gather information** (facts, timeline, affected parties)
> - **Preserve evidence** (emails, logs, documents)
>
> Questions: Contact [CMT Leader Name/Phone]

---

**Step 3: Immediate Containment** (Minutes 30-60)

**While CMT Convenes, Take These Actions**:

**For Data Breaches**:
- Disconnect affected systems from network (contain breach)
- Preserve forensic evidence (don't delete logs)
- Contact IT security vendor, insurance carrier (cyber liability)

**For Media Inquiries**:
- Log inquiry (reporter name, outlet, deadline, questions asked)
- Respond: "We're gathering information, will provide statement by [TIME]"
- Do NOT say "No comment" (implies guilt or incompetence)

**For Staff Misconduct**:
- Place employee on administrative leave (paid, pending investigation)
- Secure employee's access (disable systems, retrieve keys/badges)
- Preserve evidence (emails, documents, witness statements)

**For Financial Crises**:
- Freeze non-essential spending
- Contact bank, line of credit provider (if applicable)
- Prepare cash flow projection

---

### Phase 2: Assessment & Strategy (Hours 1-4)

**CMT First Meeting Agenda** (60-90 minutes)

**1. Situation Report** (10 minutes)
- What happened? (facts only, no speculation)
- When did it happen? (timeline)
- Who is affected? (patients, staff, donors, partners)
- What is the scope? (# affected, $ impact, reputational risk)

**2. Confirm Severity Level** (5 minutes)
- CMT votes on Level 1, 2, or 3
- If disagreement, CMT Leader decides (err toward higher severity)

**3. Identify Stakeholders** (10 minutes)
- Who must be notified? (patients, donors, Board, media, regulators)
- What is their notification priority? (Tier 1 = immediate, Tier 2 = within 24 hours, Tier 3 = FYI only)

**4. Legal/Regulatory Assessment** (15 minutes)
- Legal Counsel briefs CMT on:
  - Notification obligations (HIPAA, state breach laws, contractual requirements)
  - Litigation risk (who might sue, for what)
  - Regulatory reporting (IRS Form 990 disclosure, state AG notification)
  - Privilege considerations (what can't be disclosed)

**5. Communications Strategy** (20 minutes)
- Key messages (3-5 bullet points)â€”what Foundation wants stakeholders to know
- Spokesperson designation (who speaks to media, donors, etc.)
- Communication channels (email, press release, website, social media)
- Timeline (when to issue statements)

**6. Assign Roles & Next Steps** (20 minutes)
- Who does what by when (action items with owners and deadlines)
- Next CMT meeting time (typically 8-24 hours later)

**7. Board Notification** (10 minutes)
- Board Chair (if not already CMT member) briefed by CMT Leader
- Full Board notified via email or emergency Board call (if Level 3)

---

### Phase 3: Response Execution (Hours 4-48)

**Parallel Workstreams**:

**Communications Workstream** (Communications Lead):
- Draft stakeholder notifications (see templates below)
- Issue holding statement to media (within 2-4 hours of crisis)
- Prepare Q&A for spokespersons
- Monitor social media, news coverage
- Update website crisis page

**Operations Workstream** (Operations Lead):
- Implement business continuity plan (if operational disruption)
- Coordinate with vendors, partners
- Support affected staff
- Document incident (timeline, decisions, expenditures)

**Legal/Compliance Workstream** (Legal Counsel):
- File required notifications (HIPAA, state breach laws)
- Coordinate with insurance carriers (D&O, cyber, EPLI)
- Preserve evidence for potential litigation
- Draft legal disclosures

**Investigation Workstream** (if applicable):
- Hire external investigator (employment misconduct, fraud allegations)
- Interview witnesses
- Collect documents
- Report findings to CMT, Board

---

### Phase 4: Recovery & Lessons Learned (Days 3-30)

**Recovery Actions**:
- Implement corrective measures (policy changes, training, system upgrades)
- Restore stakeholder trust (donor meetings, patient town halls, transparency reports)
- Monitor for secondary crises (copycat attacks, additional whistleblowers, media follow-ups)

**Post-Crisis Review** (within 7 days of crisis resolution):
- CMT convenes for 2-hour debrief
- What went well? What went poorly?
- Update Crisis Management Plan based on lessons learned
- Document for Board (crisis summary report)

## External Communication Protocols

### Stakeholder Notification Priority Tiers

**Tier 1: Immediate Notification** (within 2-4 hours)
- **Who**: Directly affected parties, legal/regulatory obligations
- **Examples**: Patients whose data was breached, regulators (HIPAA, state AG), insurance carriers, legal counsel
- **Method**: Phone call (high-touch) or email (if many affected)
- **Message**: Factual, apologetic (if Foundation at fault), clear on next steps

**Tier 2: Same-Day Notification** (within 4-8 hours)
- **Who**: Key stakeholders who will be impacted or will hear about crisis via media
- **Examples**: Major donors ($10K+/year), Board members, key partners (pharma, academic), staff
- **Method**: Email or phone call (donor size-dependent)
- **Message**: Transparent about situation, reassure about response, request patience/support

**Tier 3: 24-48 Hour Notification** (within 1-2 days)
- **Who**: General stakeholder community
- **Examples**: Full donor list, patient email list, newsletter subscribers, website visitors
- **Method**: Email blast, website post, social media
- **Message**: Brief summary, link to full statement, reassurance about mission continuity

## Social Media Management During Crises

### Social Media Lockdown Protocol

**When to Lock Down Social Media** (prevent rogue posts, uncoordinated messaging):

**Level 2-3 Crises Involving**:
- Allegations of misconduct
- Patient harm

**Lockdown Steps** (within 30 minutes of crisis identification):
1. **Pause all scheduled posts** (cancel queued contentâ€”may seem tone-deaf during crisis)

   - Mentions of Foundation

**Response Template**:

## Crisis Simulation Exercises

### Annual Crisis Drill (Tabletop Exercise)

**Purpose**: Test Crisis Management Plan, train CMT, identify gaps

**Frequency**: Annually (more frequently if high-risk environment or recent crisis)

**Format**: 2-hour tabletop exercise (CMT + Board Chair participate)

**Facilitator**: External consultant or experienced crisis management professional (brings objectivity)

---

**Tabletop Exercise Agenda**:

**Phase 1: Scenario Introduction** (15 min)
- Facilitator presents fictional crisis (realistic, relevant to Foundation)
- Example: "It's 9am Monday. Your IT Director alerts you that the patient registry was hacked over the weekend. 5,000 patient records may have been accessed. A reporter from the New York Times just called asking for commentâ€”she already knows about the breach and is writing a story."

**Phase 2: Initial Response** (30 min)
- CMT discusses: What do we do first? Who do we notify? What do we say?
- Facilitator injects new information every 10 minutes ("Board Chair's daughter is one of the affected patients and she's tweeting angrily about it")

**Phase 3: Communication Exercise** (30 min)
- CMT drafts holding statement
- Designate spokesperson, practice delivering message
- Facilitator plays reporter, asks tough questions

**Phase 4: Decision Dilemmas** (30 min)
- Facilitator presents tough choices: "Pay $100K ransom to prevent hackers from releasing data? Or refuse and let them post it online?"
- CMT debates, makes decision

**Phase 5: Debrief** (15 min)
- What went well? What gaps identified?
- Update Crisis Management Plan based on learnings

---

**Example Scenarios for Tabletop Drills**:
1. **Data Breach** (most likely crisis for patient registry)
2. **Executive Director Sudden Death** (tests succession plan)
3. **Major Donor Scandal** (largest donor indicted for fraud, media asks if Foundation will return donations)
4. **Patient Harm** (patient claims Foundation's advice led to delayed diagnosis, now has end-stage lung disease)
5. **Embezzlement** (staff member stole $100K, discovered during audit)
6. **Ransomware** (systems locked, attackers demand payment)
