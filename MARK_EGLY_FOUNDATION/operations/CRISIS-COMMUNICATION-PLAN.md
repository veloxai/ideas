## Crisis Communication Philosophy

### Why Communication Matters in Crisis

**Reality**: In crisis, perception = reality. Foundation's reputation built over years can collapse in hours if communication mishandled.

**Golden Rule**: **Communicate early, often, transparently**‚Äîeven if information limited.

**Core Principles**:

**1. Speed Over Perfection**
- **First 2 Hours Critical** (news cycle, social media amplification)‚Äîget holding statement out, even if incomplete
- "No comment" interpreted as guilt‚Äîalways say *something* (even "We're investigating")
- Speed builds trust ("They're on it"), silence breeds speculation

**2. One Voice**
- **Designated spokesperson** (usually ED, backup Communications Manager)‚Äîall media inquiries directed to them
- Prevent mixed messages (Board member says one thing, staff another‚Äîconfuses public)
- Staff reminded: "Refer media to [spokesperson], don't improvise"

**3. Transparency & Honesty**
- **Admit what you know, don't know** ("We're investigating X, should have answers by Y")
- **Own mistakes** ("We made an error, here's how we're fixing it")‚Äîapology beats defensiveness
- **Never lie** (always discovered, destroys credibility permanently‚ÄîEnron, Theranos)

**4. Empathy First**
- **Acknowledge harm** ("We're deeply sorry patients were affected")‚Äîbefore explaining circumstances
- **Human language** ("We failed" > "A process breakdown occurred"‚Äîtake responsibility, no jargon)
- **Show action** ("Here's what we're doing to make it right"‚Äîconcrete steps)

**5. Stakeholder Focus**
- **Patients First** (always)‚Äîtheir wellbeing > organizational reputation
- **Donors/Funders** (need reassurance funds used properly)
- **Staff** (internal communication before external‚Äîavoid learning from news)

### What is Crisis Communication?

**Definition**: Planned, rapid dissemination of information during crisis to:
- Inform stakeholders accurately (prevent rumors)
- Maintain trust and credibility
- Mitigate reputational damage
- Coordinate response (ensure everyone saying same thing)

**NOT**:
- ‚ùå PR spin/cover-up (transparency, not obfuscation)
- ‚ùå Legal defense (communication ‚â† legal strategy‚Äîcoordinate but distinct)
- ‚ùå Marketing campaign (crisis ‚â† opportunity to promote‚Äîtone matters)

## Crisis Classification & Response Levels

### Crisis Severity Levels (Communication Perspective)

**Level 1: Minor** (Local, Internal, Limited Reputational Risk)
- **Examples**: Staff misconduct (resolved internally), small data breach (20 records), event cancellation
- **Communication Scope**: Internal only (staff email), possibly donors if directly affected
- **Response Time**: 24 hours
- **Spokesperson**: Communications Manager (ED notified, not lead)

**Level 2: Moderate** (Regional, External Stakeholders, Some Media Interest)
- **Examples**: Patient complaint (public), grant suspension, minor financial irregularity, vendor dispute
- **Communication Scope**: Internal + affected stakeholders + proactive media statement
- **Response Time**: 4-6 hours (holding statement), 24 hours (detailed update)
- **Spokesperson**: ED (primary), Communications Manager (backup)

**Level 3: Major** (National, Widespread Media, Significant Reputational Threat)
- **Examples**: Executive misconduct, major data breach (500+ records), patient harm, fraud allegation, IRS audit/penalties
- **Communication Scope**: All stakeholders + proactive media outreach + press conference
- **Response Time**: 1-2 hours (holding statement), 6 hours (detailed update), ongoing (daily updates until resolved)
- **Spokesperson**: ED (only), Board Chair (if ED implicated)

## Communication Hierarchy

### Who Gets Told First? (Notification Order)

**Principle**: Internal before external‚Äîstaff/Board shouldn't learn from news.

**Notification Sequence** (Level 3 Crisis):

**Within 1 Hour**:
1. **Crisis Communication Team** (CCT activated, conference call)
2. **Board Chair** (phone call from ED‚Äîbrief situation, actions taken)
3. **Legal Counsel** (if not already on CCT‚Äîassess liability, mandatory reporting)

**Within 2-4 Hours**:
4. **Full Board** (email + conference call if needed‚Äîdetailed update, holding statement shared)
5. **All Staff** (emergency meeting if office open, otherwise email‚Äîsame message as Board, emphasis on "refer media to ED")
6. **Directly Affected Stakeholders** (patients impacted by crisis, vendors involved‚Äîpersonal calls if <10 people, email if >10)

**Within 4-6 Hours**:
7. **Major Donors** ($10K+ annual‚Äîpersonal call from ED or Development Director)
8. **Key Partners** (collaborating organizations, research partners‚Äîemail, follow-up call if high-profile)

**Within 6-12 Hours**:
9. **All Donors** (email blast‚Äîsame message as public statement, but "you heard it from us first" framing)
10. **General Public** (website statement, social media, press release if media inquiries)

## Stakeholder Notification Sequences

### Board Communication

**Initial Notification** (Within 1 Hour):
- **Method**: Phone call to Board Chair (ED ‚Üí Chair)
- **Content**: Brief situation summary, immediate actions, legal implications, ED recommendation
- **Chair Actions**: Approve holding statement, decide if emergency Board meeting needed

**Full Board Update** (Within 4 Hours):
- **Method**: Email to all Board members + conference call if Level 3 crisis
- **Content**: Detailed memo (2-3 pages‚Äîfacts, timeline, actions, stakeholder impact, legal/financial exposure, communications plan)
- **Attachments**: Holding statement, media coverage (if any), legal counsel memo

**Ongoing Updates**:
- **Daily email summaries** during active crisis (media coverage, stakeholder reactions, new developments)
- **Emergency Board meetings** if crisis escalates or involves Board-level decisions (ED termination, major financial commitment)

---

### Donor Communication

**Major Donors** ($10K+ Annual):
- **Method**: Personal phone call from ED (within 24 hours)
- **Script**: "I wanted you to hear this directly from me before you see it in news. [Situation]. Here's what we're doing. Your investment in our mission remains secure. I'll update you weekly."
- **Follow-up**: Handwritten note from ED (mailed within 3 days‚Äîshows personal touch)

**Mid-Level Donors** ($1K-$10K):
- **Method**: Email from ED (within 24 hours, before public statement)
- **Content**: Same facts as holding statement, but personalized ("As a valued supporter, we wanted you to hear from us first...")
- **CTA**: "Questions? Call me directly at [cell]"

**All Donors**:
- **Method**: Email blast (within 48 hours)
- **Content**: Public statement + link to crisis webpage + reassurance ("Your donations used as intended, programs continue")

---

### Patient Communication

**Directly Affected Patients** (If Crisis Involves Patient Harm, Data Breach):
- **Method**: Personal phone call from Patient Navigator or ED (within 4 hours)
- **Content**: Apology, explanation, corrective actions, resources (free credit monitoring if data breach, alternative services if program suspended)
- **Follow-up**: Written letter (mailed + emailed) confirming conversation, listing steps taken

**All Patients** (Broader Community):
- **Method**: Email newsletter (within 24 hours) + Facebook post (public + private group)
- **Content**: Situation summary, patient impact ("Programs continue normally" or "X program suspended temporarily"), how to get help
- **Reassurance**: "Your trust matters. Here's how we're ensuring this doesn't happen again."

---

### Partner Communication

**Research Partners, Co-Sponsors**:
- **Method**: Email + phone call from ED or Program Director (within 24 hours)
- **Content**: Situation, whether collaboration affected, Foundation's commitment to partnership
- **Ask**: "If media contact you, here's our statement. We're aligned."

## Social Media Lockdown Procedures

### When to Lock Down Social Media

**Triggers** (Temporarily Pause Normal Posting):
- üî¥ Level 3 crisis (executive misconduct, major scandal)
- üî¥ Coordinated attack (multiple users/bots posting negative content)
- üî¥ Misinformation spreading faster than Foundation can respond
- üî¥ Staff death or tragedy (out of respect, pause promotional content)

**Duration**: Usually 24-72 hours (until crisis messaging established, then resume selectively)

---

### Lockdown Steps

**Immediate Actions** (Within 1 Hour of Crisis Declaration):

1. **Pause Scheduled Posts**
   - Hootsuite/Buffer: Pause all queued posts (don't delete‚Äîmay resume)
   - Reason: Avoid tone-deaf posts ("Join our gala!" while crisis trending‚Äîlooks oblivious)

2. **Post Holding Statement**
   - Copy holding statement to Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn
   - Pin post to top of page (ensures first thing people see)
   - Disable comments if being flooded with trolls (not ideal, but sometimes necessary‚Äîexplain: "We're monitoring, comments temporarily paused")

3. **Monitor Mentions**
   - Assign staff to monitor @mentions, hashtags (use TweetDeck, Hootsuite streams)
   - Respond to questions with holding statement link (don't argue, don't get defensive)
   - Screenshot negative posts (evidence if defamation, but don't engage)

4. **Alert Staff**
   - Email all staff: "Social media lockdown in effect. Do NOT post about Foundation on personal accounts until further notice. Refer questions to Communications Manager."

---

### What to Post During Lockdown

**DO Post**:
- ‚úÖ **Holding statement** (facts, actions, timeline)
- ‚úÖ **Updates** (every 24-48 hours‚Äî"Here's what we've learned, next steps")
- ‚úÖ **FAQs** ("We're getting questions about X. Here's answer.")
- ‚úÖ **Correcting misinformation** ("We've seen false claims that X. Facts: Y.")

**DON'T Post**:
- ‚ùå **Promotional content** (event invites, donation asks‚Äîinsensitive during crisis)
- ‚ùå **Unrelated fluff** (AATD facts, inspirational quotes‚Äîlooks like ignoring crisis)
- ‚ùå **Defensive arguments** ("That's not true!" without evidence‚Äîescalates)

---

### When to Resume Normal Posting

**Criteria**:
- ‚úÖ Crisis resolved or stabilized (investigation complete, corrective actions implemented)
- ‚úÖ Media coverage subsided (no new articles for 48+ hours)
- ‚úÖ Social sentiment shifted neutral or positive (comments less angry/fearful)
- ‚úÖ ED approval ("We're ready to move forward")

**Resumption Strategy**:
- **Gradual** (don't immediately blast 5 posts‚Äîstart 1-2/week, build back)
- **Acknowledge** (first post: "Thank you for your patience. We're moving forward stronger. Here's how: [link to resolution statement]")
- **Focus on mission** (back to patient stories, education‚Äîrebuild trust through service)

## Message Control & Approval

### One-Voice Protocol

**Rule**: All external communication during crisis must be approved by ED (or Board Chair if ED implicated).

**Approval Workflow**:

1. **Draft** (Communications Manager writes message‚Äîholding statement, update, FAQ)
2. **Legal Review** (Legal Counsel checks for liability, mandatory disclosures‚Äî30 min turnaround)
3. **ED Approval** (ED reads, edits, approves‚Äîphone/email "APPROVED" required)
4. **Board Chair Notification** (ED forwards approved message to Chair‚ÄîFYI, not approval unless sensitive)
5. **Release** (Communications Manager posts to website, social media, emails to stakeholders)

**Bypass Protocol** (If ED Unreachable in Emergency):
- Communications Manager can release holding statement if ED unreachable after 2 hours AND crisis urgent (media calling, social media erupting)
- ED notified immediately after release
- Board Chair looped in

---

### Key Messages (Core Points to Repeat)

**Formula**: Every communication should include 3 key messages (repeated consistently across all channels‚Äîreinforces narrative).

**Example (Financial Irregularity Crisis)**:

**Key Message 1**: "We discovered the issue ourselves and immediately self-reported to regulators‚Äîdemonstrates our commitment to transparency."

**Key Message 2**: "An independent forensic auditor is conducting a comprehensive review‚Äîwe're not investigating ourselves, ensuring objectivity."

**Key Message 3**: "All programs continue without interruption, and donor funds remain secure‚Äîmission unaffected."

**Repetition**: ED says these in media interview, Communications Manager includes in stakeholder emails, Board Chair mentions in Board update‚Äîeveryone aligned.

## Rumor Control Strategies

### Why Rumors Spread

**Crisis Vacuum**: When Foundation doesn't communicate fast enough, people fill void with speculation (often worst-case scenarios).

**Social Media Amplification**: False rumor can reach 100K people in 24 hours (faster than Foundation's correction).

**Emotional Contagion**: Fear/anger spreads faster than facts‚Äî"Foundation stole money!" more shareable than "Accounting error corrected."

---

### Rumor Monitoring

**Social Listening**:
- **Google Alerts**: "Mark Egly Foundation" + crisis keywords (scandal, fraud, lawsuit)‚Äîdaily email digests
- **Social Media Monitoring**: TweetDeck for Twitter, Facebook search for posts mentioning Foundation
- **Staff Hotline**: Email/phone for staff to report rumors they hear ("Someone said Foundation is shutting down")

**Threshold for Response**: Respond if rumor reaches 1,000+ people OR comes from credible source (journalist, major partner).

---

### Rumor Response Framework

**Step 1: Assess Accuracy**
- **True**: Correct/apologize ("Yes, we made an error. Here's what happened and how we're fixing it.")
- **Partially True**: Clarify ("Some facts correct, but context missing. Here's full story.")
- **False**: Refute firmly ("This is categorically untrue. Here are facts.")

**Step 2: Choose Channel**
- **Social Media**: If rumor spreading on Twitter ‚Üí respond on Twitter (where audience is)
- **Press Statement**: If rumor in news article ‚Üí email correction to journalist, request retraction/clarification
- **Website FAQ**: If multiple rumors ‚Üí create "Setting the Record Straight" webpage

**Step 3: Fact-Check Template**

> **Setting the Record Straight: [Rumor Topic]**
>
> We've seen misinformation circulating about [topic]. Here are the facts:
>
> **CLAIM**: [Quote rumor verbatim]
> **FACT**: [Correct information with evidence‚Äîlink to audit, regulatory filing, etc.]
>
> **CLAIM**: [Another rumor]
> **FACT**: [Correction]
>
> We're committed to transparency. Questions? Email info@markegly.org or call [phone].
>
> [ED Signature]

**Why?**: Saves time (don't scramble to build page during crisis), ensures consistent messaging, gives media/stakeholders single authoritative source.

**Page Elements**:

**Latest Statement**: [Holding statement or detailed update‚Äîreplaced as crisis evolves]

## Media Bridging Techniques

### What is Bridging?

**Definition**: Technique for steering media interview back to Foundation's key messages (especially when journalist asks hostile/off-topic question).

**Formula**: Acknowledge question ‚Üí Bridge to message ‚Üí Deliver message

---

### Bridging Phrases

**Acknowledge + Bridge**:
- "That's a fair question, **and what's important to understand is**..." [key message]
- "I appreciate you asking that, **but let me put it in context**..." [key message]
- "Yes, **and here's what we're doing about it**..." [corrective action]
- "I understand the concern, **but the real issue is**..." [reframe to Foundation's narrative]
- "That's not quite accurate, **so let me clarify**..." [correct misinformation]

---

### Example Dialogue

**Journalist**: "Critics say the Foundation mismanaged donor funds. How do you respond?"

**ED** (Bridging):
"I understand why people have questions, **and that's exactly why we self-reported and engaged an independent auditor**. [Key Message 1: Transparency]. **What's important to know is** that all programs continue, every donor's contribution is being used as intended, and we're implementing new financial controls to ensure this never happens again. [Key Message 2: Mission continues]. **The real story here is** our commitment to accountability‚Äîwe didn't hide this, we brought it to light ourselves."

---

### Difficult Question Responses

**"No Comment" Alternatives** (Never Say "No Comment"‚ÄîSounds Guilty):

**Instead**:
- ‚úÖ "I can't discuss that specifically due to [legal/privacy] reasons, **but what I can tell you is**..." [what you CAN say]
- ‚úÖ "We're still investigating, so I don't have complete information yet. **What I do know is**..." [confirmed facts]
- ‚úÖ "That's not my area of expertise, **but I can connect you with [colleague] who can address that**."

**Hostile Framing** ("Isn't it true Foundation is just a scam?"):

**Don't**: Get defensive, argue with premise
**Do**: Reframe with facts
- ‚úÖ "No, and here's why that's inaccurate: [facts]. Last year we served 200 patients, published audited financials showing 85% of funds to programs, and earned Charity Navigator 4-star rating. Our record speaks for itself."

## Post-Crisis Debriefing

### Debrief Meeting (Within 7 Days of Resolution)

**Attendees**: Crisis Communication Team + any other involved staff

**Agenda** (90 minutes):
1. **What Happened** (10 min): Chronology recap
2. **Communication Performance** (30 min):
   - What did we do well? (Speed, transparency, message consistency?)
   - What could we improve? (Delayed notifications, confusing messages, poor media management?)
3. **Stakeholder Feedback** (20 min): What did Board, donors, patients, media say?
4. **Recommendations** (20 min): Specific changes to Crisis Communication Plan, training needs
5. **Action Items** (10 min): Who does what by when (update plan, schedule training, etc.)

**Output**: Written debrief memo (shared with Board, filed for future reference).

**Purpose**: Test Crisis Communication Plan without real crisis‚Äîidentify gaps, train new staff.

**Simulation Steps**:

**Outcome**: Updated Crisis Communication Plan, staff confidence in protocols.

### Communication Roles Within CMT

**Crisis Management Team Structure** (From Document 19):
- **CMT Lead** (ED): Overall crisis response + primary spokesperson
- **Operations** (COO): Handle operational response (no communication role unless relaying facts to Communications Manager)
- **Legal**: Advise CMT Lead + review all communications
- **Communications Manager**: CCT Lead, drafts all messages, manages media

**Communication Workflow During Crisis**:
1. **CMT meets** (assess crisis, decide actions)
2. **Communications Manager attends CMT meeting** (hears firsthand what's happening, actions planned)
3. **Communications Manager drafts message** (based on CMT decisions)
4. **Legal reviews, ED approves** (message aligned with crisis response strategy)
5. **Communications Manager releases** (stakeholder notifications, media, social media)

## Conclusion

### Communication as Crisis Lifeline

**Bottom Line**: In crisis, Foundation's reputation lives or dies based on how it communicates. Fast, honest, empathetic communication = trust preserved. Slow, evasive, defensive communication = trust destroyed.

**Real-World Truth**: Nonprofits recover from mistakes‚Äîhumans understand errors, forgive when accountability shown. But nonprofits rarely recover from cover-ups, lies, "no comment." Crisis communication isn't PR spin‚Äîit's moral obligation to stakeholders who trust Foundation with their money, health, hope.
