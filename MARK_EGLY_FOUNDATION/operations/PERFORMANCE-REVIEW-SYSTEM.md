## Performance Management Philosophy

### Foundation's Approach to Performance

**Mission-Driven Performance**: Individual success = mission success. Performance reviews evaluate not just what employees accomplish (outputs) but how it advances Alpha-1 awareness, patient support, and research (outcomes).

**Growth, Not Punishment**: Reviews identify strengths to amplify and gaps to developâ€”not just "gotcha" documentation for termination. 80% of review = forward-looking (goals for next year, development plan), 20% = backward-looking (last year's accomplishments).

**Ongoing, Not Once-a-Year**: Annual review = formal documentation, but feedback continuous (quarterly check-ins, weekly 1-on-1s, real-time praise/coaching). No surprises at annual reviewâ€”employee should already know where they stand.

**Fairness & Consistency**: Same standards across roles (no favoritismâ€”rockstar gets honest feedback on improvement areas, struggling employee gets recognition for wins). Calibration process ensures managers rate consistently (not easy graders vs. tough graders).

---

### Benefits of Robust Performance Management

**For Employees**:

- âœ… **Clarity**: Know what's expected (goals, competencies, success criteriaâ€”not guessing what manager wants)
- âœ… **Recognition**: Wins celebrated (patient testimonial, grant funded, system implementedâ€”formal acknowledgment)
- âœ… **Development**: Skills built (training, stretch projects, mentorshipâ€”grow career, not stagnate)
- âœ… **Fairness**: Promotion/raise decisions transparent (performance ratings + criteria visibleâ€”not black box)

**For Foundation**:

- âœ… **Alignment**: Everyone rowing same direction (strategic plan â†’ department goals â†’ individual goalsâ€”cascading objectives)
- âœ… **Accountability**: Performance documented (if underperformer, paper trail supports PIP or terminationâ€”legally defensible)
- âœ… **Retention**: Top performers stay (high ratings + raises + career path = engagement; neglecting = lose best to competitors)
- âœ… **Succession**: Identify future leaders (high-potentials fast-trackedâ€”Development Coordinator â†’ Director â†’ VP track)

### Core Principles

**1. Two-Way Conversation** (Not Manager Lecture):

- Employee self-assessment (reflect on wins, challenges, growth areasâ€”share before meeting)
- Manager feedback (observations, data, examplesâ€”specific, not vague)
- Collaborative goal-setting (next year's prioritiesâ€”employee ownership increases buy-in)

**2. Evidence-Based** (Not Opinion):

- Use data (grants submitted, patients enrolled, fundraising $, project completion ratesâ€”quantitative where possible)
- Use examples (specific instancesâ€”"You led Alpha-1 Awareness Month campaign, raised $50K, exceeded $35K goal by 43%"â€”not "You're great at fundraising")
- Avoid recency bias (review full yearâ€”not just last 2 months fresh in memory)

**3. Developmental** (Not Just Evaluative):

- Identify 2-3 growth areas (skills to buildâ€”public speaking, data analysis, grant writing)
- Create plan (training, mentorship, stretch projectâ€”actionable steps, not "get better at X")
- Follow up quarterly (progress on development planâ€”adjust if needed)

## Goal Setting & SMART Goals

### Goal-Setting Process

**When**: May (after annual review complete, before Q1 starts)

**How Many**: 5 SMART goals per employee (fewer = lack of ambition, more = diluted focus)

**Who Sets**: Collaborative (manager proposes draft based on strategic plan, employee refines based on interests/capacity, agree together)

---

### SMART Criteria

**S = Specific**: Clear, unambiguous (not "improve fundraising"â€”"increase individual donor revenue by 20% from $100K to $120K")

**M = Measurable**: Quantifiable (number, $, %, dateâ€”know when achieved)

**A = Achievable**: Challenging but realistic (stretch goal = 110% effort required, not impossible 300%)

**R = Relevant**: Ties to strategic plan or job description (not randomâ€”Development Officer shouldn't have goal "Improve IT security"â€”that's IT Manager's goal)

**T = Time-Bound**: Deadline (by December 31, by Q3, by end of fiscal yearâ€”not open-ended)

## 360-Degree Feedback Process

### Purpose

**Traditional Review** = Manager evaluates employee (one perspectiveâ€”what if manager biased, doesn't see day-to-day work?)

**360-Degree** = Multiple perspectives:

- Manager (supervisor viewâ€”strategic alignment, goal achievement)
- Peers (colleaguesâ€”collaboration, communication, reliability)
- Direct Reports (if managerâ€”leadership, support, clarity)
- Self (employee reflectionâ€”self-awareness, growth areas)

**Result**: Holistic view (blind spots revealedâ€”employee thinks they're great communicator, but peers say emails confusing; manager thinks employee needs improvement, but peers say they're rockstar)

---

### Who Participates

**All Employees**: Annual 360-degree feedback (not just managersâ€”everyone benefits from multi-source input)

**Raters** (Employee selects + manager adds):

- **Self**: Employee (requiredâ€”self-assessment)
- **Manager**: Direct supervisor (requiredâ€”primary rater)
- **Peers**: 2-4 colleagues (work closely withâ€”cross-functional preferred, not just same department)
- **Direct Reports**: If manage staff, 2-4 direct reports (anonymousâ€”safety to be candid)
- **External Stakeholders** (Optional): Board members, donors, partners, patients (if role involvesâ€”Development Director gets donor feedback, Patient Navigator gets patient feedback)

**Selection Criteria**:

- Work together regularly (â‰¥3 monthsâ€”new colleague doesn't have enough observation)
- Diverse perspectives (not all friendsâ€”include someone who's seen you struggle)
- Confidentiality (feedback anonymousâ€”except manager and self)

---

### 360-Degree Feedback Survey

**Platform**: Culture Amp ($5/employeeâ€”360-degree feedback tool), SurveyMonkey (free tier adequate Year 1-2), or Google Forms (manual but zero cost)

**Survey Structure** (20 questions, 10 minutes):

**Section 1: Core Competencies** (Rate 1-5 scale):

1. Demonstrates mission alignment (passionate about Alpha-1 cause, decisions prioritize patients)
2. Collaborates effectively (works well with others, shares knowledge, resolves conflicts constructively)
3. Communicates clearly (emails, meetings, presentationsâ€”concise, understandable, timely)
4. Accountable and reliable (meets deadlines, owns mistakes, follows through on commitments)
5. Adapts to change (embraces new processes, learns quickly, flexible when priorities shift)
6. Shows sound judgment (makes good decisions, escalates appropriately, acts ethically)
7. Takes initiative (proactive, improves processes, innovates)
8. Delivers quality work (attention to detail, meets standards, error-free)

**Section 2: Role-Specific** (Tailored by job):

- Development: Builds strong donor relationships
- Patient Navigator: Shows empathy and patience with patients
- Research: Ensures data accuracy and IRB compliance

**Section 3: Open-Ended** (Free text):

1. What does this person do exceptionally well? (strengths to continue)
2. What is one area for improvement? (specific, actionableâ€”not "be better," say "respond to emails within 24 hours")
3. Any additional comments?

**Anonymity**: Peer and direct report feedback anonymous (aggregatedâ€”can't identify individual rater)â€”encourages honesty

### Calibration Session (Late March)

**Outcome**: Consistent, fair ratings (employees perceive fairnessâ€”"My manager isn't easy/hard grader, just accurate")

## Performance Improvement Plans

### When PIP Required

**Trigger**: Rating "Needs Improvement" (2/5) or "Unsatisfactory" (1/5)

**Not Punishment**: PIP = structured support (clear expectations, coaching, timelineâ€”give employee chance to succeed before termination)

**Duration**: 60-90 days (shorter = too little time to improve; longer = drags on if not improving)

---

### PIP Process

**Step 1: PIP Meeting** (Manager + Employee + COO):

- **Discuss Performance Gaps**: Specific deficiencies (not "bad attitude"â€”"Missed 4 of 10 deadlines in Q4, submitted reports with â‰¥5 errors requiring rework")
- **Set Expectations**: What "success" looks like (e.g., "Meet 100% of deadlines, submit error-free reports, respond to emails within 24 hours")
- **Create Action Plan**: Steps to improve (weekly check-ins with manager, time management training, peer shadowing)
- **Define Timeline**: 60 or 90 days (end date clearâ€”June 1 start, July 30 end)
- **Explain Consequences**: If don't improve, termination (serious but fair warning)

**Step 2: Weekly Check-Ins** (Manager + Employee):

- Review progress (what's working? what's not?)
- Provide coaching (real-time feedback, not waiting until PIP end)
- Adjust plan if needed (if training doesn't help, try different approach)

**Step 3: Mid-PIP Review** (30 days in):

- Formal progress assessment (on track? at risk? not improving?)
- Written feedback (document improvement or continued deficiencies)
- Decision point (if zero improvement, may extend PIP or proceed to terminationâ€”but give fair chance)

**Step 4: Final PIP Review** (End of 60-90 days):

- **Outcome 1: Successful** (performance meets expectationsâ€”remove from PIP, continue employment, quarterly check-ins next 6 months to ensure sustained improvement)
- **Outcome 2: Partial** (some improvement but not enoughâ€”extend PIP another 30 days, or transition to role better fit, or terminate if inadequate progress)
- **Outcome 3: Unsuccessful** (no improvement or worsenedâ€”terminate employment, provide severance if eligible, exit procedures)

---

### PIP Documentation (Critical for Legal Defense)

**PIP Form** (Template):

- Employee info (name, title, department, manager)
- Performance deficiencies (specific examplesâ€”dates, incidents, impact)
- Expectations for improvement (measurableâ€”"Meet 100% deadlines" not "improve performance")
- Support provided (training, coaching, resources)
- Timeline (start date, check-in dates, end date)
- Consequences (termination if not improve)
- Signatures (employee, manager, COOâ€”employee signs acknowledging receipt, can add comments if disagrees)

**Weekly Check-In Notes**: Manager documents each meeting (progress, challenges, coaching providedâ€”shows good-faith effort to support employee)

**Final Outcome**: Successful completion letter or termination letter (if termination, references PIP processâ€”shows Foundation gave fair chance)
