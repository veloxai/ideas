## 501(c)(3) Compliance Requirements

### IRS Requirements for Tax-Exempt Status

To maintain 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status, the Mark Egly Foundation must:

**1. Organize & Operate for Exempt Purpose**

- âœ… Articles of Incorporation state charitable purpose
- âœ… Activities further charitable mission (no private benefit)
- âœ… Net earnings do not benefit individuals

**2. Prohibition on Private Inurement**

- âœ… No earnings benefit private shareholders/individuals
- âœ… Compensation to staff/board must be reasonable
- âœ… Transactions with related parties at fair market value

**3. Prohibition on Political Campaign Activity**

- âœ… No participation in political campaigns
- âœ… No endorsement of candidates
- âœ… No contributions to political campaigns

**4. Limitation on Lobbying**

- âœ… Lobbying must be insubstantial part of activities
- âœ… Educational advocacy (issue education) is permitted
- âœ… Direct lobbying (specific legislation) is limited

**5. Public Disclosure Requirements**

- âœ… Make Form 990 available for public inspection
- âœ… Make exemption application (Form 1023) available
- âœ… Provide copies upon request (within 30 days)

### Annual Compliance Checklist

**Federal Requirements**:

âœ… **Form 990** (Return of Organization Exempt from Income Tax)

- Due: 4.5 months after fiscal year end (March 15 for Oct 31 year-end)
- Required if gross receipts >$200K OR assets >$500K
- Form 990-EZ if gross receipts <$200K and assets <$500K
- Form 990-N (e-Postcard) if gross receipts <$50K

âœ… **Form 1099-MISC** (for contractors paid >$600)

- Due: January 31 annually
- Issue to all contractors and service providers

âœ… **Payroll Tax Forms** (if staff)

- Form 941 (quarterly payroll tax)
- Form W-2 (annual employee wages)
- Form W-3 (transmittal of W-2s)

**State Requirements** (varies by state):

âœ… **Annual Report** (Secretary of State)

- Due date varies by state
- Updates to registered agent, directors, address

âœ… **Charitable Registration** (Attorney General or Charity Bureau)

- Required in most states for fundraising
- Initial registration + annual renewals
- Unified Registration Statement (URS) accepted in many states

âœ… **State Tax Filings**

- State income tax exemption (if separate from federal)
- Sales tax exemption certificate (for purchases)
- Property tax exemption (if applicable)

**Other Compliance**:

âœ… **Board Meeting Minutes**

- Document all board meetings and key decisions
- Required for audit and IRS compliance

âœ… **Conflict of Interest Disclosures**

- Annual disclosure from all board members
- Documentation of conflict resolution

âœ… **Document Retention**

- Permanent: Articles, bylaws, IRS determination letter, board minutes
- 7 years: Financial records, Form 990s, payroll records
- 3 years: Contracts, correspondence

---

### Activities Requiring IRS Approval

**Unrelated Business Income Tax (UBIT)**:

- If foundation earns income from activities unrelated to mission
- Example: If we sold merchandise unrelated to AATD
- Must file Form 990-T if UBIT >$1,000

**Substantial Change in Activities**:

- Notify IRS if mission or activities change substantially
- May require new determination letter

**Related Organizations**:

- Disclose relationships with related organizations (Form 990 Schedule R)
- Example: If we create a for-profit subsidiary for patent licensing

## Accounting & Reporting

### Accounting Standards

**Method**: Accrual basis accounting

- Revenue recognized when earned (not when cash received)
- Expenses recognized when incurred (not when paid)
- Provides more accurate picture of financial position

**Software**: QuickBooks Online (Nonprofit Edition)

- Chart of accounts aligned with Form 990
- Multi-user access with permission levels
- Integration with banking and payroll

**Chart of Accounts Structure**:

**Assets**:

- Cash and cash equivalents
- Accounts receivable (grants, pledges)
- Prepaid expenses
- Fixed assets (equipment, furniture)

**Liabilities**:

- Accounts payable
- Accrued expenses (payroll, vacation)
- Deferred revenue (grants received in advance)
- Line of credit (if applicable)

**Net Assets** (Fund Accounting):

- Without donor restrictions (unrestricted)
- With donor restrictions (temporarily or permanently restricted)

**Revenue**:

- Individual contributions
- Foundation grants
- Corporate donations
- Government grants
- Program service revenue (CME fees, etc.)
- Investment income
- In-kind donations

**Expenses** (Functional Classification):

- Program expenses:
  - Education programs
  - Support programs
  - Research programs
  - Advocacy programs
- Management & general (overhead)
- Fundraising

---

### Financial Statements

**Monthly Financial Statements** (for internal use):

1. **Statement of Financial Position** (Balance Sheet)
   - Assets, liabilities, net assets
   - Shows financial health at point in time

2. **Statement of Activities** (Income Statement)
   - Revenue and expenses for the month/year-to-date
   - Budget vs. actual comparison
   - Shows financial performance over time

3. **Statement of Cash Flows**
   - Cash inflows and outflows
   - Operating, investing, financing activities
   - Critical for cash management

4. **Budget vs. Actual Report**
   - Compare actual results to approved budget
   - Variance analysis (favorable/unfavorable)
   - Explanations for significant variances

**Annual Financial Statements** (for audit/public):

- Prepared by CPA according to GAAP
- Includes notes to financial statements
- Audit opinion letter (if audited)

---

## Internal Controls

### Purpose of Internal Controls

Prevent fraud, errors, and mismanagement through checks and balances.

---

### Cash Handling Controls

**Receipts**:

- All checks restrictively endorsed immediately ("For Deposit Only")
- Dual custody for cash/checks (two people present when opening mail)
- Daily deposits (or weekly if volume is low)
- Donation acknowledgment letters sent within 48 hours
- Gifts recorded in donor database (Salesforce) same day

**Credit Card Processing**:

- PCI-compliant payment processor (Stripe, PayPal Giving Fund)
- Monthly reconciliation of processor statements to accounting
- Donors receive immediate email receipt

**Online Donations**:

- Secure donation platform integrated with website
- Daily download of transactions to accounting system
- Thank you email auto-generated

---

### Cash Disbursement Controls

**Checks**:

- Two signatures required for checks >$5,000
- No blank checks signed
- Checks made payable to specific vendors (not "Cash")
- Supporting documentation attached to all checks
- Voided checks retained and marked "VOID"

**Electronic Payments (ACH, Wire)**:

- Dual approval required (initiator â‰  approver)
- Vendor verified before payment
- Confirmation sent to Executive Director

**Credit Cards**:

- Foundation credit card in organization's name
- Limited to Executive Director and key staff
- Itemized receipts required for all charges
- Monthly review and approval by supervisor

**Petty Cash** (if used):

- Maximum $200 in petty cash fund
- Custodian assigned
- Reimbursement requires receipts
- Periodic surprise counts

---

### Segregation of Duties

**Key Principle**: No single person controls all aspects of a transaction

**Example Segregation**:

| Function               | Person Responsible             |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Approve expense        | Executive Director             |
| Record transaction     | Finance Manager                |
| Sign check             | Executive Director + Treasurer |
| Reconcile bank account | Finance Manager                |
| Review reconciliation  | Executive Director             |

**For Small Organizations** (Year 1):

- If segregation not possible due to staff size, board Treasurer provides oversight
- Monthly bank reconciliation reviewed by Treasurer
- Periodic surprise audits by Finance Committee

---

### Access Controls

**Financial Systems**:

- Role-based access (view only, edit, approve)
- Strong passwords (changed every 90 days)
- Multi-factor authentication enabled
- Terminated employees' access removed immediately

**Banking**:

- Online banking access limited to Executive Director and Finance Manager
- Separate logins (no shared credentials)
- Transaction alerts enabled for large amounts

**Physical Security**:

- Checks locked in secure location
- Financial documents in locked file cabinets
- Shred confidential documents when disposed

## Audit & Review

### When Audit Required

**IRS Guidelines**:

- Not legally required by IRS
- However, many grantors require audited financials

**Best Practice**:

- **<$250K budget**: No audit (board oversight sufficient)
- **$250K-$500K**: Financial review by CPA
- **>$500K**: Full audit by independent CPA

**Mark Egly Foundation Plan**:

- Year 1 ($500K budget): Financial review
- Year 2+ (>$500K): Annual audit

---

### Audit Process

**1. Select Auditor** (every 3-5 years, rotate if needed):

- Qualified CPA firm with nonprofit experience
- Request proposals from 3 firms
- Finance Committee recommends, board approves

**2. Audit Planning** (June-July):

- Auditor meets with Finance Committee
- Agree on scope and timeline
- Prepare audit schedule (documents needed)

**3. Fieldwork** (August-September):

- Auditor reviews financial records
- Tests transactions and controls
- Interviews staff and board members

**4. Draft Report** (October):

- Auditor prepares draft financial statements
- Management letter identifies any deficiencies
- Finance Committee reviews with auditor

**5. Final Report** (November):

- Auditor presents to Finance Committee and full board
- Board accepts audit and approves financial statements
- Form 990 prepared using audited financials

**6. Public Disclosure**:

- Audited financials published on website
- Included in annual report
- Submitted to GuideStar, Charity Navigator

---

### Management Letter

**Purpose**: Auditor provides recommendations for improvements

**Common Topics**:

- Internal control deficiencies
- Accounting policy clarifications
- Process improvements
- Compliance issues

**Foundation Response**:

- Management responds to each recommendation
- Action plan for implementation
- Timeline for completion
- Follow-up in next year's audit

## Tax Compliance

### Form 990 Preparation

**Timeline**:

- Fiscal year ends: October 31
- Form 990 due: March 15 (4.5 months later)
- Extension available: Additional 6 months (September 15)

**Who Prepares**:

- CPA or tax professional (recommended)
- Executive Director provides information
- Treasurer reviews before filing
- Board reviews and approves

**Key Sections**:

- **Part I**: Summary of revenue, expenses, net assets
- **Part III**: Program service accomplishments (mission impact)
- **Part VI**: Governance policies and practices
- **Part VII**: Compensation of officers, directors, key employees
- **Part VIII**: Statement of revenue
- **Part IX**: Statement of functional expenses
- **Part X**: Balance sheet

**Schedules** (if applicable):

- Schedule A: Public charity status
- Schedule B: Contributors (IRS only, not public)
- Schedule C: Lobbying activities
- Schedule D: Supplemental financial statements
- Schedule O: Supplemental information

### Donor Acknowledgment Requirements

**IRS Requirements for Donors** (foundation must provide):

**Donations <$250**:

- Written receipt (letter or email)
- Include: Organization name, date, amount
- No goods/services statement

**Donations â‰¥$250**:

- Written acknowledgment required (for donor's tax deduction)
- Must include:
  - Amount of cash contribution
  - Description of non-cash contribution
  - Statement: "No goods or services provided" OR
  - Value of goods/services if any provided
- Must be contemporaneous (before donor files tax return)

**Quid Pro Quo Contributions >$75**:

- If donor receives something in exchange (e.g., gala ticket)
- Disclose fair market value of benefit received
- Deductible amount = donation - value of benefit

### State Charitable Registration

**Why Required**: Most states require registration before soliciting donations

**Registration Requirements** (varies by state):

- Initial registration form
- Copy of IRS determination letter
- Copy of Form 990
- Financial statements
- Registration fee ($25-$400)

**Annual Renewal**:

- File annual report with state
- Submit current Form 990
- Pay renewal fee

**Unified Registration Statement (URS)**:

- Accepted by 40+ states
- File once, submit to multiple states
- Simplifies multi-state compliance

**Mark Egly Foundation Plan**:

- Register in home state (Year 1)
- Register in states where major fundraising (Year 1-2)
- Consider all 50 states if nationwide solicitation (Year 3+)
