## Event Safety Philosophy

### Why Event Safety Matters

**Reality**: Foundation hosts events for AATD patientsâ€”many with compromised lung function (FEV1 <50%), mobility limitations, oxygen dependence. Events carry inherent risks:
- **Medical emergencies**: Respiratory distress, cardiac events, falls (patient population older, sicker than general public)

- **Reputational damage**: Serious incident â†’ negative media coverage ("Patient dies at Mark Egly Foundation walk"â€”devastating)

**Moral Obligation**: Foundation invites patients to eventsâ€”responsible for their safety. "First, do no harm" applies to events as much as medicine.

### Core Safety Principles

**1. Prevention Over Response**
- âœ… **Anticipate risks** (venue walkthrough, identify hazardsâ€”uneven pavement, narrow exits, insufficient parking)
- âœ… **Mitigate proactively** (place "Watch Your Step" signs, widen walkways, arrange shuttle service)
- âŒ **Don't react after incident** (waiting until someone trips to address hazardâ€”too late)

**2. Participant-Appropriate Planning**
- âœ… **AATD-specific considerations**: Patients may need oxygen, frequent rest breaks, shaded seating (not generic event planningâ€”tailor to population)
- âœ… **Family-friendly**: Events include children, elderly caregivers (diverse needsâ€”wheelchair access, changing stations, quiet rooms)

**3. Transparency & Communication**
- âœ… **Disclose risks** (event registration: "Walk involves 2-mile outdoor route, uneven terrainâ€”participants assume risk")

**4. Regulatory Compliance**
- âœ… **Permits**: City permits (parade, park use, road closures), health permits (food service), fire marshal approval (venue capacity)
- âœ… **Insurance**: Event liability coverage ($1M-$2M per occurrence)

## Event Risk Assessment

### Risk Assessment Matrix

**Purpose**: Identify potential hazards, assess likelihood Ã— severity, prioritize mitigation efforts.

**Risk Formula**: **Risk Score = Likelihood (1-5) Ã— Severity (1-5)**
- **Likelihood**: 1 = Rare, 3 = Possible, 5 = Likely
- **Severity**: 1 = Minor (first aid), 3 = Moderate (ER visit), 5 = Severe (hospitalization/death)

## Event Insurance Requirements

### Types of Event Insurance

**1. General Liability Insurance**
- **Coverage**: Bodily injury, property damage caused by event (participant trips over cable, breaks armâ€”insurance covers medical bills, legal defense)
- **Limits**: $1M per occurrence, $2M aggregate (standard for nonprofit events)
- **Cost**: $500-$2,000 per event (varies by attendance, activities, venue requirements)

**2. Special Event Insurance** (One-Time Coverage):
- **When Needed**: Large public events (walk with 500+ participants), events with elevated risk (obstacle course, zip lineâ€”Foundation unlikely to host, but know it exists)
- **Coverage**: Same as general liability, but specifically for single event (Foundation's annual policy may exclude certain eventsâ€”require separate coverage)
- **Cost**: $300-$1,500 per event

**3. Liquor Liability** (If Serving Alcohol):
- **Coverage**: Injuries/damage caused by intoxicated guest (drunk guest drives home, causes accidentâ€”victim sues Foundation for over-serving)
- **When Required**: Galas serving wine/beer (even if not sellingâ€”giving away alcohol = liability)
- **Cost**: $500-$1,000 per event (add-on to general liability)

**4. Participant Accident Insurance** (Optional):
- **Coverage**: Medical expenses if participant injured during event (covers deductibles, copaysâ€”supplements participant's health insurance)
- **When Useful**: Athletic events (runs, obstacle courses) where injury risk higher
- **Cost**: $1-$5 per participant (inexpensiveâ€”$500 for 500-participant walk)

---

### Certificate of Insurance (COI)

**What is COI?**: Document proving Foundation has insuranceâ€”venues, cities require COI before approving event.

**COI Requirements** (Typical Venue):
- **Coverage**: $1M per occurrence, $2M aggregate general liability
- **Additional Insured**: Venue/city named as "additional insured" (if Foundation's event causes damage, venue's also protected)
- **Cancellation Notice**: Insurer will notify venue 30 days before canceling policy (protects venue from suddenly uninsured event)

**Process**:
1. Foundation contacts insurance broker (60 days before eventâ€”"Need COI for walk at City Park")
2. Broker issues COI (lists Foundation, venue as additional insured, coverage limitsâ€”PDF emailed within 2-3 days)
3. Foundation forwards COI to venue (event permit approved upon COI receipt)
