## Welcome & Mission

### About the Mark Egly Foundation

The Mark Egly Foundation is dedicated to transforming Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency (AATD) diagnosis, research, and patient care. Our mission: increase diagnosis rates from 10% to 50%, build a 10,000-patient research registry, and accelerate development of a cure.

Named in honor of Mark Egly, a tireless AATD advocate who passed away in 2024, the Foundation carries forward his vision of a world where every person with Alpha-1 is diagnosed, supported, and hopeful.

### Our Values

- **Patient-First**: Every decision prioritizes patient well-being, dignity, and empowerment
- **Scientific Rigor**: We base programs on evidence, data, and best practices
- **Collaboration**: We partner with researchers, clinicians, industry, and patient organizations
- **Transparency**: We operate with integrity, accountability, and openness
- **Compassion**: We meet patients and families with empathy, respect, and cultural humility
- **Innovation**: We embrace new ideas, technologies, and approaches to accelerate progress

### Equal Opportunity Employer

The Mark Egly Foundation is an Equal Opportunity Employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, gender identity, and sexual orientation), national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, or any other legally protected characteristic. This policy applies to all employment practices: recruitment, hiring, compensation, benefits, promotion, training, discipline, and termination.

## Employment Basics

### Employment Categories

**Full-Time Employees**: Work 40 hours/week (or 37.5 hours/week with unpaid lunch). Eligible for all benefits (health insurance, 401(k), PTO).

**Part-Time Employees**: Work <30 hours/week. Eligible for prorated PTO; health insurance eligibility varies by state law and hours worked (ACA threshold: 30 hours/week average).

**Exempt (Salaried)**: Not eligible for overtime pay under FLSA. Typical roles: Executive Director, Directors, Managers, Professional staff (requires salary >$684/week and duties test).

**Non-Exempt (Hourly)**: Eligible for overtime pay (1.5Ã— regular rate for hours >40/week). Typical roles: Administrative Assistants, Coordinators, part-time staff.

**Temporary/Contract**: Fixed-term (e.g., 6-month grant-funded position). Not eligible for benefits unless specified in offer letter.

**Interns**: Unpaid or paid (paid interns = minimum wage + overtime if non-exempt). Educational credit arrangements must comply with DOL intern test.

### Harassment Prevention

**Prohibited Conduct**:

- **Sexual Harassment**: Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, verbal/physical conduct of sexual nature (quid pro quo: "sleep with me or you're fired"; hostile environment: repeated sexual jokes, inappropriate touching, leering)
- **Discriminatory Harassment**: Offensive remarks, jokes, slurs based on protected characteristics (race, religion, disability, etc.) creating hostile work environment
- **Bullying**: Repeated mistreatment (verbal abuse, sabotage, intimidation, humiliation)
- **Retaliation**: Adverse action against employee for reporting harassment, discrimination, or participating in investigation (retaliation = separate violation, equally prohibited)

**Examples of Harassment**:

- Sexual comments about appearance, body, clothing
- Sharing sexual images, jokes, memes (email, Slack, text)
- Unwanted physical contact (hugs, shoulder rubs, standing too close)
- Romantic/sexual advances after being told "no"
- Racial jokes, ethnic slurs, mocking accents
- Religious ridicule or proselytizing (pressuring to convert)
- Disability mockery ("you're so OCD," "that's retarded")

**NOT Harassment**:

- Constructive feedback ("Your report had errorsâ€”please proofread")
- Performance management (written warnings, PIP)
- Interpersonal conflict (disagreement over project approachâ€”resolve professionally)
- Single offensive comment (may be inappropriate but not legally "harassment" unless severeâ€”e.g., one-time racial slur = immediate discipline but not hostile environment unless repeated)

### Reporting & Investigation

**How to Report**:

1. **Supervisor**: First option (if supervisor not involved in complaint)
2. **COO**: If supervisor involved, or uncomfortable reporting to supervisor
3. **Board Chair**: If COO involved (email boardchair@markeglyfoundation.org)
4. **Anonymous Hotline**: (Year 2-3) Third-party hotline (EthicsPoint, Navexâ€”$1K-$2K/year)

**Report Format**: Verbal or written (written preferred for documentationâ€”email, Google Form). Include: who, what, when, where, witnesses.

**Investigation**:

- Initiated within 48 hours of report
- Investigator (COO or external HR consultant if conflict) interviews complainant, accused, witnesses
- Confidentiality maintained (only those who need to knowâ€”investigator, COO, legal, accused)
- Completed within 30 days (or as soon as reasonably possible)

**Outcome**:

- **Substantiated**: Discipline up to termination (severity-dependentâ€”single inappropriate joke = warning; groping = immediate termination)
- **Unsubstantiated**: Insufficient evidence (doesn't mean falseâ€”just can't prove by preponderance of evidence)
- **False Report**: Intentionally false report = discipline (rareâ€”most reports made in good faith even if not substantiated)

**Retaliation Prohibition**: Reporting, participating in investigation = protected activity. Any retaliation (demotion, exclusion, cold shoulder) = separate violation, subject to discipline.

## Disciplinary Procedures

### Progressive Discipline

**Philosophy**: Corrective, not punitive (goal = improve performance, retain talentâ€”not "gotcha")

**Steps**:

1. **Verbal Counseling** (First offense, minor issueâ€”late to meeting, missed deadline): Supervisor discusses issue, documents conversation in employee file
2. **Written Warning** (Repeated issue or moderate severityâ€”pattern of tardiness, subpar work quality): Formal write-up, employee signs acknowledgment (signature = receipt, not agreement), placed in personnel file
3. **Final Written Warning** (Continued issues): Last chanceâ€”states specific improvement required, timeline (e.g., 30 days), consequence if not met (termination)
4. **Termination** (Failure to improve or severe violation)

**Exceptions (Immediate Terminationâ€”No Progressive Discipline)**:

- Gross misconduct: Theft, violence, sexual harassment, fraud, intoxication at work, HIPAA breach (intentional disclosure of PHI)
- Insubordination: Refusal to perform lawful, reasonable job duty
- Falsification: Timesheet fraud, resume lies, forged documents

---

### Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

**When Used**: Performance deficiencies (not behavioralâ€”e.g., missing targets, poor quality work, low productivity)

**PIP Structure**:

- **Performance Gaps**: Specific deficiencies (e.g., "Missed 3 grant deadlines in Q3, submitted report with 15 errors")
- **Improvement Goals**: Measurable (e.g., "Submit all Q4 grant proposals on time with <2 errors per document")
- **Support Provided**: Training, mentorship, resources, more frequent check-ins
- **Timeline**: 30-90 days (complexity-dependent)
- **Outcome**:
  - **Success**: Goals met â†’ remove from PIP, continue employment (may still affect annual review rating)
  - **Partial**: Some improvement â†’ extend PIP 30 days or move to final warning
  - **Failure**: Goals not met â†’ termination
