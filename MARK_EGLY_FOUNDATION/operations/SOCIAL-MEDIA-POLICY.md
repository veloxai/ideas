## Social Media Philosophy

### Foundation's Social Media Mission

**Goal**: Build vibrant online community where AATD patients, caregivers, researchers, and advocates connect, learn, share experiences, and drive awareness‚Äîultimately improving diagnosis rates and patient outcomes.

**Core Values**:

**1. Community-First**
- Social media is conversation, not broadcast‚Äîlisten as much as post
- Amplify patient voices (share their stories, celebrate milestones)
- Respond to every comment/message within 24 hours (show people matter)

**2. Authentic & Human**
- Foundation has personality‚Äîwarm, hopeful, evidence-based (not corporate/sterile)
- Use real photos (patients, staff, events), not stock images
- Admit mistakes, show behind-the-scenes (transparency builds trust)

**3. Educational & Empowering**
- Every post should teach something (AATD facts, self-advocacy tips, research updates)
- Empower patients with knowledge (genetic testing, treatment options, clinical trials)
- Translate science into plain language (no jargon)

**4. Inclusive & Representative**
- Reflect AATD community diversity (age, race, gender, geography, disease severity)
- Accessible content (alt text for images, captions for videos)
- Multiple languages (Spanish, if budget allows‚Äî10% U.S. Hispanic AATD patients)

**5. Ethical & Compliant**
- HIPAA-compliant (never share patient info without explicit consent)
- FTC-compliant (disclose partnerships, avoid medical advice)
- Platform terms of service (no spamming, impersonation, hate speech)

## Personal vs. Organizational Accounts

### Staff Personal Accounts

**Policy**: Staff can have personal social media accounts‚ÄîFoundation respects free speech‚Äîbut must follow guidelines when mentioning Foundation or AATD.

**Guidelines**:
1. **Identify affiliation** (in bio: "Personal views, not my employer's")
2. **Don't claim to speak for Foundation** (unless authorized spokesperson)

**Enforcement**: First violation = verbal warning (ED), second = written warning, third = termination (if egregious‚Äîe.g., sharing patient info).

## Employee Social Media Guidelines

### "ABCs of Social Media" (Staff Training)

**A = Authentic**: Be yourself, but represent Foundation's values (empathy, accuracy, inclusivity)
**B = Boundaries**: Separate personal from professional (don't argue politics on Foundation account)
**C = Confidentiality**: HIPAA applies online (never screenshot patient messages, share identifiable info)
**D = Disclosure**: If discussing Foundation, note affiliation ("I work at Mark Egly Foundation, views my own")
**E = Ethics**: Follow FTC guidelines (disclose sponsored posts, affiliate links)

---

**DO**:
- ‚úÖ Share Foundation content (helps amplify reach)

## Privacy & HIPAA Compliance

### HIPAA Rules for Social Media

**Protected Health Information (PHI)** = Name + Health Info

**Never Post Without Written Consent**:
- ‚ùå "John Smith was diagnosed with AATD and started treatment"‚Äîidentifies person + condition
- ‚ùå Screenshots of patient messages (even if name hidden, could be identifiable)
- ‚ùå Photos of patients at events (faces visible‚Äîneed photo release)

**OK to Post** (De-identified or Consented):
- ‚úÖ "A 45-year-old patient from Ohio was recently diagnosed"‚Äîno identifying info
- ‚úÖ Patient story with written consent + photo release (signed form)
- ‚úÖ Aggregate data ("We served 200 patients this year"‚Äîno individuals identified)

---

### Photo & Video Releases

**Consent Form** (Required for All Patient Content):
- Patient name, date, signature
- Specific use ("Foundation may use my photo/story on social media, website, print materials")
- Revocability ("I can withdraw consent anytime by emailing info@markegly.org")
- Privacy options (checkbox: "Use first name only" or "Use initials only")

**Storage**: Digital copies in secure folder (password-protected), linked to patient record.

---

### Minors & Dependents

**If Posting About Children with AATD**:
- **Parental consent required** (both parents if joint custody)
- **Consider child's maturity** (teens 13+ should co-sign consent)
- **Protect identity** (first name only, no school names, avoid identifying tattoos/scars)

## Crisis Response on Social Media

### Social Media Crisis Triggers

**Red Flags** (Escalate Immediately):
- üî¥ Viral negative post (patient complaint, donor criticism‚Äî1,000+ shares)
- üî¥ Impersonation account (fake Foundation account)
- üî¥ Misinformation spreading (false claims about AATD, Foundation programs)
- üî¥ Coordinated attack (multiple users posting same negative message‚Äîcould be bots, competitors)
- üî¥ Staff misconduct (employee posts inappropriate content, Foundation tagged)

---

### Crisis Response Protocol

**Immediate Actions** (Within 1 Hour):
1. **Alert ED + Board Chair** (phone/text‚Äîdon't wait for email)
2. **Screenshot everything** (posts, comments, DMs‚Äîevidence for legal if needed)
3. **Consult legal counsel** (before responding if serious‚Äîdefamation, patient harm claims)
4. **Pause scheduled posts** (don't post cheerful content during crisis)
5. **Monitor 24/7** (Crisis Communication Plan Document 19‚Äîassign rotating shifts)

---

### Crisis Response Templates

**Holding Statement** (Buy Time While Investigating):
> "We're aware of [issue] and take it seriously. We're reviewing the matter thoroughly and will provide more information soon. The wellbeing of AATD patients is our top priority."

**Acknowledgment + Action**:
> "We've heard your concerns about [issue]. We're taking the following steps: [1, 2, 3]. We're committed to transparency and will update you by [date]. Thank you for holding us accountable."

**Misinformation Correction**:
> "We've seen misinformation circulating about [topic]. Here are the facts: [1, 2, 3‚Äîwith sources]. We're committed to science-based information. Full statement: [link]."

---

### What NOT to Do in Crisis

**Avoid**:
- ‚ùå **Delete criticism** (unless spam/violates platform rules‚Äîdeleting looks guilty, Streisand effect)
- ‚ùå **Argue with critics** (defensive, emotional responses escalate‚Äîstay professional)
- ‚ùå **"No comment"** (sounds evasive‚Äîuse holding statement)
- ‚ùå **Blame others** ("This is the media's fault"‚Äîtake responsibility if Foundation erred)
- ‚ùå **Go silent** (silence interpreted as guilt‚Äîcommunicate frequently, even if limited info)

## Influencer Partnerships

### What is Influencer Marketing?

**Definition**: Partner with individuals who have large social media followings (10K-1M+) to promote Foundation‚Äîthey post about AATD, tag Foundation, drive awareness.

**Types**:
- **Micro-Influencers** (10K-100K followers): Health bloggers, patient advocates, nurse influencers
- **Macro-Influencers** (100K-1M followers): Celebrity patients (if any), health journalists
- **Celebrity** (1M+ followers): Rare (expensive, usually requires personal connection)

---

### Foundation's Influencer Strategy

**Year 1-3**: No paid influencer partnerships (budget constraints)‚Äîfocus on organic partnerships (patient advocates who post voluntarily)

**Year 4+**: Micro-influencer partnerships ($500-$2,000/post or product donation/event sponsorship)

**Selection Criteria**:
- ‚úÖ **Authentic connection** (AATD patient, caregiver, or genuinely passionate about rare disease)
- ‚úÖ **Engaged audience** (2-5% engagement rate‚Äî10K followers with 500 likes > 100K followers with 500 likes)
- ‚úÖ **Values alignment** (posts align with Foundation's mission, not controversial)

---

- **Clear language**: "#ad" or "#sponsored" at beginning of post (not buried in hashtags)
