## 1. Contract Management Philosophy

### Foundation's Approach to Contracts

**Contracts = Strategic Tools**: Every contract represents a relationship, obligation, or riskâ€”not just paperwork. Well-managed contracts protect the Foundation legally, financially, and operationally while enabling mission delivery (vendor services, research partnerships, employment relationships).

**Lifecycle Mindset**: Contract management doesn't end at signatureâ€”it extends through performance monitoring, renewal decisions, amendments, and eventual termination or expiration. Poor tracking = missed renewals, auto-renewals at unfavorable terms, or service disruptions.

**Risk-Proportionate Rigor**: Not all contracts require attorney review (e.g., $50/month SaaS subscription = template sufficient), but high-value, complex, or novel agreements demand legal scrutiny. System balances efficiency with protection.

---

### Benefits of Structured Contract Management

**For Foundation**:

- âœ… **Legal Protection**: Enforceable terms, clear obligations, dispute resolution mechanisms (avoid "handshake deals" that fail in court)
- âœ… **Cost Control**: Track renewal dates, negotiate better terms, avoid auto-renewals at increased prices ($500-$5K saved annually by proactive renegotiation)
- âœ… **Operational Continuity**: Know when contracts expire, plan vendor transitions or extensions (no service interruptionsâ€”critical for REDCap hosting, Salesforce, payroll)
- âœ… **Compliance**: Ensure HIPAA BAAs, insurance requirements, indemnification, IP ownership terms present (avoid regulatory violations or IP disputes)
- âœ… **Accountability**: Clear owner for each contract (performance monitoring, relationship management, renewal decisions)

**For Partners/Vendors**:

- âœ… **Clarity**: Both parties know expectations, deliverables, payment terms (reduces disputes)
- âœ… **Professionalism**: Well-drafted contracts signal Foundation's maturity, seriousness (attracts better partners)

## 2. Types of Contracts

### Contract Categories (Foundation Context)

**1. Vendor/Service Agreements** (Most Common):

- **SaaS Subscriptions**: REDCap hosting, Salesforce, Google Workspace, 1Password, Zoom, Asana, Mailchimp (monthly/annual subscriptions)
- **Professional Services**: Legal counsel (retainer), CPA (accounting/audit), MSSP (cybersecurity), grant consultants, web developers
- **Office/Operations**: Office lease, insurance policies, janitorial service, internet/phone, utilities

**2. Employment & Contractor Agreements**:

- **Employment Offers**: Offer letters for staff (terms, salary, benefits, at-will or term)
- **Independent Contractors**: Grant writers, event planners, designers, consultants (1099 contracts with scope, deliverables, payment)
- **Volunteers**: Volunteer agreements (liability waiver, confidentiality, role expectations)
- **Interns**: Internship agreements (unpaid/paid, academic credit, learning objectives)

**3. Partnership & Collaboration Agreements**:

- **Research Collaborations**: Academic partnerships (data sharing, co-authorship, IRB coordination)
- **Corporate Sponsorships**: Pharma or biotech sponsors (funding, branding, restrictions)
- **Patient Organization MOUs**: Coalitions, joint campaigns (resource sharing, co-branding, roles)
- **Event Partnerships**: Co-hosted conferences, galas (revenue split, responsibilities)

**4. Real Property & Facilities**:

- **Office Lease**: Commercial lease for Foundation headquarters (term, rent, escalation, maintenance, early termination)
- **Storage**: Warehouse or offsite records storage (if physical archives)
- **Event Venues**: Venue contracts for annual gala, conferences (dates, capacity, cancellation terms)

**5. Intellectual Property Agreements**:

- **Trademark Licenses**: Use of "Mark Egly Foundation" name/logo by partners (restrictions, quality control)
- **Content Licenses**: Stock photos, music, software licenses (perpetual vs. subscription)
- **Researcher Agreements**: IP ownership for Foundation-funded research (grant-back rights, commercialization sharing)

**6. Grant & Funding Agreements**:

- **Grant Awards (Foundation = Grantee)**: NIH, foundations, corporate grants (terms, reporting, allowable costs, IP, close-out)
- **Sub-Awards (Foundation = Grantor)**: If Foundation makes grants to researchers (rare Year 1-3, common Year 5+)

**7. Data Use & Privacy Agreements**:

- **Data Use Agreements (DUAs)**: Sharing registry data with researchers (HIPAA-compliant, permitted uses, security, destruction)
- **Business Associate Agreements (BAAs)**: Vendors accessing PHI (HIPAA-requiredâ€”REDCap host, Salesforce, email marketing if patient lists)
- **Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs)**: Protect confidential info when discussing partnerships, sharing donor lists, or providing patient data for proposals

**8. Insurance Policies**:

- General liability, D&O, cyber insurance, workers' comp (technically policies = contracts with insurersâ€”track renewals, coverage changes)

## 3. Contract Lifecycle Overview

### Seven Stages of Contract Lifecycle

**Stage 1: Request & Need Identification**:

- Trigger: Business need arises (need CPA for audit, need grant writer, need new SaaS tool)
- Owner: Department lead (COO, ED, Research Coordinator) identifies need, proposes vendor/partner

**Stage 2: Drafting & Negotiation**:

- Use template (if availableâ€”vendor agreement, employment offer) or custom draft (legal counsel for complex)
- Negotiate terms (price, deliverables, liability, termination, IP, dispute resolution)
- Redline iterations (track versionsâ€”"v1 Foundation draft," "v2 Vendor markup," "v3 Final")

**Stage 3: Review & Approval**:

- Legal review (if high-value or complexâ€”threshold: >$10K/year or novel terms)
- Financial review (COO/CFOâ€”budget impact, payment terms)
- ED approval (final sign-off per signature authority matrixâ€”see Section 6)

**Stage 4: Execution**:

- Both parties sign (electronic via DocuSign or manual ink signatures)
- Fully executed copy distributed to both parties
- Store in contract repository (Google Drive or contract management software)

**Stage 5: Performance & Monitoring**:

- Track deliverables, milestones, payments (COO or contract owner monitorsâ€”vendor delivering on time? quality acceptable?)
- Address performance issues (written notice if vendor breaches, cure period, escalate to termination if not remedied)

**Stage 6: Renewal or Expiration**:

- Review contract 90-120 days before expiration (continue? renegotiate? switch vendors?)
- If renew: Initiate renewal process (may require amended contract or simple renewal notice)
- If terminate: Follow termination procedures (notice period, data return, transition to new vendor)

**Stage 7: Close-Out & Archive**:

- Final payment, final deliverables received
- Archive executed contract + amendments (retain 7 years per records retention policy)
- Document lessons learned (vendor performance, contract terms that worked or didn'tâ€”inform future contracts)

## 4. Contract Templates & Standardization

### Why Templates Matter

**Efficiency**: Don't reinvent wheelâ€”standard vendor agreement template saves 5-10 hours attorney time per contract ($1K-$2K savings)

**Consistency**: Same core terms across similar contracts (liability caps, indemnification, governing lawâ€”reduces confusion, easier to compare)

**Risk Mitigation**: Pre-vetted terms protect Foundation (attorney drafted template includes favorable clausesâ€”vendor can propose changes but starting point strong)

---

### Foundation's Template Library

**Tier 1: Standard Templates** (Attorney-Drafted, Reusable):

- [ ] **Vendor Services Agreement** (professional servicesâ€”consultants, contractors; includes scope, payment, IP ownership, confidentiality, termination)
- [ ] **SaaS Subscription Agreement** (standard for cloud softwareâ€”license grant, data ownership, BAA if PHI, auto-renewal, termination)
- [ ] **Employment Offer Letter** (at-will employmentâ€”position, salary, benefits, start date, contingencies like background check)
- [ ] **Independent Contractor Agreement** (1099 contractorsâ€”scope, deliverables, payment schedule, IP assignment, no employment relationship)

**Tier 2: Custom Contracts** (Attorney-Drafted, Unique):

### Template Usage Guidelines

**When to Use Template**:

- âœ… Routine transaction (hiring consultant, subscribing to SaaS, engaging vendor for standard service)
- âœ… Low dollar value (<$10K/yearâ€”risk-proportionate)
- âœ… No novel legal issues (straightforward services, no complex IP or liability questions)

- âŒ High value (>$10K/year or >$25K total contract value)
- âŒ Novel terms (first-time contract typeâ€”e.g., first biobank partnership, first international collaboration)

**Process**: COO starts with template â†’ fills in blanks (parties, scope, price, term) â†’ sends to legal counsel if customization or high-value â†’ negotiate with counterparty â†’ finalize

## 5. Review & Approval Workflows

### Standard Workflow (Most Contracts)

**Step 1: Business Owner Initiates**:

- Department lead (COO, ED, Research Coordinator) identifies need (e.g., need CPA for Year 2 audit)
- Drafts contract using template or obtains vendor's proposed contract
- Completes Contract Request Form (Google Formâ€”contract type, counterparty, value, term, business justification)

**Step 2: Legal Review** (If Requiredâ€”see thresholds):

- COO forwards draft to legal counsel (external attorney on retainer or pro bono counsel)
- Attorney reviews for legal risks (liability, indemnification, IP, complianceâ€”HIPAA BAA present if PHI?, insurance adequate?)
- Attorney provides redlines or approval memo (2-5 business days turnaround)

**Step 3: Financial Review** (COO or CFOâ€”Year 1 COO = CFO):

- Verify budget availability (contract cost fits within approved budget line item?)
- Review payment terms (upfront? installments? milestone-based? Foundation cash flow adequate?)
- Approve or flag concerns (if over budget, escalate to ED or defer to next fiscal year)

**Step 4: ED Approval** (Per Signature Authorityâ€”see Section 6):

- ED reviews business case, legal/financial sign-offs
- Approves or denies (denial reasons: over budget, strategic misalignment, legal risk too high)
- If approved, ED authorizes signature (ED signs or delegates to COO if within COO authority)

**Step 5: Execution**:

- Send to counterparty for signature (DocuSign or email PDF)
- Once both parties sign, distribute fully executed copy to: contract owner, COO, legal counsel (if reviewed)
- Upload to contract repository (Google Drive folder: "Contracts - Executed")

---

### Expedited Workflow (Low-Value, Low-Risk)

**Thresholds**:

- <$5K total contract value
- Standard template used (no attorney review)
- No PHI, IP, or liability concerns

**Process**:

- Business owner drafts using template
- COO reviews and approves (same-day or 24-hour turnaround)
- COO signs (per signature authority) or escalates to ED if needed
- Execute and file

**Example**: Subscribing to Zoom Pro ($15/month, $180/year) = expedited (use vendor's terms, COO approves, COO signs via click-wrap or PO)

## 6. Signature Authority Matrix

### Who Can Sign What?

**Purpose**: Prevent unauthorized commitments (staff can't sign $100K contract without approval), ensure appropriate oversight (Board approval for major commitments), enable operational efficiency (don't bottleneck ED for every $500 purchase)

---

### Authority Levels

| **Signatory**          | **Authority Limit**                               | **Contract Types**                                                     | **Board Pre-Approval Required?**        |
| ---------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| **Executive Director** | Up to $50K/year; $100K total contract value       | All contracts within budget                                            | No (unless >$100K or multi-year >$200K) |
| **COO**                | Up to $10K/year; $25K total                       | Vendor agreements, SaaS, consultants, employment offers (<$75K salary) | No                                      |
| **Department Heads**   | Up to $2K/year                                    | Routine vendor services, supplies                                      | No (with COO pre-approval)              |
| **Board Chair**        | Unlimited (with Board vote)                       | Real estate, debt, major grants, ED employment contract                | Yes (Board vote required)               |
| **Board Treasurer**    | Co-signature required for $50K-$100K ED contracts | Financial contracts, loans                                             | Yes (Board vote)                        |

---

### Special Cases

**Real Estate** (Office Lease, Purchase):

- Board approval required (vote at Board meetingâ€”majority or supermajority per bylaws)
- ED + Board Chair co-sign

**Debt / Loans**:

- Board approval required (borrowing >$25Kâ€”line of credit, mortgage, bonds)
- ED + Board Treasurer co-sign

**Multi-Year Commitments** (>3 years or >$200K total):

- Board approval required (long-term obligation binds future Boardsâ€”governance prudence)
- ED signs after Board vote

**Grant Awards to Foundation** (Foundation = Grantee):

- ED signature sufficient (accepting funds = operational decision, not Board-level unless funder requires Board resolution)
- Inform Board at next meeting (transparencyâ€”"We received $100K NIH grant")

**ED Employment Contract**:

- Board approval required (Board hires ED, negotiates compensation)
- Board Chair signs on behalf of Foundation

---

### Delegation

**ED May Delegate** (in writing):

- COO signature authority up to COO's limit ($10K/year)â€”e.g., "COO authorized to sign vendor contracts <$10K/year on my behalf"
- Temporary delegation if ED unavailable (travel, medical leaveâ€”"COO serves as Acting ED with full signature authority for 2 weeks")

**COO May Not Delegate**: Department heads can't sign contracts without COO explicit authorization

## 7. Execution & Counterparty Management

### Execution Methods

**Electronic Signature** (Preferred Year 1+):

- **Platform**: DocuSign ($10-$25/month per userâ€”Foundation Business plan)
- **Process**: Upload PDF â†’ add signature fields â†’ send to counterparty email â†’ both parties sign electronically â†’ auto-distribute fully executed PDF

**Wet Ink Signature** (Rareâ€”if counterparty requires or high-formality contracts):

## 9. Renewal & Expiration Tracking

### Why Renewal Tracking Matters

**Risk of Missed Renewals**:

- Service interruption (REDCap hosting expires â†’ registry offline, research haltsâ€”$10K-$50K project disruption)
- Auto-renewal at unfavorable terms (SaaS vendor auto-renews at 20% price increase because Foundation missed 60-day cancellation windowâ€”pay $2K more annually or breach contract to cancel)
- Lapsed insurance (workers' comp policy not renewed â†’ employee injured, Foundation pays out-of-pocket $50K-$500K medical + potential OSHA fines)

---

### Renewal Process

**Timeline** (Standard):

- **T-120 days**: COO reviews upcoming expirations (quarterly scanâ€”contracts expiring in next 90 days)
- **T-90 days**: Contract owner (department lead) evaluates: Renew? Renegotiate? Switch vendors? Let expire?
- **T-60 days**: If renew, initiate renewal process (vendor outreach, price negotiation, draft renewal amendment or new contract if major changes)
- **T-30 days**: Execute renewal agreement (before auto-renewal deadline if applicableâ€”many SaaS contracts require 30-60 day cancellation notice)
- **T-0 (Expiration)**: New term begins seamlessly (or contract terminates if decided not to renew)

---

### Renewal Decision Matrix

**Renew (No Changes)**:

- âœ… Vendor performance excellent (deliverables met, responsive, quality high)
- âœ… Price competitive (market research shows comparable or better than alternatives)
- âœ… No strategic changes (Foundation still needs serviceâ€”e.g., still need CPA, still need REDCap)

**Renew (Renegotiate Terms)**:

- âš ï¸ Price increased significantly (vendor proposes 20% increaseâ€”negotiate down to 5-10% or threaten to switch)
- âš ï¸ Scope changed (Foundation needs more/lessâ€”REDCap users increased from 5 to 15, need volume discount)
- âš ï¸ Vendor performance issues (missed deadlines, quality declinedâ€”renegotiate SLA, penalties, or exit)

**Do Not Renew (Switch Vendors or Eliminate Service)**:

- âŒ Vendor breach (significant performance failure, security breach, HIPAA violationâ€”terminate for cause)
- âŒ Better alternative (competitor offers superior service, lower price, better featuresâ€”e.g., migrate Salesforce to HubSpot if saves $5K/year)
- âŒ No longer needed (Foundation pivots strategyâ€”eliminate patient navigation program, don't need patient navigator job posting service)

## 10. Amendment & Modification Procedures

### When Amendments Required

**Contract Changes During Term**:

- **Scope Change**: Add deliverables, increase/decrease service level (e.g., hire grant writer for 2 more grants/yearâ€”amend to increase scope and fee)
- **Price Adjustment**: Mid-term price change (inflation adjustment, volume discount retroactively applied)
- **Term Extension**: Extend expiration date (pilot project successful, extend 6 months while negotiate full contract renewal)
- **Party Change**: Vendor acquired by another company (successor-in-interest clauseâ€”new entity assumes contract)

---

### Amendment Process

**Step 1: Mutual Agreement**:

- Both parties agree change needed (email exchange, meeting notes documenting intent)
- Draft amendment (use "Amendment No. 1 to [Contract Name]" template)

**Step 2: Amendment Document**:

- **Recitals**: Reference original contract ("WHEREAS, parties entered into Vendor Services Agreement dated January 1, 2024...")
- **Amendments**: Specific changes ("Section 3.1 'Scope of Work' is amended to add: '...additional 5 grants per year...'")
- **Unchanged Terms**: "All other terms remain in full force and effect"
- **Signatures**: Both parties sign (same authority level as original contractâ€”if ED signed original, ED signs amendment)

**Step 3: Execute & File**:

- Both parties sign amendment
- Attach amendment to original contract in repository (Google Driveâ€”save as `2024-01-01-Vendor-Agreement-Amendment-1.pdf` in same folder as original)
- Update contract summary sheet (reflect new scope, price, expiration date)

**Best Practice**: Never modify original contract via email or verbal agreementâ€”always formal written amendment (protects both parties, avoids "he said / she said" disputes)

## 11. Termination & Exit Procedures

### Termination Scenarios

**1. Term Expiration** (Natural End):

- Contract reaches end of term (12-month agreement ends December 31, 2025â€”no renewal)
- Process: No action required (contract terminates automatically unless renewal clause states auto-renew)

**2. Termination for Convenience** (Either Party Can Exit):

- Many contracts include clause: "Either party may terminate upon 30/60/90 days written notice"
- Foundation decides to terminate (vendor performance adequate but switching to cheaper alternative, or eliminating service)
- Process: Send written termination notice (email + certified mailâ€”specify termination date 30+ days out), pay through termination date, return vendor property if any

**3. Termination for Cause** (Breach):

- Vendor breaches material term (misses critical deadline, fails to deliver, breaches confidentiality, HIPAA violation)
- Foundation sends "Notice of Breach" (writtenâ€”specify breach, demand cure within 15-30 days per contract cure period)
- If not cured, Foundation sends "Notice of Termination for Cause" (effective immediately or per contract notice period)
- No further payment due (unless contract specifies payment for work completed to dateâ€”pro-rated)

**4. Mutual Termination**:

- Both parties agree relationship not working (vendor can't deliver, Foundation can't pay, strategic misalignment)
- Negotiate termination agreement (settlementâ€”Foundation may owe partial payment, vendor may waive fees, mutual release of claims)

---

### Exit Checklist

**Before Termination Effective Date**:

- [ ] Identify replacement vendor (if applicableâ€”don't terminate REDCap hosting until new host ready)
- [ ] Data migration (export data, transfer to Foundation or new vendorâ€”e.g., Salesforce data export before canceling)
- [ ] Vendor property return (laptops, access badges, keys if vendor had on-site access)
- [ ] Foundation property return (prepaid fees, equipment Foundation loaned to vendor)
- [ ] Access revocation (revoke vendor's access to Foundation systemsâ€”email, VPN, databases)
- [ ] Final invoice reconciliation (pay outstanding invoices, close out account, zero balance)
- [ ] Confirm data destruction (vendor must delete Foundation data per contractâ€”written certification required)

**Post-Termination**:

- [ ] Archive contract + termination notice (Google Drive Expired folderâ€”retain 7 years)
- [ ] Update vendor repository (mark "Terminated," end date, reason, performance notesâ€”inform future decisions if consider re-engaging)
- [ ] Lessons learned (COO documents what worked, what didn'tâ€”avoid repeating mistakes with next vendor)

## 12. Vendor & Partner Risk Assessment

### Pre-Contract Risk Evaluation

**Purpose**: Assess vendor/partner before signing contractâ€”financial stability, reputation, security, complianceâ€”avoid contracting with risky entities that could harm Foundation (data breach, financial failure, reputational association)
