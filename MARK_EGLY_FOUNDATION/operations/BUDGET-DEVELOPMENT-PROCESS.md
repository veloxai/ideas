## Budget Philosophy & Principles

### Foundation's Budgeting Values

**Principle 1: Mission-Driven Resource Allocation**
Every dollar budgeted must advance AATD patient advocacy, research, education, or support. Budget is strategic documentâ€”not just financial spreadsheet.

**Principle 2: Fiscal Responsibility**
Budget balanced (revenues â‰¥ expenses) or planned deficit funded by reserves. No surprise deficits, no wishful revenue projections.

**Principle 3: Transparency & Accountability**
All departments participate, Board approves, staff held accountable. Budget assumptions documented, variances explained.

**Principle 4: Flexibility Within Discipline**
Budget is roadmap, not handcuffsâ€”allow reasonable adjustments (10-15% between line items) without Board re-approval, but major changes require oversight.

**Principle 5: Growth Mindset**
Budget for impact, not just survival. If opportunity arises (major grant, new partnership), Foundation can adaptâ€”but within fiscal prudence.

### Budget Types

**Annual Operating Budget** (This Document's Focus):
- **Scope**: Day-to-day operations (salaries, programs, rent, supplies)
- **Timeframe**: Fiscal year (January 1 - December 31)
- **Purpose**: Authorize spending, guide operations, measure performance

**Capital Budget** (See Capital Budget Section):
- **Scope**: Long-term assets (building renovations, equipment >$5K, technology infrastructure)
- **Timeframe**: Multi-year (5-10 year plan)
- **Purpose**: Plan major investments, assess ROI

**Program Budgets** (See Program-Level Section):
- **Scope**: Individual programs (Patient Navigation, Research Grants, Education)
- **Timeframe**: Program duration (1-3 years, often aligned with grants)
- **Purpose**: Track program-specific revenues/expenses, evaluate program sustainability

## Revenue Projections & Forecasting

### Revenue Forecasting Methods

**Method 1: Historical Trend Analysis** (Most Common)
- **Data**: Review past 3-5 years revenue by source
- **Trend**: Calculate average annual growth rate
- **Projection**: Apply growth rate to current year, adjust for known changes

**Example**:
- FY2023 individual giving: $400,000
- FY2024 individual giving: $450,000 (12.5% growth)
- FY2025 individual giving: $500,000 (11.1% growth)
- **Average growth**: 11.8%/year
- **FY2026 projection**: $500,000 Ã— 1.118 = $559,000 (round to $560,000)

---

**Method 2: Pipeline Analysis** (For Grants, Major Gifts)
- **Identify prospects**: All active grant applications, donor cultivation
- **Assign probability**: High (70-90%), Medium (40-60%), Low (10-30%)
- **Calculate expected value**: Amount Ã— Probability

**Example**:

| **Grant/Donor**      | **Amount** | **Probability** | **Expected Value** | **Decision Timeline** |
| -------------------- | ---------- | --------------- | ------------------ | --------------------- |
| ABC Foundation       | $150,000   | 80% (finalist)  | $120,000           | November 2025         |
| XYZ Corporation      | $100,000   | 50% (submitted) | $50,000            | January 2026          |
| Smith Family (donor) | $50,000    | 70% (cultivated)| $35,000            | Q1 2026               |
| Federal Grant (HRSA) | $500,000   | 30% (competitive)| $150,000          | March 2026            |
| **Total Pipeline**   | **$800,000**| -              | **$355,000**       |                       |

**Conservative Budget**: Use expected value ($355,000), not full pipeline ($800,000)â€”avoids over-optimistic budgeting.

---

**Method 3: Capacity-Based Forecasting** (For Donor Acquisition)
- **Calculate capacity**: How many donors can Development team cultivate/solicit?
- **Apply conversion rates**: Based on historical data

**Example**:
- **Major gift prospects**: 50 identified, 2 FTE staff can manage 25 each
- **Conversion rate**: 20% (10 donors will give)
- **Average gift**: $30,000
- **Revenue**: 10 donors Ã— $30,000 = **$300,000**

### Conservative vs. Optimistic Budgeting

**Conservative Approach** (Foundation's Policy for Year 1-3):
- **Revenue**: Budget 85-90% of realistic projections (cushion for underperformance)
- **Expenses**: Budget 100-105% of realistic needs (cushion for overruns)
- **Result**: Built-in surplus or small planned deficit (covered by reserves)

**Balanced Approach** (Year 4-7):
- **Revenue**: Budget 90-95% of projections
- **Expenses**: Budget 100% of needs
- **Result**: Breakeven or small surplus

**Growth Approach** (Year 8+, Mature Foundation):
- **Revenue**: Budget 95-100% of projections (confidence in fundraising capacity)
- **Expenses**: Budget 100-105% (invest in growthâ€”new programs, staff)
- **Result**: Balanced budget, strategic investments

**Foundation's FY2026 Budget Stance**: Balanced (Year 5 of operations, proven track record, confident in $1.77M revenue projection).

## Expense Budget Development

### Cost Categories

**Fixed Costs** (predictable, recurring):
- Salaries & benefits (contractual)
- Rent (lease agreement)
- Insurance premiums (annual policies)
- Software subscriptions (annual licenses)
- **Total fixed**: ~70-75% of operating budget

**Variable Costs** (fluctuate with activity):
- Program supplies (depends on # patients served)
- Travel (depends on # conferences, donor visits)
- Marketing (campaign-driven)
- Event expenses (depend on event size)
- **Total variable**: ~15-20% of operating budget

**Semi-Variable Costs** (partially fixed, partially variable):
- Utilities (base cost + usage)
- Postage (some regular mailings, some ad-hoc)
- Professional development (some budgeted, some opportunistic)
- **Total semi-variable**: ~5-10% of operating budget

**Budget Flexibility**: Variable costs easier to cut if revenue shortfall (pause marketing campaign, reduce travel)â€”fixed costs harder (can't fire staff mid-year without cause, can't break lease).

---

### Zero-Based vs. Incremental Budgeting

**Incremental Budgeting** (Common, Efficient):
- Start with prior year's budget
- Apply inflation adjustments (3% for most categories)
- Add new initiatives (new programs, staff)
- **Pros**: Fast, simple, maintains continuity
- **Cons**: Perpetuates inefficiencies ("we've always spent $50K on X")

**Zero-Based Budgeting** (Time-Intensive, Strategic):
- Start from zero each year
- Justify every dollar (even existing programs)
- **Pros**: Forces prioritization, identifies waste
- **Cons**: Labor-intensive, can disrupt operations

**Foundation's Approach**:
- **Incremental budgeting** (Year 1-5)â€”building infrastructure, rapid growth
- **Zero-based budgeting** (every 3-5 years)â€”strategic reset, ensure alignment with evolved mission

## Restricted vs. Unrestricted Funds

### Fund Accounting Basics

**Unrestricted Funds**:
- **Definition**: No donor restrictionsâ€”Board discretion how spent
- **Sources**: Individual donations, unrestricted grants, event revenue, investment income
- **Uses**: Operations, programs, reserves, strategic initiatives
- **Accounting**: "Net Assets Without Donor Restrictions" (GAAP term)

**Temporarily Restricted Funds**:
- **Definition**: Donor restricts use (purpose or time)
- **Example**: "$100,000 for AATD research grants, to be spent by December 31, 2026"
- **Sources**: Restricted grants, donor-designated gifts
- **Uses**: Only for restricted purpose (Foundation must track, prove compliance)
- **Accounting**: "Net Assets With Donor Restrictions" â†’ Released when spent on purpose

**Permanently Restricted Funds**:
- **Definition**: Endowment principalâ€”never spent (only income spent)
- **Example**: "$1M endowment, spend 5% annually ($50K) forever"
- **Accounting**: "Net Assets With Donor Restrictionsâ€”Perpetual"

## Budget Consolidation & Review

### CFO Consolidation Process (September)

**Step 1: Compile All Departmental Budgets**
- Import revenue projections into master budget spreadsheet
- Import expense projections by department, category
- Calculate totals (revenue, expenses, surplus/deficit)

**Step 2: Identify Gaps**
- Revenue shortfall: Projected expenses $1.9M, revenue $1.7M = $200K gap
- Over-optimistic projections: Development projects 50% growth (realistic?)

**Step 3: Facilitate Trade-Offs** (Budget Review Meetings)
- **If revenue shortfall**:
  - Ask Development to increase goal OR
  - Ask departments to trim expenses (10% across-the-board cut) OR
  - Board approves planned deficit (funded by reserves)
- **If conflicting priorities**:
  - Program wants 3 new navigators ($300K), Research wants 5 grants ($250K), total $550K available â†’ negotiate compromise (2 navigators + 4 grants)

## Budget Amendments & Adjustments

### When Amendment Required

**Board Re-Approval Needed If**:
- Total budget changes >10% ($177K for $1.77M budget)
- New program added (not in original budget)
- Major revenue source lost (>$100K grant denied)
- Strategic shift (Board decides to launch new initiative mid-year)

**Process**:
1. CFO prepares amendment memo (justification, revised budget, impact on reserves)
2. Finance Committee reviews, recommends to Board
3. Board votes (simple majority)
4. Amended budget becomes official

---

### Line-Item Flexibility

**Allowed Without Board Re-Approval**:
- Transfers <10% between line items within same category (e.g., move $5K from "Supplies" to "Postage" within Operations)
- Transfers between departments if total budget unchanged (e.g., move $10K from Development to Programsâ€”requires CFO + ED approval)

**Not Allowed Without Board Approval**:
- Transfers >10% between major categories (e.g., move $100K from Programs to Personnel)
- Overspending total budget (creates deficit beyond approved level)

**Documentation**: All line-item transfers logged in "Budget Adjustment Register"â€”reported to Board quarterly.

## Capital Budget vs. Operating Budget

**Definition**: Expenditures on assets with useful life >1 year and cost >$5,000 (Foundation's capitalization threshold).

**Accounting Treatment**:
