## Logo Guidelines

### Primary Logo

**Description**: Wordmark "MARK EGLY FOUNDATION" with icon of **stylized lungs + heart** integrated into the "E" of "EGLY"

**Symbolism**:

- **Lungs**: AATD affects lungs (emphysema)
- **Heart**: Compassion, care, and love at center of our mission

- `MEF-Logo-Primary.svg` (vector, scalable)
- `MEF-Logo-Primary.png` (raster, high-res)

---

### Logo Variations

**Horizontal Logo**:

- Full foundation name + icon
- **Use**: Most common (website header, letterhead, presentations)
- Minimum width: 2 inches / 200px

**Vertical Logo**:

- Foundation name stacked, icon above
- **Use**: Narrow spaces (social media profile, banners)
- Minimum width: 1.5 inches / 150px

**Icon Only**:

- Just the lungs/heart icon
- **Use**: App icons, favicons, small social graphics
- **Only when "Mark Egly Foundation" is already visible**

**Tagline Version**:

- Logo + tagline "Eliminating AATD Diagnostic Delays"
- **Use**: First impressions (new materials, ads, brochures)

---

**Maintain clear space around logo equal to the height of the "M" in "MARK"**

---

### Color Accessibility

**Contrast Requirements** (WCAG AA):

- Text must have 4.5:1 contrast ratio with background
- Large text (18pt+) must have 3:1 contrast ratio

**Approved Combinations**:

- ‚úÖ Deep Blue text on White background (16.8:1)
- ‚úÖ White text on Deep Blue background (16.8:1)
- ‚úÖ Teal text on White background (4.8:1)
- ‚úÖ Warm Gray text on White background (4.7:1)
- ‚ùå Teal text on Deep Blue (2.9:1 - insufficient)

**Always test color combinations for accessibility.**

## Typography

### Primary Typeface: **Inter**

**Why Inter**:

- Modern, clean, professional
- Excellent readability (web and print)
- Open-source (free)
- Wide range of weights

**Download**: Google Fonts or rsms.me/inter

---

### Type Hierarchy

**Headlines (H1)**:

- Font: Inter Bold (700)
- Size: 48pt / 3rem
- Color: Deep Blue (#003D5B)
- Use: Page titles, major sections

**Subheadings (H2)**:

- Font: Inter Semi-Bold (600)
- Size: 36pt / 2.25rem
- Color: Deep Blue or Teal
- Use: Section titles

**Subheadings (H3)**:

- Font: Inter Medium (500)
- Size: 24pt / 1.5rem
- Color: Deep Blue or Warm Gray
- Use: Subsection titles

**Body Text**:

- Font: Inter Regular (400)
- Size: 16pt / 1rem
- Color: Warm Gray (#6B7280)
- Line Height: 1.6
- Use: Paragraphs, body content

**Call-to-Action Text**:

- Font: Inter Bold (700)
- Size: 18pt / 1.125rem
- Color: White (on Coral button)
- Use: Buttons, key actions

**Caption Text**:

- Font: Inter Regular (400)
- Size: 14pt / 0.875rem
- Color: Warm Gray
- Use: Photo captions, footnotes

---

### Type Guidelines

**Sentence Case vs Title Case**:

- Headlines: Title Case ("Eliminating Diagnostic Delays")
- Subheadings: Sentence case ("What is AATD?")
- Body: Sentence case

**Line Length**:

- Optimal: 50-75 characters per line
- Maximum: 90 characters

**Alignment**:

- Body text: Left-aligned (easier to read)
- Headlines: Left or center (depending on layout)
- Never justify body text (creates awkward spacing)

---

### Alternate Typeface: **Georgia** (Print Fallback)

**Use**: If Inter is unavailable (rare)

- Headlines: Georgia Bold
- Body: Georgia Regular

## Photography & Imagery

### Photography Style

**Authentic & Human**:

- Real AATD patients (with permission)
- Candid moments, not overly staged
- Diverse ages, races, genders, abilities
- Natural lighting (avoid harsh flash)

**Emotion & Hope**:

- Smiling faces, positive moments
- Connection (patients with family, doctors, community)
- Hopefulness without ignoring reality

**Avoid**:

- ‚ùå Stock photos that feel generic or fake
- ‚ùå Overly clinical or sterile medical settings
- ‚ùå Images that emphasize sickness over person
- ‚ùå Photos without proper consent/releases

---

### Photo Subjects

**Preferred**:

- ‚úÖ AATD patients living full lives
- ‚úÖ Families supporting each other
- ‚úÖ Doctors and patients collaborating
- ‚úÖ Advocates at rallies or meetings

### Image Treatment

**Color Overlay** (optional):

- Light Teal overlay (20% opacity) on photos for consistency
- Use for hero images or backgrounds

**Filters**:

- Slight desaturation for professional look

**Text on Images**:

- Ensure sufficient contrast (darken or lighten image behind text)
- Use white or Deep Blue text on photos
- Add subtle dark gradient behind text if needed

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Voice (Consistent Across All Communications)

**Urgent**:

- We communicate the stakes clearly
- "Every year of delay means progression of disease"
- "Time matters. Let's act now."

**Hopeful**:

- We inspire belief that change is possible
- "Together, we're cutting diagnostic delays in half"
- "You're not alone in this."

**Expert**:

- We speak with authority and evidence
- "Research shows early detection saves lives"
- "Our CME course has trained 500 physicians"

**Compassionate**:

- We acknowledge struggle and offer support
- "We know how hard this journey is"
- "We're here to help you navigate"

**Accessible**:

- We explain complex topics simply
- No jargon without explanation
- "AATD (Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency) is a genetic condition..."

---

### Tone (Adapts to Context)

**Patient-Facing Materials** (empathetic, supportive):

- "You deserve answers. We're here to help you get tested."
- Focus on emotional support and practical resources

**Medical/Professional Audience** (authoritative, evidence-based):

- "Diagnostic delays average 7-8 years, costing patients lung function and quality of life."
- Focus on clinical data and CME opportunities

**Fundraising/Donor Communications** (inspiring, urgent):

- "Your gift today funds 10 AATD tests, potentially saving lives."
- Focus on impact and return on investment

**Advocacy/Policy Materials** (assertive, data-driven):

- "State-by-state analysis shows newborn screening for AATD reduces delays by 90%."
- Focus on policy solutions and legislative asks

---

### Writing Style

**Active Voice**: "We diagnose patients faster" (not "Patients are diagnosed faster by us")

**Short Sentences**: Especially for complex topics

**Personal Pronouns**: "We," "You," "Our" (not third-person "The foundation")

**Bullet Points**: For readability (like this section!)

**Storytelling**: Lead with patient stories, then data

**Calls-to-Action**: Direct and clear ("Donate Now," not "Consider Supporting")

## Brand Don'ts

### Logo Don'ts

‚ùå Don't stretch or distort logo
‚ùå Don't change logo colors
‚ùå Don't add effects (shadows, glows, gradients)
‚ùå Don't rotate or flip logo
‚ùå Don't place logo on low-contrast background
‚ùå Don't use outdated logo versions

---

### Color Don'ts

‚ùå Don't use colors outside approved palette
‚ùå Don't use low-contrast combinations
‚ùå Don't use neon or overly saturated colors
‚ùå Don't use black for body text (use Warm Gray instead)

---

### Typography Don'ts

‚ùå Don't use more than 2 font families in one piece
‚ùå Don't use all caps for body text (hard to read)
‚ùå Don't use ornate or script fonts (hard to read, inconsistent with brand)
‚ùå Don't set body text smaller than 14pt (accessibility)

---

### Photography Don'ts

‚ùå Don't use photos without patient consent
‚ùå Don't use generic stock photos
‚ùå Don't use photos that emphasize sickness over person
‚ùå Don't use low-resolution or pixelated images

---

### Messaging Don'ts

‚ùå Don't use jargon without explanation
‚ùå Don't be overly medical or clinical in patient materials
‚ùå Don't be preachy or condescending
‚ùå Don't make promises we can't keep
‚ùå Don't focus only on problems (balance with solutions and hope)
