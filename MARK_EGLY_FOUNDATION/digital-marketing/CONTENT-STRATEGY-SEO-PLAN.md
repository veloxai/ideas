## Executive Summary

Content is the engine that drives website traffic, educates audiences, builds community, and converts visitors into supporters. A strategic content and SEO plan ensures the Mark Egly Foundation reaches the right audiences with the right messages at the right time.

**Strategic Objectives:**

1. **Drive Organic Traffic:** Rank #1-3 on Google for key Alpha-1 search terms within 12 months
2. **Educate & Convert:** Turn website visitors into patients served, donors acquired, and advocates mobilized
3. **Build Authority:** Establish Mark Egly Foundation as trusted Alpha-1 information source
4. **Support Programs:** Create content enabling Patient Assistance, Research Grants, Testing Programs, and Fundraising
5. **Engage Community:** Foster ongoing relationship with patients, families, donors through regular content

**Content Pillars (5 Core Topics):**

1. **Alpha-1 Education** (What is Alpha-1, diagnosis, treatment, living with condition)
2. **Patient Support & Stories** (Assistance programs, patient journeys, hope and community)
3. **Research & Science** (Grant opportunities, research findings, clinical trials)
4. **Advocacy & Awareness** (Testing campaigns, policy updates, rare disease advocacy)
5. **Foundation News & Impact** (Events, partnerships, financial transparency, donor stories)

**Content Mix:**

- **Website Pages:** 60-80 core pages (static, evergreen content)
- **Blog Posts:** 2-4 per month (48-96 annually)
- **Patient Stories:** 1-2 per month (12-24 annually)
- **Videos:** 10-20 in Year 1 (Mark's story, patient testimonials, educational explainers)
- **Email Newsletters:** Monthly (12 annually)
- **Social Media:** Daily posts (365+ annually)
- **Downloadable Resources:** 10-15 guides/toolkits/fact sheets

**Year 1 SEO Goals:**

- **Organic Traffic:** 5,000-10,000 visitors/month by Month 12
- **Keyword Rankings:** Top 3 positions for 10+ primary keywords, Top 10 for 50+ secondary keywords
- **Backlinks:** 50-100 high-quality backlinks from medical sites, patient organizations, news outlets
- **Domain Authority:** Increase from 0 to 25-35 (Moz metric, scale 1-100)

**Budget:** $25,000-$40,000 annually

- Content creation: $15,000-$25,000 (writers, video, design)
- SEO tools & services: $5,000-$10,000
- Paid promotion (boost high-performing content): $5,000

### SEO Strategy by Phase

**Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)**

**Goals:**

- Establish website as legitimate, trustworthy source (Google trust signals)
- Optimize core pages for primary keywords
- Fix technical SEO issues

**Tactics:**

1. **Technical SEO Audit & Fixes:**
   - **Site Speed:** Optimize images, enable caching, minify CSS/JS (goal: <3 seconds load time)
   - **Mobile-Friendly:** Ensure responsive design, test on Google Mobile-Friendly Test
   - **SSL Certificate:** HTTPS enabled (security + SEO ranking factor)
   - **XML Sitemap:** Create and submit to Google Search Console
   - **Robots.txt:** Configure to allow search engines to crawl site
   - **Structured Data (Schema Markup):** Add Organization schema, Article schema for blog posts, FAQ schema
   - **Fix Crawl Errors:** Identify and fix 404 errors, redirect broken links

2. **On-Page SEO for Core Pages:**
   - **Homepage:** Optimize for "Mark Egly Foundation" (branded) and "Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency support" (general)
   - **What is Alpha-1:** Optimize for "alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency," "what is alpha-1," "AATD"
   - **Diagnosis & Testing:** Optimize for "alpha-1 genetic testing," "alpha-1 diagnosis"
   - **Patient Assistance Program:** Optimize for "alpha-1 financial assistance," "alpha-1 patient assistance"
   - **For Each Page:**
     - Keyword in page title (H1)
     - Keyword in URL slug (e.g., /what-is-alpha-1)
     - Keyword in meta title (60 characters max)
     - Compelling meta description with keyword (155 characters max)
     - Keyword naturally in first 100 words of content
     - Keyword in at least 1 H2 subheading
     - Keyword density 0.5-2% (natural, not stuffed)
     - Internal links to related pages
     - Image alt text with keywords

3. **Google My Business:**
   - Create Google Business Profile (free)
   - Verify address (important for local SEO if Foundation has physical office)
   - Add business category: "Non-profit organization," "Health foundation"
   - Add photos, website link, contact info
   - Post updates weekly (events, news, patient stories)

4. **Google Search Console & Analytics:**
   - Set up Google Search Console (GSC) to monitor search performance
   - Set up Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to track traffic and conversions
   - Connect GSC and GA4 for unified reporting

**Deliverables:**

- Technical SEO audit report with fixes implemented
- 10-15 core pages fully optimized
- Google Business Profile live
- GSC and GA4 configured

## Part 2: Content Creation Framework

### Content Types & Specifications

**1. Blog Posts (2-4 per month)**

**Format:**

- **Length:** 800-1,500 words (sweet spot for engagement + SEO)
- **Structure:**
  - Compelling headline (8-12 words, includes keyword)
  - Hook paragraph (why reader should care)
  - 3-5 main sections with H2 subheadings
  - Bullet points, numbered lists for scannability
  - 1-2 images (stock photos or custom graphics)
  - Call-to-action at end (e.g., "Apply for Patient Assistance," "Join our newsletter")
  - Author bio (staff or guest contributor)
- **Tone:** Educational, compassionate, hopeful (not clinical or alarmist)
- **Examples:**
  - "5 Things I Wish I Knew When I Was Diagnosed with Alpha-1" (patient perspective)
  - "How Alpha-1 is Diagnosed: A Step-by-Step Guide" (educational)
  - "New Research: Promising Developments in Alpha-1 Treatment" (news)

**SEO Checklist:**

- [ ] Primary keyword in title, URL, meta description
- [ ] Keyword in first 100 words
- [ ] Keyword in at least one H2
- [ ] 3-5 internal links to related pages
- [ ] 1-2 external links to authoritative sources (medical journals, government health sites)
- [ ] Image alt text with keywords
- [ ] Meta description 150-155 characters
- [ ] Readability score: 60+ (Flesch-Kincaid, 8th grade or below)

---

**2. Patient Stories (1-2 per month)**

**Format:**

- **Length:** 1,000-1,500 words
- **Structure:**
  - Patient name and location (with permission)
  - Hero photo (professional headshot or lifestyle photo)
  - Story arc:
    1. Life before diagnosis (symptoms, struggles, misdiagnosis)
    2. Getting diagnosed (how they discovered Alpha-1, testing experience)
    3. Treatment journey (challenges, victories)
    4. Life today (hope, advocacy, advice to others)
  - Pull quotes (2-3 powerful statements highlighted)
  - CTA: "Share Your Story" (recruit more stories)
- **Video Option:** 3-5 min video interview (transcript on page)
- **Examples:**
  - "Sarah's Story: From 6 Years of Misdiagnosis to Alpha-1 Advocate"
  - "Why Firefighter Mike Got Tested for Alpha-1 – And What He Learned"
  - "Finding Hope: How Alpha-1 Patient Assistance Changed Jennifer's Life"

**Impact:**

- Humanizes Foundation
- SEO: Patient stories get high engagement (time on page), social shares
- Fundraising: Donors connect emotionally with real people
- Patient recruitment: Other patients see themselves in stories, apply for help

---

**3. Pillar Content (1-2 comprehensive guides)**

**Format:**

- **Length:** 3,000-5,000 words (comprehensive, authoritative)
- **Structure:**
  - Table of contents (linked to sections)
  - 10-20 sections with H2/H3 hierarchy
  - Multimedia (images, videos, infographics)
  - FAQ section
  - Resources and citations (link to medical journals, government sources)
  - Downloadable PDF version (gated for email capture)
- **Examples:**
  - "The Complete Guide to Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency"
  - "Alpha-1 Genetic Testing: The Definitive Guide"

**SEO Power:**

- Long-form content ranks higher (Google favors comprehensive resources)
- Attracts backlinks (other sites reference comprehensive guides)
- Establishes topical authority (Google recognizes Foundation as expert)

## Part 4: SEO Tools & Technology Stack

### Essential SEO Tools

**1. Google Search Console (Free)**

- **Purpose:** Monitor how Google sees website, identify search queries driving traffic, find crawl errors
- **Key Reports:**
  - Performance: Clicks, impressions, CTR, average position by keyword
  - Coverage: Indexing status, errors, warnings
  - Enhancements: Mobile usability, Core Web Vitals (page speed)
- **Usage:** Weekly check for errors, monthly performance review

**2. Google Analytics 4 (Free)**

- **Purpose:** Track website traffic, user behavior, conversions
- **Key Reports:**
  - Acquisition: Where traffic comes from (organic search, social media, referrals)
  - Engagement: Pages per session, time on site, bounce rate
  - Conversions: Donation completions, application submissions, newsletter signups
- **Usage:** Weekly check, monthly deep dive

**3. SEMrush or Ahrefs ($99-$399/month) - Choose One**

- **Purpose:** Keyword research, competitor analysis, backlink tracking, rank tracking
- **Key Features:**
  - Keyword Magic Tool: Find long-tail keywords
  - Site Audit: Identify technical SEO issues
  - Position Tracking: Monitor keyword rankings daily
  - Backlink Audit: Track who links to site
  - Competitor Analysis: See what keywords competitors rank for
- **Recommendation:** SEMrush $119/month plan (Pro level, suitable for small site)

**4. Yoast SEO (WordPress Plugin, Free-$99/year)**

- **Purpose:** On-page SEO optimization within WordPress
- **Key Features:**
  - SEO analysis (checks keyword usage, meta descriptions, readability)
  - XML sitemap generation
  - Breadcrumb navigation
  - Schema markup
- **Recommendation:** Free version sufficient for Year 1, upgrade to Premium ($99/year) in Year 2 if needed

**5. Screaming Frog SEO Spider (Free for 500 pages, £149/year unlimited)**

- **Purpose:** Technical SEO audits (crawl site like Google does)
- **Key Features:**
  - Find broken links (404 errors)
  - Analyze page titles, meta descriptions, headings
  - Identify duplicate content
  - Check redirects
- **Usage:** Quarterly comprehensive site audit

**6. Google PageSpeed Insights (Free)**

- **Purpose:** Analyze page load speed, get optimization recommendations
- **Key Metrics:**
  - Largest Contentful Paint (LCP): Should be <2.5s
  - First Input Delay (FID): Should be <100ms
  - Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS): Should be <0.1
- **Usage:** Test homepage and key pages monthly, fix issues impacting Core Web Vitals

**7. Hemingway Editor (Free) or Grammarly ($12-$15/month)**

- **Purpose:** Improve content readability, catch grammar errors
- **Key Features:**
  - Readability score (aim for 8th grade or lower for patient content)
  - Identifies complex sentences, passive voice, adverbs
  - Grammar and spelling checks (Grammarly)
- **Usage:** Run all blog posts and patient-facing content through before publishing

**8. Canva ($12.99/month, Pro plan)**

- **Purpose:** Create social media graphics, infographics, downloadable resource covers
- **Key Features:**
  - Templates for social posts, infographics, PDFs

### Optimization Cycle

**Monthly Optimization Process:**

1. **Review Analytics (Week 1 of month):**
   - Identify top-performing content (high traffic, low bounce rate, high conversions)
   - Identify underperforming content (low traffic, high bounce rate)
   - Review keyword ranking changes

2. **Optimize Underperformers (Week 2):**
   - **Low-Traffic Pages:**
     - Improve on-page SEO (better title, meta description, keyword optimization)
     - Add more internal links from high-traffic pages
     - Update content (add 300-500 words, freshen images, add video)
   - **High-Traffic, High-Bounce Pages:**
     - Improve content quality (more engaging intro, clearer structure)
     - Add compelling CTAs
     - Improve page speed
     - Add related content links

3. **Double Down on Winners (Week 3):**
   - **High-Traffic, High-Engagement Content:**
     - Update and expand (add new section, update statistics)
     - Create related content (if "What is Alpha-1" performs well, create "Alpha-1 FAQs," "Alpha-1 Treatment Guide")
     - Promote more heavily (paid ads, social media boost, email feature)
     - Build backlinks (outreach to sites to link to top content)

4. **Content Refresh (Week 4):**
   - Update old content (statistics, broken links, outdated information)
   - Add "Last Updated: [Date]" at top (signals freshness to Google)
   - Republish with new social media promotion

**Quarterly Deep Dive:**

- Comprehensive SEO audit (Screaming Frog crawl, fix all errors)
- Competitor analysis (what new content are Alpha-1 Foundation, COPD Foundation publishing?)
- Keyword gap analysis (what keywords do competitors rank for that we don't?)
- Backlink gap analysis (who links to competitors but not us?)
- Content gap analysis (what topics haven't we covered yet?)

## Part 7: Content Guidelines & Brand Voice

### Writing Style Guide

**Tone & Voice:**

- **Hopeful:** Emphasize progress, solutions, and possibility (not fear or doom)
- **Compassionate:** Acknowledge struggles, validate feelings
- **Empowering:** Provide actionable steps, encourage advocacy
- **Authoritative:** Cite credible sources, demonstrate expertise (but not arrogant)
- **Accessible:** Use plain language, define medical terms, avoid jargon

**Voice Examples:**

- ❌ Clinical/Distant: "Patients with AATD phenotype Pi\*ZZ exhibit decreased circulating levels of functional AAT."
- ✅ Accessible/Warm: "If you have two Z genes (one from each parent), your body doesn't make enough of the protective protein AAT. This is what doctors call the ZZ type of Alpha-1."

**Grammar & Style:**

- Use AP Style (Associated Press) for consistency
- Active voice preferred: "We help patients" not "Patients are helped by us"
- Contractions okay: "you're" not "you are" (conversational)
- Second person: "You can apply" not "One can apply" (direct, engaging)
- Short sentences: 15-20 words average
- Short paragraphs: 3-4 sentences maximum

**Medical Term Handling:**

- First use: Full term + acronym + plain explanation
  - "Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency (AATD), a genetic condition where your body doesn't make enough of a protective protein called AAT..."
- Subsequent uses: Acronym or short form
  - "If you have Alpha-1..." or "People with AATD..."
- Avoid unnecessary jargon: Use "genetic testing" not "genotyping"

**Inclusivity:**

- Diverse representation: Feature patients of different ages, races, genders, disease stages
- Avoid assumptions: "If you or your loved one has Alpha-1" (includes patients and caregivers)
- Person-first language: "Person with Alpha-1" not "Alpha-1 patient" (patient is more than their disease)
- Sensitivity to varying outcomes: Acknowledge that Alpha-1 affects people differently (some remain healthy, others develop severe disease)

### Content Approval Workflow

**Blog Posts & Patient Stories:**

1. Draft by writer
2. Review by Content Manager (accuracy, SEO, tone)
3. Medical review (if medical claims, reviewed by Medical Advisor or physician on Advisory Board)
4. Legal review (if needed for sensitive topics like insurance, employment)
5. Final approval by Executive Director or Communications Director
6. Publication

**Patient Stories Specific:**

- Patient review and written consent before publication (HIPAA compliance)
- Option for anonymity (first name only, no photos) if patient requests
- Patient has right to request story removal at any time

**Timeline:**

- Blog posts: 1 week from draft to publication
- Patient stories: 2-3 weeks (includes patient interview, draft, review, approval, photo/video production)
