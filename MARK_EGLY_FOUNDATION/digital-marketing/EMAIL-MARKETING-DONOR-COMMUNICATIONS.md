## Executive Summary

This document outlines a comprehensive email marketing and donor communications strategy to build relationships, steward donors, engage patients, and drive revenue. Email remains the highest ROI marketing channel (average $36-$42 return per $1 spent for nonprofits).

**Year 1 Objectives:**

- Build email list to 2,500-3,500 subscribers
- Achieve 25-35% open rate (above nonprofit average)
- Generate $25,000-$50,000 in email-driven donations
- Implement 5-7 automated nurture sequences
- Send 24-36 campaigns (2-3 per month)
- Maintain <2% unsubscribe rate

**Resource Requirements:**

- Email Marketing Coordinator (0.3-0.5 FTE): $12,000-$20,000
- Email Service Provider (ESP): $500-$2,000/year
- Design/content tools: $300-$800/year

## Part 1: List Building Strategy

### 1.1 List Growth Tactics

**Website Opt-In Forms (40% of new subscribers):**

**1. Homepage Pop-Up (Exit Intent):**

- Trigger: User moves mouse toward close/back button
- Offer: "Stay Connected - Get Alpha-1 Resources & Hope Delivered"
- Fields: Email, First Name, Zip Code
- Expected: 3-5% conversion = 30-50 signups/month from 1,000 visitors

**2. Sidebar/Footer Subscribe Form:**

- Persistent on all pages
- Simple: "Join Our Community"
- Expected: 1-2% conversion = 10-20 signups/month

**3. Blog Post Inline Forms:**

- Embedded in articles after 2-3 paragraphs
- Context-specific offers: "Get our Alpha-1 Testing Guide"
- Expected: 5-8% conversion = 20-30 signups/month from blog traffic

**4. Content Upgrades (Lead Magnets):**

- Downloadable guides, checklists, resource lists
- Examples:
  - "Ultimate Alpha-1 Diagnosis Checklist" (PDF)
  - "Financial Assistance Resource Guide" (PDF)
  - "Questions to Ask Your Pulmonologist" (checklist)
  - "Alpha-1 Family Testing Action Plan" (workbook)
- Expected: 15-25% conversion = 30-50 signups/month

**Social Media & Paid Ads (25% of new subscribers):**

- Facebook/Instagram ads driving to landing pages
- Social media link in bio (Linktree/Beacons)
- Pinned posts with email signup CTA
- Budget: $200-$400/month = 50-100 signups
- Cost per subscriber: $2-$4

**Events & In-Person (20% of new subscribers):**

- Paper signup sheets at events (Annual Gala, walks, health fairs)
- QR codes on printed materials
- Post-event follow-up email encouraging signup
- Expected: 100-200 signups from major events

**Partnerships & Cross-Promotion (10% of new subscribers):**

- Alpha-1 Foundation newsletter mention
- Partner organization email swaps
- Healthcare provider referrals (pulmonologist offices)
- Expected: 20-40 signups/month

**Patient & Donor Imports (5% of new subscribers):**

- Existing donor database import (with permission)
- Patient contact imports (HIPAA compliant)
- Board and staff personal networks
- One-time boost: 200-500 contacts

## Part 3: Email Types & Calendar

### 3.1 Email Types

**1. Newsletters (Monthly) - 12/year**

- **Purpose:** Consistent touchpoint, community building, education
- **Content:** Patient story (feature), Foundation news (2-3 items), upcoming events, research update, call-to-action
- **Length:** 400-600 words, 3-5 sections
- **Send:** First Wednesday of month, 10am ET
- **Goal:** 28-35% open rate, 3-5% click rate

**2. Fundraising Appeals (6-8/year)**

- **Purpose:** Direct donation requests
- **Content:** Compelling story, specific need/project, impact statement, clear CTA
- **Length:** 300-500 words, single focus
- **Send:** Strategic timing (year-end, Giving Tuesday, Alpha-1 Awareness Month)
- **Goal:** 1-3% conversion rate, $50-$150 average gift

**3. Patient Stories (6-8/year)**

- **Purpose:** Inspire, educate, humanize disease
- **Content:** Interview-style patient feature, photos, hope message
- **Length:** 500-800 words
- **Send:** Bi-weekly or monthly between newsletters
- **Goal:** 25-30% open rate, high forwarding/sharing

**4. Event Invitations & Updates (8-12/year)**

- **Purpose:** Drive registrations, ticket sales
- **Content:** Event details, speaker highlights, early bird offers, logistics
- **Length:** 200-400 words
- **Send:** 6 weeks, 4 weeks, 2 weeks, 1 week before event + post-event thank you
- **Goal:** 5-10% conversion to registration

**5. Educational Content (4-6/year)**

- **Purpose:** Provide value, establish authority
- **Content:** Alpha-1 topics (genetics, testing, treatment, research), expert Q&A
- **Length:** 600-1,000 words
- **Send:** Quarterly, tied to content strategy
- **Goal:** 30-35% open rate, high engagement time

**6. Advocacy & Action Alerts (2-4/year)**

- **Purpose:** Policy engagement, grassroots activation
- **Content:** Legislation details, action steps (call/email/sign), urgency
- **Length:** 200-400 words
- **Send:** Time-sensitive, as needed
- **Goal:** 10-20% take action rate

### 3.3 Send Time Optimization

**Best Days:**

- **Tuesdays-Thursdays:** Highest open rates for nonprofits
- **Avoid Mondays:** Inbox overload from weekend
- **Avoid Fridays:** People checking out for weekend

**Best Times:**

- **10am-11am ET:** After morning routine, before lunch
- **2pm-3pm ET:** Post-lunch, mid-afternoon
- **Test and optimize:** A/B test send times for your audience

**Frequency:**

- **Optimal:** 2-4 emails per month for general list
- **Maximum:** 1 email per week (avoid fatigue)
- **VIP/Major Donors:** Up to 6-8 emails per month (more engaged)

## Part 5: Email Design & Copywriting

### 5.1 Template Design

**Layout:**

- **Single Column:** Mobile-first, 600px wide max
- **Header:** Logo, navigation (optional), social icons
- **Hero Image:** Relevant photo, 600x300px, alt text required
- **Body:** Clear hierarchy, scannable, white space
- **CTA Button:** High contrast, 44x44px minimum (mobile touch target)
- **Footer:** Address, unsubscribe, social links, contact info

**Branding:**

- **Colors:** Foundation brand colors, consistent across emails
- **Fonts:** Web-safe (Arial, Georgia) or web fonts (Google Fonts)
- **Logo:** High-res, properly sized, linked to website
- **Images:** Optimized (<200KB each), descriptive alt text

**Accessibility:**

- **Text Size:** 14-16px minimum body text
- **Contrast:** WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 for text)
- **Alt Text:** All images described
- **Link Text:** Descriptive (not "click here")
- **Plain Text Version:** Always include for accessibility and spam filter

### 5.2 Subject Line Best Practices

**Length:**

- **Optimal:** 30-50 characters (mobile preview)
- **Maximum:** 60 characters before truncation

**Strategies:**

- **Personalization:** "Sarah, your Alpha-1 questions answered"
- **Urgency:** "Last chance - Gala tickets selling fast"
- **Curiosity:** "The #1 mistake after Alpha-1 diagnosis"
- **Benefit:** "Get your free Alpha-1 testing guide"
- **Emotion:** "This patient story will inspire you ðŸ’™"
- **Question:** "Have you been tested for Alpha-1?"

**Testing:**

- A/B test 2-3 variations per campaign
- Test one variable at a time (personalization, emoji, length, tone)
- Minimum 1,000 subscribers per test group for statistical significance

**Emojis:**

- Use sparingly (1-2 max, at start or end)
- Relevant to content (ðŸ’™ heart, ðŸŽ‰ celebration, ðŸ“… event)
- Test - some audiences love, others ignore

### 5.3 Copywriting Formula

**AIDA Framework:**

**Attention (Subject Line + Preheader):**

- Grab attention immediately
- Promise value or create curiosity
- Example: "Sarah beat Alpha-1 - here's how" + Preheader: "Her story will give you hope"

**Interest (Opening Paragraph):**

- Hook reader with compelling statement or question
- Relate to their pain point or aspiration
- Example: "Imagine waiting 7 years for answers about your breathing problems..."

**Desire (Body Content):**

- Build emotional connection
- Show transformation or solution
- Use stories, data, testimonials
- Example: "Meet John, who went from struggling to breathe to running his first 5K after diagnosis and treatment."

**Action (Call-to-Action):**

- Clear, specific CTA
- Single primary action (don't overload options)
- Action-oriented language
- Example: "Get Your Free Testing Guide" (not "Learn More")

**Copywriting Tips:**

- **Short Paragraphs:** 2-3 sentences max
- **Bullet Points:** Break up text, improve scannability
- **Active Voice:** "We're changing lives" not "Lives are being changed"
- **Second Person:** "You" language, direct address
- **Storytelling:** People remember stories, not statistics
- **Urgency:** Deadlines, limited spots, matching gifts
- **Proof:** Testimonials, data, results

### 5.4 Call-to-Action (CTA) Best Practices

**Button Design:**

- **Size:** Large enough for mobile tap (44x44px minimum)
- **Color:** High contrast with background, brand-aligned
- **Text:** Action verb + benefit ("Donate Now," "Get Your Guide," "Register Today")
- **Placement:** Above the fold (first screen) + repeat at end

**CTA Hierarchy:**

- **Primary CTA:** Main action, most prominent (1 per email)
- **Secondary CTA:** Optional action, less prominent (1-2 per email)
- **Tertiary CTA:** Footer links (social, website, contact)

**Testing:**

- Test button color (brand color vs. contrasting)
- Test button text (verb-focused vs. benefit-focused)
- Test placement (early vs. late in email)

## Part 6: Deliverability & Technical Setup

### 6.1 Email Authentication

**SPF (Sender Policy Framework):**

- Authorizes which mail servers can send on your behalf
- DNS TXT record: `v=spf1 include:_spf.mailerlite.com ~all`
- Prevents spoofing and improves deliverability

**DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail):**

- Digital signature proving email authenticity
- ESP provides DKIM keys to add to DNS
- Verifies email hasn't been tampered with

**DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication):**

- Policy telling receivers what to do with failed SPF/DKIM
- DNS TXT record: `v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:postmaster@markegly.org`
- Monitors and protects domain reputation

**Implementation:**

- Work with ESP and web host/DNS provider
- Critical for deliverability (especially Gmail, Yahoo)
- Test with mail-tester.com after setup

### 6.2 Sender Reputation

**Warm Up Schedule (New Domain/ESP):**

- Week 1: Send to 100 most engaged subscribers
- Week 2: Send to 300 engaged subscribers
- Week 3: Send to 1,000 engaged subscribers
- Week 4: Send to 3,000+ (slowly increase)
- Avoid: Sending to full list immediately (spam flags)

**Reputation Factors:**

- **Open Rates:** Higher = better reputation
- **Spam Complaints:** Keep <0.1%
- **Bounce Rates:** Keep <2% (hard bounces)
- **Engagement:** Clicks, replies, forwards (positive signals)
- **Unsubscribe Rate:** Keep <2%

**Monitoring:**

- Google Postmaster Tools (Gmail delivery insights)
- Microsoft SNDS (Outlook/Hotmail delivery)
- ESP deliverability reports
- Regular inbox placement tests

### 6.3 List Hygiene

**Monthly:**

- Remove hard bounces immediately
- Flag soft bounces (retry, remove after 3 fails)
- Monitor spam complaints

**Quarterly:**

- Re-engagement campaign for inactive subscribers (90+ days no opens)
- Remove permanently inactive (180+ days, no re-engagement response)
- Verify email syntax and format

**Annually:**

- Full list scrubbing with validation service
- Remove role-based emails (info@, admin@) if low engagement
- Segment and archive long-term inactive

**Why It Matters:**

- Improves deliverability (fewer bad addresses)
- Reduces ESP costs (pay per subscriber)
- Better engagement metrics
- Protects sender reputation

### 7.2 Testing Strategy

**A/B Test Variables (Priority Order):**

1. **Subject Lines (Monthly):**
   - Test: Personalization vs. generic, emoji vs. no emoji, question vs. statement
   - Split: 50/50, minimum 500 per group
   - Decide: After 4 hours, send winner to remainder

2. **Send Times (Quarterly):**
   - Test: 10am vs. 2pm vs. 7pm
   - Split: 33/33/33, minimum 300 per group
   - Decide: After 24 hours, analyze best time

3. **CTA Button (Quarterly):**
   - Test: Button color, button text, button placement
   - Split: 50/50, minimum 500 per group
   - Decide: Based on click-through rate

4. **Email Length (Semi-Annually):**
   - Test: Short (200 words) vs. Long (600 words)
   - Split: 50/50, full campaign
   - Decide: Based on engagement and conversion

5. **Personalization (Semi-Annually):**
   - Test: First name in body vs. no personalization
   - Test: Segment-specific content vs. generic
