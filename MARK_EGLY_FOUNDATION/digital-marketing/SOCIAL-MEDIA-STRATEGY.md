# Document #12: Social Media Strategy

## Mark Egly Foundation - Phase 2 Expansion

**Document Type:** Digital Marketing
**Priority:** MEDIUM
**Estimated Budget:** $10,000 - $25,000 Year 1
**Timeline:** 6 months to establish presence, 12+ months to mature
**Dependencies:** Website (Doc #10), Content Strategy (Doc #11)
**Last Updated:** November 2025

---

## Executive Summary

This document provides a comprehensive social media strategy for the Mark Egly Foundation to build community, raise awareness, engage stakeholders, and drive website traffic. The strategy focuses on platforms where Alpha-1 patients, caregivers, and healthcare providers are most active.

**Year 1 Objectives:**

- Build 2,000-3,000 combined followers across platforms
- Generate 50,000-75,000 impressions per month
- Drive 500-1,000 monthly website visits from social
- Engage 20-30 patient stories and community testimonials
- Establish 2-3 strategic influencer partnerships
- Create sustainable content production workflow

**Resource Requirements:**

- Part-time Social Media Manager (0.3-0.5 FTE): $15,000-$25,000
- Content creation tools and ads: $3,000-$8,000
- Influencer partnerships: $2,000-$5,000
- Training and consulting: $1,000-$3,000

   - Provide exclusive content to group members

2. **Facebook Live:**
   - Monthly educational sessions (15-30 minutes)
   - Event livestreams (Annual Gala, walks)
   - Patient interview series
   - Expert Q&As with pulmonologists

3. **Fundraising Tools:**
   - Facebook Fundraisers for birthdays/memorials
   - Donate button on page

- Month 1-2: Page setup, branding, initial content
- Month 3-4: Launch community group, first Facebook Live

### 2.4 Visual Branding Guidelines

**Color Palette:**

- Primary: Foundation brand colors
- Secondary: Light blue (lung/breath), green (hope/health), gray (professional)
- Avoid: Dark/depressing colors, overly medical imagery

**Typography:**

- Headers: Clean, modern sans-serif (Montserrat, Open Sans)
- Body: Readable, accessible font
- Consistent sizing and hierarchy

**Image Guidelines:**

- High-quality photos (minimum 1080x1080 for Instagram)
- Patient-centered imagery (people, not just medical)
- Diverse representation (age, gender, ethnicity)
- Natural lighting, candid moments preferred
- Always obtain written permission for patient photos

**Templates:**

- Quote graphics (Instagram/Facebook)
- Educational infographics (multi-slide carousels)
- Patient story frames
- Event announcements
- Statistic callouts

## Part 3: Community Management & Engagement

### 3.1 Response Protocols

**Comment Response Times:**

- Questions about Alpha-1: Within 4 hours (business hours)
- Emotional/support needs: Within 2 hours, empathetic response
- General comments: Within 24 hours
- Donor thank-yous: Within 1 hour (automated + personal follow-up)

**Message Response Times:**

- Urgent medical questions: Immediate (direct to resources)
- General inquiries: Within 4 hours
- Partnership/media requests: Within 24 hours, escalate to leadership

**Response Templates:**

**Medical Questions:**

```
Thank you for reaching out. While we can't provide medical advice, we recommend speaking with your pulmonologist or contacting the Alpha-1 Foundation's Medical Helpline at [phone]. We're here to support you! [Link to resources]
```

**New Diagnosis Support:**

```
We understand this can be overwhelming. You're not alone‚Äîour community is here for you. Check out our Resources page [link] and consider joining our private Facebook support group [link]. Please reach out if we can help connect you with anything specific.
```

**Donor Thank You:**

```
Thank you so much for your generous support! Your gift helps us [specific impact]. We're grateful to have you as part of the Mark Egly Foundation family. üíô
```

### 3.2 User-Generated Content (UGC)

**Encouraging UGC:**

- Monthly photo challenges (#MyAlphaWarriorMoment)
- Share your story submission form
- Tag us campaigns (@MarkEglyFoundation)
- Event photo contests
- Testimonial requests

**UGC Guidelines:**

- Always ask permission before reposting
- Credit original poster
- Use dedicated hashtag (#MarkEglyStories)
- Provide incentives (featured on website, small gift)
- Store organized library of approved UGC

**Legal Considerations:**

- Photo release forms required
- HIPAA compliance (no PHI without explicit consent)
- Minors require parent/guardian consent
- Clear terms for UGC use

### 3.3 Crisis Management

**Potential Crisis Scenarios:**

- Negative patient experience
- Medical misinformation spread
- Controversial health policy news
- Staff or board member misconduct allegations
- Fundraising or financial concerns

**Crisis Response Protocol:**

1. **Pause:** Stop all scheduled posts
2. **Assess:** Determine severity and required response level
3. **Escalate:** Notify Executive Director and Board Chair
4. **Respond:** Issue statement if needed (within 4 hours)
5. **Monitor:** Track sentiment and conversation
6. **Resume:** Return to normal posting only when appropriate

**Crisis Communication Team:**

- Social Media Manager (first responder)
- Executive Director (decision maker)
- Board Chair (spokesperson if needed)
- Legal counsel (for serious issues)

## Part 4: Paid Social Advertising

### 4.1 Advertising Budget Allocation

**Year 1 Paid Social Budget: $5,000 - $10,000**

| Platform      | Budget % | Annual $      | Purpose                                   |
| ------------- | -------- | ------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Facebook Ads  | 60%      | $3,000-$6,000 | Awareness, page growth, event promotion   |
| Instagram Ads | 30%      | $1,500-$3,000 | Awareness, story engagement, younger demo |
| LinkedIn Ads  | 10%      | $500-$1,000   | Professional outreach, donor cultivation  |

**Monthly Spend:** $400-$800 (adjust for campaigns)

### 4.2 Campaign Types

**1. Awareness Campaigns (40% of budget)**

- **Objective:** Reach new audiences, educate about Alpha-1
- **Targeting:** Adults 35-65, COPD/lung disease interests, genetics
- **Creative:** Educational videos, patient stories, infographics
- **KPIs:** Impressions, reach, video views, cost per 1,000 impressions (CPM)

**2. Page Growth Campaigns (20% of budget)**

- **Objective:** Grow Facebook and Instagram followers
- **Targeting:** Engaged audiences, similar to current followers
- **Creative:** "Follow us" call-to-action, compelling content previews
- **KPIs:** Page likes, follower growth, cost per follower

**3. Traffic Campaigns (20% of budget)**

- **Objective:** Drive website visits, resource downloads
- **Targeting:** Interested audiences, retargeting website visitors
- **Creative:** Blog post highlights, resource guides, testing info
- **KPIs:** Link clicks, landing page views, cost per click (CPC)

**4. Event Promotion (10% of budget)**

- **Objective:** Sell tickets, increase registrations
- **Targeting:** Local + supporter audiences for events
- **Creative:** Event details, speaker highlights, early bird offers
- **KPIs:** Event responses, ticket sales, cost per registration

**5. Fundraising Campaigns (10% of budget)**

- **Objective:** Drive donations during key campaigns
- **Targeting:** Donor lookalikes, engaged followers
- **Creative:** Impact stories, matching gift announcements
- **KPIs:** Donations, cost per acquisition (CPA), ROAS (return on ad spend)

### 4.3 Targeting Strategy

**Core Audiences:**

1. **Alpha-1 Patients & Families:**
   - Interests: Alpha-1, COPD, emphysema, genetic testing, rare diseases
   - Age: 30-70
   - Lookalikes based on website visitors and email list

2. **Healthcare Providers:**
   - Job titles: Pulmonologist, respiratory therapist, primary care physician
   - Interests: Medical research, pulmonology
   - LinkedIn specifically

3. **Potential Donors:**
   - Charitable giving behaviors
   - Interests: Health charities, medical research
   - Income level: Upper-middle class+
   - Lookalikes based on donor list

4. **General Awareness:**
   - Broad health-conscious audience
   - Parents (genetic testing angle)
   - Smokers/former smokers (COPD risk)

**Retargeting:**

- Website visitors (past 30/60/90 days)
- Video viewers (50%+ completion)
- Engaged social media users
- Email list (custom audience upload)

### 4.4 Ad Creative Best Practices

**Facebook/Instagram:**

- Square or vertical video (1:1 or 4:5 ratio)
- Captions/subtitles (85% watch without sound)
- Hook in first 3 seconds
- Clear call-to-action
- Mobile-optimized landing pages

**LinkedIn:**

- Professional imagery and tone
- Data and statistics (credibility)
- Thought leadership approach
- Document ads (lead magnets)
- Longer copy acceptable

**Testing:**

- A/B test audiences, creative, copy
- Test 2-3 variations per campaign
- Run for minimum 7 days before optimizing
- Kill underperformers, scale winners

## Part 5: Influencer & Partnership Strategy

### 5.1 Influencer Identification

**Tier 1: Micro-Influencers (1K-10K followers)**

- **Profile:** Alpha-1 patients, rare disease advocates, health bloggers
- **Value:** Authentic, engaged communities, affordable
- **Compensation:** $100-$500 per post or product/experience
- **Target:** 3-5 partnerships Year 1

**Tier 2: Mid-Tier Influencers (10K-100K followers)**

- **Profile:** Health/wellness influencers, patient advocates, medical professionals
- **Value:** Broader reach, credibility
- **Compensation:** $500-$2,000 per post or campaign
- **Target:** 1-2 partnerships Year 1

**Tier 3: Macro-Influencers (100K+ followers)**

- **Profile:** Celebrities with Alpha-1 connection, major health advocates
- **Value:** Massive awareness, media coverage
- **Compensation:** Pro-bono or major fee ($5K+)
- **Target:** 0-1 partnership (aspirational)

### 5.2 Partnership Opportunities

**1. Patient Influencer Program:**

- Recruit 5-10 Alpha-1 patients with social media presence
- Provide content, talking points, assets
- Amplify their stories on Foundation channels
- Offer stipends or recognition ($50-$200 per story)

**2. Healthcare Provider Partnerships:**

- Collaborate with pulmonologists and medical influencers
- Guest takeovers of Foundation accounts
- Joint educational content creation
- Professional credibility boost

**3. Rare Disease Coalitions:**

- Cross-promotion with similar organizations
- Joint awareness campaigns
- Shared audiences and resources
- Coordinate on Rare Disease Day

**4. Corporate Partners:**

- Employee social media activation
- Matching gift social campaigns
- Co-branded content
- Workplace giving promotions

### 5.3 Campaign Collaboration Framework

**Campaign Types:**

- **Awareness Campaigns:** Share educational content, personal stories
- **Fundraising Campaigns:** Promote specific appeals or events
- **Advocacy Campaigns:** Policy changes, research funding
- **Event Promotion:** Galas, walks, webinars

**Collaboration Process:**

1. **Outreach:** Personalized pitch to potential partners
2. **Agreement:** Scope, timeline, compensation, deliverables
3. **Brief:** Talking points, assets, key messages
4. **Creation:** Influencer creates content (review before posting)
5. **Amplification:** Foundation shares and promotes
6. **Reporting:** Track performance, impact, engagement

**Legal Requirements:**

- Written agreements for paid partnerships
- FTC disclosure requirements (#ad, #sponsored)
- Content approval rights
- Usage rights for repurposing

### 8.1 Roles & Responsibilities

**Social Media Manager (0.3-0.5 FTE, $15K-$25K):**

- Content planning and calendar management (5 hrs/week)
- Content creation and scheduling (8 hrs/week)
- Community management and engagement (5 hrs/week)
- Analytics and reporting (2 hrs/week)
- Total: 15-20 hours/week

**Executive Director:**

- Strategic oversight and approval
- Crisis communication spokesperson
- High-level relationship building
- Quarterly strategy review

**Marketing/Communications Director (if hired):**

- Overall digital marketing strategy
- Brand consistency oversight
- Campaign development
- Vendor and contractor management

**Volunteers/Interns:**

- Content creation support
- Community engagement assistance
- Event coverage (photography/video)
- User-generated content curation

### 8.2 Content Approval Process

**Standard Posts (Educational, News, Engagement):**

- Created by Social Media Manager
- Self-approved and scheduled
- Weekly preview shared with Executive Director

**Sensitive Content (Patient Stories, Fundraising Appeals, Partnerships):**

- Draft created by Social Media Manager
- Review and approval by Executive Director
- 48-hour advance review required

**Crisis or Controversial Content:**

- Draft by Social Media Manager
- Review by Executive Director and Board Chair
- Legal counsel if needed
- Immediate approval before posting

**Patient Stories:**

- Written consent and photo release
- Draft shared with patient for approval
- Final approval by Social Media Manager and Executive Director

### 8.3 Social Media Policy

**Foundation Staff and Board:**

- Encouraged to share Foundation content on personal profiles
- Must disclose affiliation when posting about Foundation
- May not speak on behalf of Foundation without approval
- Maintain professional and respectful tone
- No political endorsements on Foundation channels

**Patient Ambassadors and Volunteers:**

- Provided with branded assets and talking points
- May share personal experiences freely
- Should tag Foundation and use hashtags
- Must respect privacy of other patients
- Photo and video permissions required

**Prohibited Content:**

- Medical advice or diagnosis
- Endorsement of specific treatments (except evidence-based)
- Political candidate endorsements
- Discrimination or hate speech
- Patient information without explicit consent
- False or misleading claims
