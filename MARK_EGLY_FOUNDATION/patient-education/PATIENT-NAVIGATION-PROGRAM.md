## Program Overview

### What is Patient Navigation?

**Patient Navigation** is personalized, one-on-one support helping AATD patients:

- âœ… **Understand diagnosis** (genetics, prognosis, treatment options)
- âœ… **Access testing** (AAT level testing, genetic testing, pulmonary function tests)
- âœ… **Find specialists** (pulmonologists, hepatologists, AATD experts)
- âœ… **Navigate insurance** (coverage, prior authorizations, appeals)
- âœ… **Coordinate care** (appointments, medical records, second opinions)
- âœ… **Connect to resources** (financial assistance, support groups, clinical trials)
- âœ… **Advocate for themselves** (prepare for appointments, ask right questions, self-advocacy skills)

**Navigator = "Sherpa"**: Guides patient through complex healthcare terrain (doesn't replace doctor, but helps patient get most from medical care)

## Why Navigation Matters

### The Healthcare Maze Problem

**AATD patients face extraordinary complexity**:

- **Rare disease** (most doctors have never seen AATDâ€”misdiagnosis common)
- **Multi-system disease** (lungs + liver + skinâ€”need multiple specialists)
- **Complex genetics** (ZZ, MZ, MS, rare variantsâ€”confusing even for doctors)
- **Expensive treatment** (augmentation therapy $100K-$200K/yearâ€”insurance battles)
- **Fragmented care** (PCP, pulmonologist, hepatologist, geneticistâ€”no one coordinates)
- **Information overload** (Google search returns conflicting infoâ€”what to trust?)

**Without navigation, patients get lost**:

- Delayed treatment (6-12 months to get augmentation therapy approvedâ€”patients give up)
- Mismanaged care (lung doctor doesn't know about liver disease, vice versa)
- Financial catastrophe (don't know about assistance programsâ€”go into debt)
- Treatment non-adherence (overwhelmed, confused, frustratedâ€”stop infusions)

**With navigation, patients succeed**:

- Faster treatment access (Navigator handles prior auth paperworkâ€”approved in 4-6 weeks vs. 6+ months)
- Coordinated care (Navigator ensures all doctors communicate)
- Financial stability (connected to assistance programsâ€”$0 out-of-pocket for many patients)
- Better outcomes (less stress, better adherence, improved quality of life)

### Key Responsibilities

- Schedule appointments (help patient get earliest available, coordinate multiple specialists)
- Facilitate communication (ensure doctors share records, avoid duplicate tests)
- Arrange second opinions (when patient wants confirmation, help access top centers)

**3. Insurance Navigation** (25% of time):

- Verify coverage (check what patient's insurance coversâ€”testing, treatment, specialists)
- Prior authorization support (gather documentation, submit paperwork, follow up)
- Appeals (write appeal letters when denied, cite guidelines, push back)
- Financial assistance (connect to patient assistance programs, copay cards, grants)

- Testing assistance (free/discounted AAT testing, genetic testing)
- Support groups (match patient to appropriate groupâ€”virtual or local)
- Clinical trials (identify relevant trials, explain enrollment process)
- Mental health (refer to therapists, support groups, crisis resources)
- Advocacy (connect to legislative campaigns, awareness events)

**5. Administrative** (5% of time):

- Document interactions (CRM notesâ€”what discussed, actions taken, follow-up needed)
- Report outcomes (quarterly metricsâ€”patients served, satisfaction, treatment access)
- Attend team meetings (case reviews, training, quality improvement)

---

### Caseload & Time Allocation

**Intensive Cases** (20% of patients, 70% of time):

- **Definition**: Newly diagnosed, complex cases (pediatric, transplant, rare variants), insurance denials
- **Support Level**: Weekly contact (calls, emails), hands-on assistance (submit paperwork, attend appointments)
- **Duration**: 3-6 months (until patient stable, treatment started, resources connected)
- **Caseload**: 25 intensive cases per Navigator (at any given time)

**Brief Consultations** (80% of patients, 30% of time):

- **Definition**: Established patients, single-issue help (find specialist, one-time insurance question)
- **Support Level**: 1-3 touchpoints (answer question, provide resource, refer to service)
- **Duration**: 1-4 weeks
- **Caseload**: 100-150 brief cases per Navigator per year

**Year 1**: 2 Navigators â†’ 50 intensive + 200-300 brief = 500 patients total

## Services Provided

### 1. Diagnosis Support (First 90 Days)

**When Patient Contacts Foundation**:

- **Phone Call** (within 24 hoursâ€”Navigator calls newly diagnosed patient)
- **First Conversation** (60-90 min):
  - Understand patient's story (how diagnosed, symptoms, fears, questions)
  - Explain AATD basics (genetics, prognosis, treatment, NOT a death sentence)
  - Assess needs (insurance, specialist, family testing, financial concerns)

**Navigator Actions**:

- âœ… Send Welcome Packet (What is AATD guide, testing info, Foundation services)
- âœ… Schedule genetic counseling (explain inheritance, recommend family testing)

### 2. Treatment Access Support

**Augmentation Therapy Navigation**:

- **Insurance Verification**: Navigator calls insurance (verify coverage, prior auth requirements)
- **Prior Authorization**: Gather documentation (PFTs, AAT levels, doctor letter), submit paperwork
- **Follow-Up**: Track status (call insurance weekly, push for faster decision)
- **Denial Management**: If denied, write appeal (cite ATS/ERS guidelines, medical necessity), resubmit
- **Approval**: Once approved, connect patient to infusion center, home health, or self-infusion training

**Timeline Goals**:

- Without Navigator: 3-6 months (average time to start therapyâ€”many give up)
- With Navigator: 4-8 weeks (Navigator expedites process)

**Clinical Trial Navigation**:

- Identify relevant trials (match patient to appropriate studiesâ€”gene therapy, new AAT formulations)
- Explain trial (what's involved, risks/benefits, how to enroll)
- Facilitate enrollment (connect patient to research coordinator, help with travel logistics)

---

### 3. Specialist Referral & Coordination

**Finding the Right Doctor**:

- **Navigator uses database**: 200+ AATD specialists nationwide (Cleveland Clinic, National Jewish, Mayo, regional experts)
- **Filter by**: Insurance network, location, subspecialty (pediatric, liver, transplant)
- **Provide options**: Give patient 2-3 specialist names (bio, credentials, patient reviews)

**Appointment Coordination**:

- Call to schedule (Navigator can make appointment on patient's behalfâ€”if requested)
- Prepare patient (send appointment prep checklist, questions to ask)
- Send records (ensure specialist has PFTs, AAT levels, genetic testing before visit)
- Follow-up (call after appointmentâ€”what did doctor say? understand recommendations?)

**Care Team Communication**:

- Facilitate sharing (connect pulmonologist + hepatologist + PCPâ€”shared care plan)
- Avoid duplication (ensure one doctor orders PFTs, not all threeâ€”save patient time, money)

## Case Management Process

### Intake & Assessment

**How Patients Connect to Navigator**:

1. **Self-Referral**: Call Foundation (1-800-AATD-HELP), fill out form (Alpha1Life.com)
2. **Provider Referral**: Doctor refers patient (common after diagnosis)
3. **Support Group**: Member mentions need, leader connects to Navigator
4. **Outreach**: Foundation identifies newly diagnosed (through testing program), Navigator proactively reaches out

**Initial Assessment** (30-60 min phone call):

- **Demographics**: Name, age, location, insurance, employment
- **Medical History**: Diagnosis date, genotype (ZZ, MZ, etc.), symptoms, FEV1, AAT level, current treatment
- **Current Needs**: What brings you to Navigation? (new diagnosis? insurance issue? need specialist?)
- **Priorities**: What's most urgent? (start therapy? find doctor? financial help?)
- **Resources**: What have you tried? (support group? Alpha1Life.com? patient assistance programs?)
- **Barriers**: What's blocking you? (insurance denial? can't afford copays? overwhelmed?)

**Document in CRM**: All info entered (Salesforce or similarâ€”track interactions, follow-ups, outcomes)

---

### Action Plan Development

**Navigator Creates Customized Plan**:

- **Goal**: What does patient want to achieve? (start therapy, find specialist, get financial help)
- **Steps**: Break into action items (1. Verify insurance, 2. Submit prior auth, 3. Appeal if denied, 4. Connect to copay assistance)
- **Timeline**: When will each step happen? (Week 1: verify insurance, Week 2: submit paperwork, Week 4: follow up)
- **Responsibilities**: Who does what? (Navigator submits paperwork, patient signs forms, doctor writes letter)

**Share with Patient**: Email action plan (written roadmapâ€”patient knows what to expect)

---

### Execution & Follow-Up

**Navigator Takes Action**:

- Makes calls (insurance, specialists, infusion centers)
- Gathers documents (medical records, PFTs, letters of medical necessity)
- Submits paperwork (prior auths, appeals, assistance applications)
- Coordinates appointments (schedules for patient, sends reminders)

**Regular Check-Ins**:

- Call patient weekly (intensive cases) or bi-weekly (brief cases)
- Update on progress ("I submitted your prior auth, should hear back in 10 days")
- Problem-solve barriers ("Insurance denied? OK, here's our appeal strategy")

---

### Case Closure

**When is Case Closed?**:

- âœ… Goal achieved (patient on therapy, found specialist, financial assistance secured)
- âœ… Patient stable (no longer needs intensive support)
- âœ… Resources connected (knows about support group, Alpha1Life.com, how to reach Navigator again if needed)

**Closure Process**:

- **Final Call**: "We've achieved your goals. You're in good shape. Here's how to reach me if you need anything."
- **Satisfaction Survey**: "How did we do? What could we improve?"
- **Transition to Maintenance**: "I'll check in quarterly. Call anytime."

**Average Case Duration**: 3-6 months (intensive) or 1-4 weeks (brief)
