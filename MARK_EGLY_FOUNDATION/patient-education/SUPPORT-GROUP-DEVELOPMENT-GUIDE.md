## Support Group Overview

### What is an AATD Support Group?

**AATD Support Groups** are peer-led communities where patients, families, and caregivers:

- âœ… **Share experiences** (diagnosis stories, treatment challenges, coping strategies)
- âœ… **Provide emotional support** (reduce isolation, normalize feelings, offer hope)
- âœ… **Exchange practical advice** (insurance tips, travel with oxygen, workplace accommodations)
- âœ… **Build friendships** (lasting connections with people who "get it")

**NOT therapy or medical treatment**:

- Groups are peer support (not professional counselingâ€”though some have guest therapists)
- No medical advice given (share experiences, always defer to doctors)

## Types of Support Groups

### 1. General AATD Support Groups

**Who**: All AATD patients (any genotype, any stage)

**Focus**: Broad support, diagnosis stories, treatment, lifestyle, advocacy

**Best For**: Newly diagnosed, patients without access to specialized groups

**Meeting Frequency**: Monthly (90-minute meetings)

**Size**: 10-25 members (ideal: 15)

---

### 2. Specialized Support Groups

**Women with AATD**:

- Focus: Pregnancy, hormones, menopause, work-life balance, body image
- Why: Women face unique challenges (fertility concerns, childcare + chronic illness, gender bias in healthcare)

**Young Adults with AATD** (ages 18-35):

- Focus: Dating, career planning, starting families, financial planning, insurance
- Why: Different life stage challenges (building career vs. managing illness, student loans + medical debt)

**Parents of Pediatric AATD**:

- Focus: Liver disease management, genetic guilt, school advocacy, siblings, transition to adult care
- Why: Parenting a sick child is isolating, parents need peer support

**Caregivers & Family Members**:

- Focus: Caregiver burnout, communication, setting boundaries, self-care
- Why: Caregivers often neglect own needs, need space to share frustrations

**Pre-Transplant & Transplant Recipients**:

- Focus: Transplant anxiety, recovery, rejection, lifelong immunosuppression, survivor guilt
- Why: Transplant is traumatic, peer support from those who've been there is invaluable

**Carriers (MZ, MS, SZ)**:

- Focus: Health monitoring, risk management, family testing, genetic counseling
- Why: Carriers often dismissed by doctors ("You're just a carrier"), but new research shows health risks

### 4. Virtual Support Groups

**Advantages**:

- **Accessibility**: Rural patients, homebound, oxygen-dependent (can't travel)

## Meeting Structure & Facilitation

### Standard Meeting Format (90 minutes)

**Before Meeting** (15 min early):

- Set up space (chairs in circle, tissues available, snacks/drinksâ€”in-person)
- Test tech (Zoom link works, audio/video clearâ€”virtual)
- Greet early arrivals (casual chat, make them comfortable)

**Opening** (5 min):

- Welcome (introduce yourself, thank attendees)
- Confidentiality reminder ("What's shared here stays here")
- Agenda preview ("Today we're discussing X, then open sharing")

**Check-In Round** (20 min):

- Go around circle (each person shares brieflyâ€”1-2 min)
- Prompts: "How are you doing this week?" or "One high and one low since last meeting"
- Leader goes first (models vulnerability, sets tone)

**Topic Discussion** (40 min):

- Introduce topic (e.g., "Managing Fatigue with AATD")
- Leader shares briefly (personal experience or researchâ€”2-3 min)
- Open to group (invite others to share tips, challenges, questions)
- Facilitate (ensure everyone gets to speak, gently redirect tangents)

**Resource Sharing** (10 min):

- Relevant Foundation resources (handouts, Alpha1Life.com articles, upcoming events)
- Member recommendations (books, products, doctorsâ€”peer-sourced)

**Check-Out & Closing** (10 min):

- Go around (each person shares one takeaway from today's meeting)
- Announce next meeting (date, time, topic)
- Thank attendees, remind of confidentiality

**After Meeting** (5 min):

- Linger for informal chat (members often stay to connect 1-on-1)
- Note any follow-ups (member in crisis, resource requests)

## Virtual vs. In-Person Groups

### Virtual Groups (Zoom)

**Advantages**:

- Accessible (rural patients, homebound, oxygen users who can't travel)
- Convenient (no commute, pajamas OK)
- Recorded (absent members can watch laterâ€”with permission)
- Specialized (rare variant patients can find peers nationwide)

**Challenges**:

- Less personal (harder to read body language, build deep connections)
- Tech barriers (older members struggle with Zoom)
- "Zoom fatigue" (screen time exhausting)

**Best Practices**:

- **Cameras on encouraged** (not requiredâ€”but builds connection)
- **Mute when not speaking** (reduce background noise)
- **Chat feature** (share links, resources, side comments)
- **Breakout rooms** (Year 2+: split large groups for small-group discussions)
- **Recording policy** (ask permission, post to Alpha1Life.com for absent members)

---

### In-Person Groups

**Advantages**:

- Deeper connections (hugs, eye contact, body language)
- Social time (coffee before/after, friendships extend beyond meetings)
- Local advocacy (organize awareness walks, meet legislators together)

**Challenges**:

- Accessibility (oxygen users, wheelchair users, rural patients can't attend)
- Weather/travel (winter storms, traffic, parking)
- Venue costs (need free spaceâ€”library, church, hospital)

**Best Practices**:

- **Accessible venue** (wheelchair ramps, elevators, handicap parking)
- **Consistent location** (same place each monthâ€”easy to remember)
- **Snacks** (coffee, tea, light snacksâ€”welcoming atmosphere)
- **Name tags** (first meetings, new membersâ€”help with names)
- **Virtual option** (Year 2+: hybrid meetingsâ€”Zoom + in-person)

## Safety & Crisis Management

### Confidentiality Rules

**What is Confidential**:

- âœ… Personal stories shared in group (diagnosis, treatment, family issues)
- âœ… Names and identifying details (don't post photos without permission)
- âœ… Sensitive topics (mental health struggles, financial hardship, relationship problems)

**What Can Be Shared**:

- âœ… General topics discussed (e.g., "We talked about managing fatigue")
- âœ… Resources mentioned (links to articles, product recommendations)
- âœ… Your own story (you control what you share about yourself)

**Violations**: If member violates confidentiality â†’ warning (first time), removed from group (repeat offense)

---

### Crisis Protocol

**If Member Shares Suicidal Thoughts or Severe Distress**:

**During Meeting**:

1. **Acknowledge** ("Thank you for sharing. That sounds really hard.")
2. **Connect to resources** ("I want to make sure you're safe. Can we talk after the meeting?")
3. **Don't panic** (stay calm, model that it's OK to ask for help)

**After Meeting** (within 1 hour):

1. **Talk privately** ("Are you safe right now? Do you have a plan to hurt yourself?")
2. **Provide resources**:
   - 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call or text)
   - Crisis Text Line (text HOME to 741741)
   - Local ER (if immediate danger)
3. **Contact Foundation** (Patient Navigatorâ€”within 24 hours, they'll follow up)
4. **Follow up** (check in 48 hours later, 1 week later)

**Leader Boundaries**:

- You are **not a therapist** (refer to professionalsâ€”don't try to "fix" them)
- You are **not responsible** for their safety (provide resources, encourage professional help, but their choices are their own)
- **Report to Foundation** (Navigator can provide more intensive support)

---

### Handling Difficult Situations

**Member Dominates Conversation**:

- Gently interrupt: "Thanks for sharing, Maria. Let's hear from others who haven't spoken yet."
- Use timer: "We have 5 minutes leftâ€”anyone else want to share?"

**Conflict Between Members**:

- Remind ground rules: "Let's remember to respect different perspectives."
- Redirect: "It sounds like you disagree. That's OKâ€”we don't all have to agree. Let's hear other viewpoints."
- Offline resolution: "This seems heated. Let's talk offline and move on for now."

**Medical Misinformation Shared**:

- Correct gently: "I've heard that too, but I think the research says something different. Let's check Alpha1Life.com resources."
- Defer to experts: "That's a medical questionâ€”best to ask your pulmonologist."
- Don't shame: "It's easy to get confused with all the info out there."

**Member Promotes Product/MLM**:

- Stop immediately: "We don't allow product promotion in this group. Please save sales pitches for outside."
- Enforce consequences: Warning (first time), removal (repeat offense)
