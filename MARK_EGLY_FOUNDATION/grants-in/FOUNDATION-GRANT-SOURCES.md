## National Health Foundations

### Robert Wood Johnson Foundation

**Focus**: Health equity, chronic disease, public health
**Typical Grant Size**: $100,000-$500,000
**Application Cycle**: Ongoing (rolling) + annual RFPs
**Eligibility**: 501(c)(3) nonprofits

- Health Equity (disparities in diagnosis/treatment)
- Chronic Disease Prevention
- Evidence for Action (innovative interventions)

## Federal Government Grants

### National Institutes of Health (NIH)

**Agency**: US Department of Health and Human Services
**Typical Grant Size**: $100,000-$2,000,000+
**Application Cycle**: 3 cycles per year (Feb, Jun, Oct deadlines)
**Eligibility**: 501(c)(3), universities, hospitals
**Website**: grants.nih.gov

**Relevant Institutes**:

- **NHLBI (National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute)**: Primary for AATD research
- **NIDDK (National Institute of Diabetes and Digestive and Kidney Diseases)**: Liver disease
- **NIGMS (National Institute of General Medical Sciences)**: Basic research

**Grant Mechanisms**:

- **R01**: Research Project Grant ($250K-$500K/year, 3-5 years) - Most prestigious
- **R03**: Small Grant ($50K/year, 2 years) - Pilot/feasibility studies
- **R21**: Exploratory/Developmental ($200K/year, 2 years) - Innovative ideas
- **R34**: Clinical Trial Planning Grant ($225K/year, 3 years)
- **U01**: Research Program‚ÄîCooperative Agreement ($500K+/year)

**Fit**: HIGH (research focus)
**Strategy**:

- Year 1-2: R03 (pilot study‚Äîdiagnostic delay interventions)
- Year 3-5: R21 or R01 (larger studies‚ÄîAAT glucometer, CME effectiveness)
- Partner with university researcher (co-applicants)
