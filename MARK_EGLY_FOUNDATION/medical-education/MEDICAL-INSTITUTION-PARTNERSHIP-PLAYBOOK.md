## Partnership Models

### Model 1: Grand Rounds Partnership

**What it is**: One-time or annual presentation on AATD to faculty, residents, fellows

**Partnership Activities**:

- Foundation provides speaker (Mark Egly, Medical Director, or external AATD expert)
- 60-minute grand rounds presentation: "AATD: Early Detection, Better Outcomes"
- Q&A session
- Optional: CME credit (if institution accredits grand rounds)

**Value to Partner**:

- Free educational content (grand rounds need ongoing speakers)
- Fulfill CME requirements for trainees
- Raise awareness of rare disease

**Value to Foundation**:

- Reach 50-200 physicians per session
- Build relationship with institution
- Identify potential champions for deeper partnership

**Year 1 Target**: 5 grand rounds presentations

**Budget per Event**: $2,000 (speaker travel + materials)

---

### Model 2: CME Partnership

**What it is**: Institution offers foundation's CME course to its physicians

**Partnership Activities**:

- Institution promotes foundation's online CME course to faculty/staff
- Foundation provides free access codes
- Institution tracks completion (counts toward CME requirements)
- Optional: Joint CME accreditation (institution + foundation co-branded)

**Value to Partner**:

- Free CME content for physicians
- Addresses quality improvement need (COPD patients undertested for AATD)
- Supports institutional mission (rare disease awareness)

**Value to Foundation**:

- Scalable distribution (100s of physicians at once)
- Institutional endorsement (builds credibility)
- Potential for ongoing relationship

**Year 1 Target**: 3 CME partnerships

**Budget per Partnership**: $5,000 (customization, tech integration, marketing)

---

### Model 3: Curriculum Integration

**What it is**: AATD content permanently integrated into medical school or residency curriculum

**Partnership Activities**:

- Foundation provides curriculum materials:
  - Lecture slides
  - Case studies
  - Simulation scenarios (patient with undiagnosed AATD)
  - Assessment questions (for exams)
- Faculty teach content using foundation materials
- Students exposed to AATD in Year 2 or 3 (pulmonary block)
- Optional: Resident research projects on AATD

**Value to Partner**:

- Ready-made curriculum (saves faculty time)
- Addresses rare disease education gap
- Enhances reputation (innovative curriculum)

**Value to Foundation**:

- Reach future physicians (100-200 students per year, per school)
- Long-term impact (every graduating class learns about AATD)
- Influence medical education standards

**Year 1 Target**: 1 curriculum integration pilot

**Budget per Partnership**: $20,000 (curriculum development, faculty training, evaluation)

---

### Model 4: Clinical Protocol Partnership

**What it is**: Health system implements AATD testing protocol across all facilities

**Partnership Activities**:

- Foundation provides:
  - Evidence-based AATD testing protocol (WHO guidelines)
  - EMR order sets ("COPD Workup" includes AAT level)
  - Clinical decision support alerts (EMR pop-up: "Patient has COPD. Consider AATD testing.")
  - Staff training (how to order test, interpret results, counsel patients)
- Health system implements protocol system-wide
- Track outcomes: % COPD patients tested, # AATD diagnoses, time to diagnosis

**Value to Partner**:

- Quality improvement (align with best practices)
- Reduce malpractice risk (follow guidelines)
- Improve patient outcomes (early AATD detection)
- Potential for publication (QI study)

**Value to Foundation**:

- Massive scale (health systems see 10,000s of patients)
- Data collection (track real-world impact)
- Model for other health systems

**Year 1 Target**: 1 pilot health system

**Budget per Partnership**: $30,000 (protocol development, EMR integration, training)

---

### Model 5: Center of Excellence Partnership

**What it is**: Institution establishes dedicated AATD clinic or program (Tier 3 certification)

**Partnership Activities**:

- Institution commits to:
  - Dedicated AATD clinic (1+ day/week)
  - Multidisciplinary team (pulmonology, genetics, hepatology, social work)
  - AATD testing protocol (90%+ of COPD patients tested)
  - Clinical trials and research
- Foundation provides:
  - Center of Excellence certification (Tier 3)
  - Marketing support (listed on website, patient referrals)
  - Grant funding for clinic startup ($50K-$100K)
  - Collaboration on research and advocacy

**Value to Partner**:

- Establish reputation as AATD leader
- Attract AATD patients (regional/national referrals)
- Research opportunities (clinical trials, registries)
- Revenue (augmentation therapy, specialty care)

**Value to Foundation**:

- Comprehensive AATD care available to patients
- Research collaboration (outcomes data, clinical trials)
- Model for other institutions

**Year 1 Target**: 0 (focus on pilots)
**Year 3 Target**: 2 Centers of Excellence
**Year 5 Target**: 5 Centers of Excellence

**Budget per Partnership**: $100,000 (startup grant + ongoing support)

## Partnership Development Process

### Phase 1: Identify & Prioritize (Ongoing)

**Step 1: Research Potential Partners**

- Review Tier 1 target list
- Identify key contacts (department chairs, CME directors, QI leaders)
- Research institutional priorities (Do they have strategic focus on rare disease? Quality improvement?)

**Step 2: Warm Introductions**

- Leverage board connections, physician network, mutual contacts
- "I'd like to introduce you to Dr. X at Mayo Clinic. They might be interested in your AATD work."

**Step 3: Initial Outreach**

- Email or call key contact
- Brief intro: "Mark Egly Foundation works to eliminate AATD diagnostic delays. We're partnering with leading institutions to scale AATD education. Would you be open to a conversation?"

---

### Phase 2: Discovery & Alignment (1-2 months)

**Step 1: Discovery Call** (30-60 minutes)

- Learn about institution's needs and priorities
- Share foundation's mission and programs
- Explore potential alignment

**Questions to Ask**:

- What are your current AATD diagnosis rates? (Do they track?)
- What CME needs do you have?
- Are you interested in rare disease education?
- What quality improvement initiatives are underway?
- Who are key decision-makers for partnerships?

**Step 2: Needs Assessment**

- Identify specific pain points or opportunities
- "You mentioned resident education is a priority. Our AATD curriculum could fit in pulmonary rotation."

**Step 3: Proposal**

- Customize partnership proposal based on discovery
- Outline partnership model (Grand Rounds, CME, Curriculum, Protocol, Center of Excellence)
- Benefits to partner
- Requirements from partner
- Budget (if applicable)

---

### Phase 3: Negotiation & Agreement (2-4 months)

**Step 1: Present Proposal**

- In-person or video meeting with key stakeholders
- Walk through proposal
- Address questions and concerns

**Step 2: Negotiate Terms**

- Scope of partnership
- Responsibilities (who does what)
- Timeline
- Budget (funding, cost-sharing)
- Evaluation metrics (how will we measure success?)

**Step 3: Legal Review**

- Partnership agreement drafted by legal counsel (see Partnership Agreements section)
- Review by both parties
- Revisions as needed

**Step 4: Execute Agreement**

- Sign partnership agreement
- Announce publicly (press release, social media)

---

### Phase 4: Implementation (3-12 months)

**Step 1: Kickoff Meeting**

- Introduce teams
- Clarify roles and timeline
- Set expectations

**Step 2: Execute Partnership Activities**

- Grand Rounds: Schedule, prepare presentation, deliver
- CME: Develop custom content, integrate into LMS, market to physicians
- Curriculum: Develop materials, train faculty, pilot with one class
- Protocol: Develop protocol, integrate into EMR, train staff
- Center of Excellence: Establish clinic, certify, market

**Step 3: Monitor Progress**

- Regular check-ins (monthly or quarterly)
- Track metrics (enrollment, completion, testing rates, diagnoses)
- Address issues proactively

---

### Phase 5: Evaluation & Renewal (Annual)

**Step 1: Evaluate Partnership**

- Review metrics: Did we achieve goals?
- Collect feedback from partner
- Identify successes and challenges

**Step 2: Report to Board**

- Present partnership outcomes to board
- Recommend renewal or modifications

**Step 3: Renew or Expand**

- If successful, renew partnership agreement
- Consider expanding (e.g., Grand Rounds â†’ Curriculum Integration)
- If not successful, determine whether to continue, modify, or end

## Partnership Agreements

### Key Elements of Partnership Agreement

**1. Parties**

- Mark Egly Foundation (nonprofit, EIN [TBD])
- [Partner Institution Name]

**2. Purpose**

- Partnership goal: "Increase AATD awareness and early detection through [specific activities]"

**3. Term**

- Start date and end date (typically 1-3 years)
- Renewal provisions

**4. Responsibilities**

**Foundation Responsibilities**:

- Provide [CME course, curriculum materials, protocol, etc.]
- Fund [speaker travel, materials, grants, etc.]
- Marketing support
- Data collection and evaluation

**Partner Responsibilities**:

- Implement [grand rounds, CME promotion, curriculum, protocol, etc.]
- Provide [faculty time, EMR access, space, etc.]
- Track outcomes
- Share data (anonymized) with foundation

**5. Intellectual Property**

- Foundation retains IP rights to course materials, seals, trademarks
- Partner may use materials for educational purposes only (not commercial)

**6. Confidentiality**

- Both parties agree to protect confidential information
- Patient data shared must be de-identified (HIPAA compliant)

**7. Publicity**

- Both parties may announce partnership publicly
- Joint press releases and marketing materials
- Mutual approval of messaging

**8. Termination**

- Either party may terminate with 90 days' written notice
- Reasons for immediate termination (breach, legal issues)

**9. Liability & Indemnification**

- Each party responsible for own actions
- Malpractice insurance (partner's physicians covered by their institution)

**10. Governing Law**

- Agreement governed by laws of [State]

**11. Signatures**

- Authorized representatives from both parties

## Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Johns Hopkins Grand Rounds Partnership

**Goal**: Raise AATD awareness among Johns Hopkins faculty and trainees

**Partnership Model**: Grand Rounds

**Timeline**: 6 months (Q1-Q2 Year 1)

**Activities**:

- Q1: Initial outreach to Pulmonary Division Chief
- Q1: Discovery call and proposal
- Q2: Agreement signed
- Q2: Mark Egly presents grand rounds: "AATD: A Diagnostic Delay Crisis"
- Q2: 150 attendees (faculty, residents, fellows)
- Post-event: 20 physicians enroll in CME course

**Outcomes**:

- 150 physicians exposed to AATD content
- 20 CME enrollments
- 3 physicians became AATD Aware Certified
- 1 resident initiated AATD research project
- Johns Hopkins featured in foundation annual report

**Next Steps**: Explore curriculum integration for Year 2

**Budget**: $2,500 (speaker travel, materials, event logistics)

---

### Case Study 2: Cleveland Clinic Clinical Protocol Partnership

**Goal**: Implement system-wide AATD testing protocol across Cleveland Clinic health system

**Partnership Model**: Clinical Protocol

**Timeline**: 12 months (Year 1)

**Activities**:

- Q1: Discovery phase (assess current testing rates, EMR systems)
- Q2: Protocol development:
  - "COPD Workup" order set includes AAT level
  - EMR alert: "Patient has COPD diagnosis. AATD testing recommended per WHO guidelines. Order AAT level?"
- Q3: Staff training (100 physicians, 200 nurses)
- Q4: Go-live (protocol active in EMR)
- Year 1 end: Evaluate outcomes

**Outcomes**:

- Baseline: 12% of COPD patients tested for AATD
- 6 months post-implementation: 68% tested
- 12 months: 82% tested
- 47 new AATD diagnoses in Year 1 (vs. 12 previous year)
- Average diagnostic delay reduced from 6.5 years to 2.1 years (among Cleveland Clinic patients)

**Publications**:

- Joint manuscript: "Implementing System-Wide AATD Testing Protocol: A Quality Improvement Study"
- Presented at ATS conference

**Next Steps**: Scale protocol to other Cleveland Clinic locations; other health systems replicate model

**Budget**: $35,000 (protocol development, EMR integration, training)

### Case Study 3: University of Florida Center of Excellence

**Goal**: Establish UF as premier AATD Center of Excellence

**Partnership Model**: Center of Excellence (Tier 3)

**Timeline**: 24 months (Years 2-3)

**Activities**:

- Year 2:
  - Grant funding: $75,000 to establish AATD Clinic
  - Hire AATD Program Coordinator
  - Develop multidisciplinary team (pulmonology, genetics, hepatology, social work, nutrition)
  - Launch AATD Clinic (1 day/week)
- Year 3:
  - Expand to 2 days/week
  - Implement clinical trial (augmentation therapy outcomes study)
  - Certify as Center of Excellence (Tier 3)
  - Market nationally (patient referrals from across Southeast)

**Outcomes**:

- 200 AATD patients enrolled in clinic (Year 3)
- 15 patients enrolled in clinical trial
- UF becomes regional referral center for AATD
- 3 peer-reviewed publications
- Model for other institutions

**Next Steps**: Expand to 3 days/week; establish telemedicine for rural patients

**Budget**: $150,000 (Years 2-3 combined: startup grant, ongoing support)
