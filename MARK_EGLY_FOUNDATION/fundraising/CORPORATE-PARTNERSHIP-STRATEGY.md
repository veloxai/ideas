## Partnership Models

### Model 1: Strategic Partnership ($100K-$500K+/year)

**Description**: Deep, multi-year partnership with shared goals

**Example: Grifols Foundation + Mark Egly Foundation**

**Components**:

- **Research Funding**: $200K/year for AATD research grants (co-branded)
- **Patient Education**: Co-develop AATD patient education materials (Grifols provides content, MEF distributes)
- **Advocacy**: Joint advocacy (prior authorization reform, insurance coverage)
- **Brand Integration**: Grifols logo on Foundation website, co-branded materials

**Benefits for Grifols**:

- CSR impact (support AATD community)
- Improved patient outcomes (earlier diagnosis ‚Üí more patients eligible for Prolastin)
- Brand visibility (positive association with patient advocacy)

**Benefits for Foundation**:

- Significant revenue ($200K+/year)
- Credibility (pharmaceutical partner validates Foundation's work)
- Expertise (Grifols' AATD knowledge, research connections)

**Duration**: 3-5 years (renewable)

## Employee Giving Programs

### Why Employee Giving Matters

**Scale**:

- Large employers have 10,000-100,000+ employees
- If 10% participate at $50/year ‚Üí $50K-$500K/year
- Plus: Corporate matching (doubles impact)

**Engagement**:

- Employees feel good (payroll deduction = painless giving)
- Employer benefits (CSR, employee morale, retention)

---

### Employee Giving Models

**Model 1: Workplace Giving Campaigns**:

- **What**: Annual or ongoing payroll deduction campaigns
- **Process**:
  1. Foundation applies to be included in company's workplace giving program (e.g., United Way, Benevity)
  2. Employees choose charities from menu
  3. Donations deducted from paycheck (pre-tax if through United Way)
  4. Corporate match (many companies match employee donations 1:1 or 2:1)

**Target Platforms**:

- **United Way** (traditional workplace giving)
- **Benevity** (corporate giving platform‚Äîused by Microsoft, Google, etc.)
- **YourCause** (CSRconnect platform‚Äîused by many Fortune 500)
- **Direct employer programs** (some companies run their own)

**Model 2: Employee Matching Gifts**:

- **What**: Company matches employee donations (1:1 or 2:1)
- **Process**:
  1. Employee donates to Foundation
  2. Employee submits match request to employer
  3. Employer verifies donation + sends match

**Target Companies**: Most Fortune 500 companies have matching gift programs

**Model 3: Volunteer Grants**:

- **What**: Company donates $ for every hour employee volunteers
- **Typical**: $10-$25 per volunteer hour
- **Process**:
  1. Employee volunteers with Foundation (e.g., at awareness event, support group)
  2. Employee logs hours with employer
  3. Employer donates based on hours

**Target Companies**: Tech companies (Microsoft, Google, Apple), consulting firms (Deloitte, EY)

---

### Employee Giving Campaign Strategy

**Step 1: Identify Target Employers**:

- Healthcare companies (pharmaceutical, insurance, medical devices)
- Large employers in Foundation's geographic area (Florida, other priority states)
- Companies with strong CSR programs

**Step 2: Register with Platforms**:

- Apply to United Way, Benevity, YourCause (one-time application process)
- Profile: Foundation name, mission, impact stories, logo

**Step 3: Corporate Champion Outreach**:

- Identify employee champion (someone at company who will advocate for Foundation)
- Could be:
  - AATD patient who works there
  - Board member's colleague
  - HR/CSR manager (if existing relationship)
- Champion promotes Foundation to colleagues (lunch-and-learn, email, intranet)

**Step 4: Promotion**:

- Provide materials to employer (one-pagers, videos, patient stories)
- Offer to present (virtual or in-person lunch-and-learn)
- Leverage annual workplace giving campaign (Fall = peak season)

**Step 5: Stewardship**:

- Thank employer (recognition on website, annual report)
- Impact report (show how employee donations were used)
- Annual update (maintain relationship for Year 2)

## Cause Marketing

### What is Cause Marketing?

**Cause Marketing** = Business + Nonprofit partnership where company donates % of sales to charity

**Example**: "For every [Product] sold, [Company] donates $X to Mark Egly Foundation"

---

### Cause Marketing Benefits

**For Company**:

- Drives sales (customers want to buy from companies that give back)
- Brand differentiation (stand out from competitors)
- Positive PR (media coverage, social media buzz)

**For Foundation**:

- Revenue (% of sales)
- Brand awareness (company's marketing = free advertising for Foundation)
- New donor acquisition (customers become donors)

---

### Cause Marketing Examples

**Example 1: Golf Equipment Manufacturer**:

- **Partner**: Callaway Golf
- **Campaign**: "Drive for a Cure" (AATD awareness month, November)
- **Offer**: $10 from every golf club sold goes to Mark Egly Foundation
- **Promotion**: In-store signage, online ads, social media (#DriveForACure)
- **Projected Revenue**: 10,000 clubs sold √ó $10 = $100K

**Example 2: Pharmacy Chain**:

- **Partner**: CVS Health
- **Campaign**: "Test for AATD" (COPD Awareness Month, November)
- **Offer**: Free AATD screening at CVS MinuteClinics (sponsored by Foundation + CVS)
- **Promotion**: In-store posters, pharmacy bag inserts, social media
- **Projected Impact**: 1,000 people screened, 50 AATD cases identified

**Example 3: Genetic Testing Company**:

- **Partner**: 23andMe
- **Campaign**: "Know Your Alpha-1 Status"
- **Offer**: 23andMe adds AATD carrier screening to health reports; $5 from every health kit sold ‚Üí Foundation
- **Promotion**: Email to 23andMe customers, blog post, Foundation social media
- **Projected Revenue**: 20,000 kits √ó $5 = $100K

---

### Cause Marketing Best Practices

**Do**:

- ‚úÖ **Align values** (partner with companies whose values match Foundation's)
- ‚úÖ **Clear messaging** (customers understand where money goes)
- ‚úÖ **Time-limited** (urgency drives sales‚Äî"November only")
- ‚úÖ **Track results** (measure sales, revenue, awareness)
- ‚úÖ **Authentic** (genuine partnership, not just marketing gimmick)

**Don't**:

- ‚ùå **"Pinkwashing"** (superficial cause marketing‚Äîcompany benefits, charity gets little)
- ‚ùå **Unclear terms** (how much goes to Foundation? when? be transparent)
- ‚ùå **Misaligned partners** (e.g., tobacco company‚Äîconflicts with lung health mission)
