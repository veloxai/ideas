## Fundraising Events Overview

### Philosophy & Goals

**Mission Alignment**: Every fundraising event must:

- Raise awareness of Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency (AATD)
- Build community among patients, families, and supporters
- Generate revenue for research, patient support, and advocacy
- Honor Mark Egly's legacy and vision

**Revenue vs. Engagement Balance**:

- Not every event needs to be high-revenue (some are community-building)
- Balance big signature events with lower-cost engagement opportunities
- Long-term donor cultivation is as valuable as immediate revenue

### Annual Fundraising Goals (Years 1-3)

**Year 1: $500,000** (total from all events)

- Annual Gala: $200,000 net
- Golf Classic: $150,000 net
- Walk/Run Events: $75,000 net
- Peer-to-Peer Campaigns: $50,000 net
- Virtual Events & Other: $25,000 net

**Year 2: $750,000**

- Scale up attendance and sponsorships
- Add second golf tournament (regional expansion)
- Launch corporate team challenges

**Year 3: $1,000,000+**

- Multi-city events
- Major donor cultivation events
- Endowment campaign kickoff

### Event Portfolio Strategy

**Signature Events** (2-3 per year):

- High-production, high-revenue, high-profile
- Annual Gala, Golf Classic
- Require 6-12 months planning
- Budget: $50K-$100K investment, 3-5x ROI goal

**Community Events** (4-6 per year):

- Mid-size, local engagement, moderate revenue
- Walk/Run events, regional galas
- 3-6 months planning
- Budget: $10K-$30K investment, 2-3x ROI

**Low-Overhead Events** (ongoing):

- Virtual webinars, peer-to-peer campaigns
- Minimal staff time, leverages technology
- Rolling basis
- Budget: $1K-$5K investment, 5-10x ROI

## Annual Gala

### Event Overview

**Theme**: "Breathing Hope: An Evening to Transform AATD"
**Date**: Late spring (May) - ideal weather, post-tax season, pre-summer vacations
**Location**: Upscale hotel ballroom or event venue (rotating cities)
**Attendance Goal**: 300-400 guests (Year 1) → 500-600 (Year 3)
**Ticket Prices**: $250 individual, $2,500 table of 10
**Net Revenue Goal**: $200,000 (Year 1) → $400,000 (Year 3)

### Event Format (3.5 hours)

**6:00-7:00 PM: Cocktail Reception & Silent Auction**

- Welcome drinks and hors d'oeuvres
- Silent auction items displayed (online bidding via smartphone)
- Patient story photo gallery
- Live music (jazz trio or string quartet)
- Networking and mingling

**7:00-8:00 PM: Dinner**

- Plated 3-course meal (dietary accommodations available)
- Table conversations (icebreaker cards at each place setting)
- Background music

**8:00-9:00 PM: Program**

- **Welcome** (5 min): Foundation Board Chair
- **Mark Egly's Story** (10 min): Video tribute or Mark speaks (if able)
- **Patient Impact Story** (10 min): AATD patient shares journey (diagnostic odyssey, treatment, hope)
- **Research Update** (10 min): Funded researcher presents breakthrough (layperson-friendly)
- **Fund-a-Need** (15 min): Live appeal for specific program (e.g., "Fund 10 genetic tests at $500 each")
- **Live Auction** (10 min): 3-5 high-value items (luxury travel, sports memorabilia, experiences)
- **Closing Remarks & Thank You** (5 min): Executive Director

**9:00-9:30 PM: Dessert & Dancing**

- Dessert bar and coffee
- DJ or live band
- More networking and celebration

## Mark Egly Celebrity Golf Classic

### Event Overview

**Theme**: "Swing for a Cure: The Mark Egly Celebrity Golf Classic"
**Tagline**: "Drive Away AATD - One Swing at a Time"
**Date**: Late summer (August or September) - best golf weather
**Location**: Premier golf course (country club or resort), rotating annually
**Format**: Scramble (4-person teams), 18 holes
**Attendance Goal**: 144 golfers (36 foursomes) Year 1 → 200+ (50 foursomes) Year 3
**Entry Fee**: $1,000 per golfer ($4,000 per foursome)
**Net Revenue Goal**: $150,000 (Year 1) → $300,000 (Year 3)

### Why Golf?

**Mark Egly's PGA Membership**:

- Mark is a PGA member and golf professional - **this is THE signature event**
- Authentic connection to founder's passion
- Opens doors to celebrity golfers, PGA Tour pros, sponsors
- Differentiates Foundation from other AATD organizations

**Target Audience**:

- Corporate sponsors (golf popular with business community)
- Affluent individual donors
- PGA members and golf enthusiasts
- Sports celebrities (athletes from other sports who golf)

**Fundraising Potential**:

- High entry fees (golfers expect to pay)
- Strong sponsorship opportunities (signage, branded holes)
- Silent auction and raffle (golf memorabilia)
- Low overhead (country club provides most infrastructure)

### Event Format

**8:00 AM: Registration & Continental Breakfast**

- Check-in at clubhouse
- Breakfast buffet (coffee, pastries, fruit)
- Distribute swag bags (tournament shirt, golf balls, tees, sponsors' items)
- Meet and greet with Mark Egly and celebrity golfers

**9:00 AM: Opening Ceremony**

- Welcome from Mark Egly (brief remarks about AATD and tournament purpose)
- Introduction of celebrity golfers
- Shotgun start announcement (all teams start simultaneously on different holes)

**9:30 AM - 2:00 PM: Golf Tournament (Scramble Format)**

- 18 holes, scramble format (team selects best shot, all hit from there)
- Par: 72, total time ~4.5 hours
- Beverage cart circulating throughout
- On-course activities:
  - **Hole-in-One Contest** (sponsored, prize: car or cash)
  - **Longest Drive** (men's and women's)
  - **Closest to the Pin** (multiple holes)
  - **Putting Challenge** (practice green, during registration)
  - **Mulligan Sales** (extra stroke for $50 each, limit 2 per golfer)

**2:30 PM: Lunch & Awards Ceremony**

- Buffet lunch at clubhouse
- Awards for top 3 teams (trophies + prizes)
- Awards for individual contests (longest drive, closest to pin)
- Silent auction winners announced
- Patient impact story (3 minutes)
- Thank you to sponsors and participants
- Invitation to next year's event

## Walk/Run Events

### Event Overview

**Theme**: "Breathe Easy 5K Walk/Run for Alpha-1"
**Tagline**: "Every Step Brings Us Closer to a Cure"
**Date**: Spring (April-May) or Fall (September-October) - ideal for outdoor activity
**Location**: Local park, trail, or downtown route
**Format**: 5K (3.1 miles) walk or run, chip-timed for runners
**Attendance Goal**: 300 participants (Year 1) → 800+ (Year 3)
**Entry Fee**: $40 adult, $25 child (<12), $100 family (2 adults + 2 kids)

- Music (DJ or playlist)

**8:30 AM: Opening Ceremony**

- Welcome from Executive Director
- Brief AATD awareness talk (3 minutes)
- Patient story (2 minutes)
- National Anthem
- Race instructions and safety briefing

**9:00 AM: Race Start**

- Staggered start (runners first, then walkers, or waves by pace)
- Chip timing for runners (official results)
- Water stations at miles 1 and 2
- Course marshals and volunteers throughout

**9:30 AM - 10:30 AM: Participants Finish**

- Finish line with clock display
- Medals for all finishers
- Water, snacks, and recovery area
- Photo booth with props

**10:30 AM: Awards Ceremony**

- Top 3 overall male/female runners
- Age group winners (5-year increments)
- Top fundraiser recognition
- Raffle prize drawings (all participants entered)

**11:00 AM: Event Concludes**

- Thank participants and volunteers
- Encourage fundraising page sharing
- Promote next year's event

## Peer-to-Peer Fundraising Campaigns

### Campaign Overview

**Concept**: Empower supporters to fundraise on Foundation's behalf using personal networks

**Platforms**:

- Classy, Funraise, Mightycause, Facebook Fundraisers
- Hosted on Foundation website with CRM integration

**Campaign Types**:

1. **Birthday Fundraisers** (ongoing)
2. **In Honor/Memory Campaigns** (ongoing)
3. **Giving Tuesday Campaign** (November)
4. **Month-Long Challenges** (e.g., Dry January, Move More in March)

**Revenue Goal**: $50,000 (Year 1) → $100,000+ (Year 3)

### Birthday Fundraisers

**Concept**: Ask supporters to "give up gifts" and request donations to Foundation instead

**Promotion**:

- Email campaign to all donors and supporters (monthly)
- Social media posts featuring examples
- Facebook integration (easy to set up Facebook birthday fundraiser)

**Suggested Ask**: "This year, instead of gifts, I'm asking for donations to the Mark Egly Foundation to support AATD research and patient care."

**Tools Provided**:

- Email template for personal invitation
- Social media graphics
- Sample fundraising page text
- Thank you message template

**Incentives**:

- $250+: Personalized thank you video from Foundation leadership
- $500+: Mark Egly handwritten note
- $1,000+: Phone call with Mark Egly
- $2,500+: Recognition on Foundation website "Top Fundraisers" page

### In Honor/Memory Campaigns

**Concept**: Fundraise in honor of loved one with AATD or in memory of someone lost to disease

**Example**: "In loving memory of my father who battled Alpha-1, I'm raising funds to help others get diagnosed earlier."

**Support Provided**:

- Dedicated memorial/tribute page
- Option to upload photo and story
- Recurring donations accepted
- Acknowledgment to family (with permission)

**Promotion**:

- Bereavement outreach (sensitive, 6-12 months after loss)
- Patient community engagement
- Support group partnerships

### Giving Tuesday Campaign

**Timing**: Tuesday after Thanksgiving (late November)
**Duration**: 24 hours (or extended to 72 hours)
**Goal**: $25,000 (Year 1) → $50,000 (Year 3)

**Campaign Elements**:

1. **Matching Gift Challenge**: Secure $10K match from major donor (doubles impact)
2. **Hourly Updates**: Social media posts showing progress toward goal
3. **Fundraiser Challenge**: Encourage 50+ people to create personal fundraising pages
4. **Donor Wall**: Real-time display of donors on website (opt-in)
5. **Impact Storytelling**: Share throughout day how donations help (e.g., "$50 = 1 genetic test")

**Marketing Blitz**:

- Email sequence (3 emails: week before, day of, day after)
- Social media (10+ posts across Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn)
- Peer-to-peer activation (ask top supporters to fundraise)
- PR push (press release, media interviews)

### Month-Long Challenges

**Examples**:

- **Dry January** (no alcohol): "Give up drinks, give to research" ($25/day not drinking = $775 donation)
- **Move More in March**: Log miles walked/run, donate per mile
- **Breathe Easy April**: $50 for every outdoor activity completed
- **Golf More in May**: Donate per round played (tie to Golf Classic promotion)

**Mechanics**:

- Participants pledge to challenge and commit to fundraising goal
- Track progress on personal page
- Share updates on social media
- Foundation provides weekly encouragement emails

**Incentives**:

- Completion certificate (PDF)
- Social media shoutout
- Top 10 fundraisers: Special recognition and prizes

## Event Logistics & Operations

### Venue Selection Criteria

**Capacity**: Right-sized (80% capacity ideal - not too empty, not cramped)
**Accessibility**: ADA compliant, parking available
**Location**: Central, easy to reach, safe neighborhood
**Amenities**: A/V equipment, catering options, adequate restrooms
**Cost**: Negotiable, in-kind donations encouraged
**Availability**: Flexible date options

### Vendor Management

**Key Vendors**:

- Caterer (food & beverage)
- A/V production company
- Photographer/videographer
- Decorator/florist
- Entertainment (DJ, band, auctioneer)
- Printing (invitations, signage, programs)

**Contracts**:

- Get everything in writing
- Cancellation policies clearly stated
- Payment schedule (deposit + final payment)
- Insurance requirements (liability)

**In-Kind Donations**:

- Ask vendors to donate services (tax deduction)
- Recognition as sponsor if donate

### Volunteer Coordination

**Recruitment**:

- Board members (all expected to volunteer)
- Event committee members
- Previous volunteers
- Patient community
- Corporate volunteer groups
- Student groups (university service learning)

**Roles** (examples for Gala):

- Registration table (check-in, name tags)
- Silent auction monitors (help with bidding)
- Bid spotters (live auction)
- Guest services (answer questions, troubleshoot)
- Set-up and tear-down crew

**Training**:

- Pre-event orientation (in-person or video)
- Written instructions and talking points
- Volunteer coordinator on-site for questions

**Recognition**:

- Thank you email after event
- Volunteer appreciation event (annual)
- Recognition in annual report
- Free ticket to event (if applicable)

### Risk Management

**Insurance**:

- Event liability insurance (required by most venues)
- Special event rider on Foundation's general liability policy
- Liquor liability (if serving alcohol)
- Weather insurance (for outdoor events, optional)

**Safety & Security**:

- First aid kit on-site
- Medical personnel (for large events like 5K)
- Security personnel (for high-value auctions)
- Emergency action plan (fire, weather, medical emergency)

**Contingency Plans**:

- Weather backup (tent, indoor alternative)
- A/V failure (backup equipment)
- Speaker no-show (backup speaker)
- Low attendance (adjust food order, seating)

## Budget Management & ROI

### Event Budgeting Best Practices

**Expense Categories**:

- Venue rental
- Food & beverage
- A/V and entertainment
- Marketing and printing
- Supplies and décor
- Insurance and permits
- Staff and consultant fees (if any)
- Contingency (10% of budget)

**Revenue Categories**:

- Ticket sales
- Sponsorships
- Auction and raffle
- Individual donations (fund-a-need)
- Other (vendor booths, merchandise)

**Financial Goals**:

- Net revenue target (after all expenses)
- ROI minimum: 2:1 (raise $2 for every $1 spent)
- Stretch goal: 3:1 or higher

### Cash Flow Management

**Pre-Event**:

- Sponsor payments (typically advance payment)
- Ticket sales (online, in advance)
- Vendor deposits paid (50% typical)

**Post-Event**:

- Final vendor payments (30 days)
- Auction and pledge collection (30-60 days)
- Sponsorship fulfillment (recognition, benefits delivered)

**Accounting**:

- Separate event budgets in accounting system
- Reconcile within 30 days of event
- Board-approved budget vs. actual report
