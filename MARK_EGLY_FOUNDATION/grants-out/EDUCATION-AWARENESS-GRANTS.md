## Program Overview

### Mission

**The Mark Egly Foundation Education & Awareness Grants Program funds initiatives to**:

- **Educate healthcare providers** (reduce diagnostic delay through better provider awareness)
- **Empower patients** (knowledge is powerâ€”help patients navigate AATD)
- **Raise public awareness** (AATD is rare, but not that rareâ€”300K+ undiagnosed)
- **Eliminate stigma** (lung disease isn't always smoking-related)

**Education is the fastest path to reducing diagnostic delays.**

---

### Program Philosophy

**Provider Education First**:

- Providers (especially PCPs, pulmonologists) are gatekeepers to diagnosis
- If providers don't think of AATD, patients don't get tested
- CME-accredited education is investment in early diagnosis

**Patient-Centric Materials**:

- Written in plain language (no jargon)
- Culturally appropriate (diverse audiences)
- Accessible (multiple formatsâ€”print, video, audio)
- Actionable (what can patients DO with this info?)

**Community-Based Approach**:

- Partner with trusted community organizations
- Meet people where they are (health fairs, churches, community centers)
- Grassroots engagement (not top-down)

**Measurable Impact**:

- Every education grant must have clear outcomes
- Track reach (# people educated), behavior change (# tested), diagnoses (# found)

## Medical Education Grants

### Grant Type 1A: CME Program Development

**Purpose**: Create CME-accredited education for healthcare providers

**Funding Range**: $10,000 - $30,000

**Eligible Applicants**:

- Medical schools
- Academic medical centers
- Professional associations (ACP, CHEST, ATS)
- CME providers (Medscape, Pri-Med, etc.)

**Project Examples**:

- "AATD 101 for Primary Care Providers" (1-hour online CME, $15K)
- "Reducing Diagnostic Delay in AATD" (live symposium + enduring materials, $25K)
- "AATD and Liver Disease: What Hepatologists Need to Know" (webinar series, $12K)

**Required Deliverables**:

- CME-accredited content (AMA PRA Category 1 Credits)
- Learning objectives aligned with AATD diagnostic competencies
- Pre/post-test (measure knowledge gain)
- Evaluation survey (satisfaction, intent to change practice)
- Impact report (# participants, knowledge gain, behavior change)

**Impact Metrics**:

- **Reach**: # providers completing CME
- **Learning**: Pre/post-test score improvement
- **Behavior**: % providers who report ordering more AAT tests (3-month follow-up survey)
- **Outcome**: # AATD diagnoses attributed to CME (track via participant surveys)

## Application & Review

### Application Process

**Step 1: Letter of Intent (LOI)**:

- Online form (Foundation website)
- Required info: Project title, applicant, brief description (250 words), amount requested
- Review: 2 weeks
- Outcome: Invited to full application or not

**Step 2: Full Application**:

- Application form (online)
- Project narrative (3 pages max):
  - Problem statement (why is this needed?)
  - Project description (what will you do?)
  - Target audience (who will benefit?)
  - Evaluation plan (how will you measure success?)
  - Sustainability (how will project continue after grant ends?)
- Budget & justification (1 page)
- Organizational information (mission, 501c3 status, financials)
- Letters of support (optional)

**Step 3: Review**:

- Review panel: Medical educators, patient advocates, communication experts
- Review criteria: Need, feasibility, impact, budget, sustainability
- Decision: 60 days

**Step 4: Award**:

- Grant agreement (terms, reporting requirements)
- Payment: 50% upfront, 50% at midpoint (after progress report)

---

### Review Criteria

**Scored 1-5 (5 = excellent)**:

1. **Need** (20%):
   - Clear evidence of need for this education/awareness project?
   - Addresses AATD education gap?

2. **Approach** (25%):
   - Sound strategy?
   - Evidence-based (if applicable)?
   - Feasible in timeline/budget?

3. **Reach** (15%):
   - How many people will be reached?
   - Appropriate target audience?

4. **Impact** (25%):
   - Clear, measurable outcomes?
   - Will reduce diagnostic delay (if provider education)?
   - Will empower patients (if patient education)?

5. **Budget** (10%):
   - Reasonable costs?
   - Good value for money?

6. **Sustainability** (5%):
   - Will project continue after grant ends?
   - Scalable to other communities?

**Overall Impact Score**: Average of 6 criteria

**Funding Decision**:

- Score â‰¥ 4.0: Fund
- Score 3.0-3.9: Discuss (may fund with modifications)
- Score < 3.0: Do not fund

---

### Reporting Requirements

**Progress Report** (midpoint):

- Due: 6 months into project (or at midpoint for <12-month grants)
- Content: Progress vs. milestones, challenges, budget update
- Triggers: Second payment (50% of grant)

**Final Report**:

- Due: 60 days after project end
- Content:
  - Summary of activities
  - Impact metrics (reach, engagement, outcomes)
  - Lessons learned
  - Sustainability plan (what happens next?)
  - Financial report (budget vs. actual)

**Impact Tracking**:

- Foundation will follow up annually (Years 1-3 post-grant)
- Long-term impact survey (Did project achieve lasting change?)
