## Program Overview

### Mission

**The Mark Egly Foundation Patient Assistance Microgrants Program provides direct financial assistance to AATD patients for**:

- **Diagnostic testing** (AAT blood test, genetic testing, travel to specialist)
- **Emergency assistance** (utility bills, rent, medical bills during crisis)
- **Treatment access** (copays, medications, equipment)
- **Quality of life** (pulmonary rehab, mental health counseling, support group travel)

**Because no patient should face financial barriers to AATD diagnosis or care.**

---

### Program Philosophy

**Direct Patient Impact**:

- Money goes directly to patients (or paid to providers on behalf of patients)
- Fast turnaround (application to decision in 7-14 days)
- No bureaucracy (simple application, minimal paperwork)

**Meet Patients Where They Are**:

- Financial hardship is common in AATD (medical costs, lost work, disability)
- Many patients live paycheck to paycheck
- Small amounts ($500-$2,000) can make big difference

**Focus on Diagnosis + Crisis**:

- Priority: Help undiagnosed people get tested
- Priority: Emergency assistance (prevent eviction, keep lights on)
- Priority: Treatment access (copays for augmentation therapy)

**Dignity and Respect**:

- No judgment (everyone deserves help)
- Privacy protected (confidential process)
- Patient-centered (what does the patient need?)

## Grant Types

### Type 1: Diagnostic Assistance Grants ($100-$1,500)

**Purpose**: Help undiagnosed individuals get AATD testing

**Eligible Expenses**:

- **AAT blood test** ($50-$150)
  - Lab fees (if not covered by insurance)
  - Copay for doctor visit to order test
- **Genetic testing** ($100-$500)
  - Genotyping or sequencing (if not covered by insurance)
- **Travel to specialist** ($200-$1,000)
  - Gas, mileage, or airfare to see AATD specialist
  - Hotel if overnight stay required
  - Meals (per diem rates)
- **Pulmonary function testing** ($100-$500)
  - Spirometry, DLCO (if needed to support AATD diagnosis)

**Examples**:

- $150: AAT blood test + doctor visit copay (uninsured patient)
- $500: Genetic testing (insurance denied coverage)
- $800: Travel to AATD specialist 300 miles away (gas + hotel)

**Priority**: High (diagnosis is Foundation's core mission)

---

### Type 2: Emergency Assistance Grants ($500-$2,000)

**Purpose**: Help AATD patients in financial crisis

**Eligible Expenses**:

- **Utility bills** (electricity, gas, water)
  - Prevent shutoff during illness
- **Rent/mortgage** (one month maximum)
  - Prevent eviction or foreclosure
- **Medical bills** (past due)
  - Hospital, doctor, pharmacy bills
- **Transportation** (car repair, bus pass)
  - Get to medical appointments

**Examples**:

- $500: Electric bill (shutoff notice during hospitalization)
- $1,200: One month's rent (lost work due to AATD exacerbation)
- $1,500: Past-due medical bills (payment plan required by clinic)

**Priority**: High (crisis intervention)

**Special Rules**:

- One emergency grant per patient per year
- Verification required (shutoff notice, eviction notice, medical bills)
- Direct payment to creditor (not to patient)

---

### Type 3: Treatment Access Grants ($500-$2,000)

**Purpose**: Help AATD patients access treatment

**Eligible Expenses**:

- **Augmentation therapy copays** ($500-$2,000)
  - Insurance copay for AAT infusions (Prolastin, Aralast, Zemaira, Glassia)
  - One month supply
- **Medications** ($100-$500)
  - COPD medications (inhalers, nebulizers)
  - Liver medications
  - Oxygen (if not covered by insurance)
- **Medical equipment** ($200-$1,000)
  - Nebulizer, pulse oximeter, spirometer
  - Oxygen concentrator (supplemental if insurance doesn't cover full cost)
- **Pulmonary rehabilitation** ($500-$1,500)
  - Copays for pulmonary rehab program (typically 12-week program)

**Examples**:

- $1,500: Augmentation therapy copay (one month)
- $400: COPD medications (inhalers + nebulizer meds)
- $800: Pulmonary rehabilitation copays (12 sessions √ó $67)

**Priority**: Medium-High (treatment is critical, but many have insurance coverage)

**Special Rules**:

- Proof of AATD diagnosis required (lab report)
- Prescription or doctor's order required
- Payment to pharmacy/provider (not to patient)

---

### Type 4: Quality of Life Grants ($250-$1,500)

**Purpose**: Improve AATD patient quality of life

**Eligible Expenses**:

- **Mental health counseling** ($250-$1,000)
  - Therapy sessions (copays for 4-8 sessions)
  - AATD-related anxiety, depression, adjustment
- **Support group participation** ($200-$800)
  - Travel to AATD support group or conference
  - Registration fees
- **Adaptive equipment** ($300-$1,500)
  - Wheelchair, walker, grab bars, shower chair
  - Make daily life safer/easier
- **Respite care** ($500-$1,500)
  - Temporary caregiver for patient (so family can rest)
  - Or: Temporary caregiver for patient's children (so patient can rest)

**Examples**:

- $600: Mental health counseling (8 sessions √ó $75 copay)
- $500: Travel to Alpha-1 Foundation Conference (airfare + hotel)
- $1,200: Wheelchair (insurance denied coverage)

**Priority**: Medium (important for holistic care)

**Special Rules**:

- Must relate to AATD (not general life expenses)
- Letter from healthcare provider (recommending mental health, equipment, etc.)

---

### Type 5: Family Support Grants ($100-$1,000)

**Purpose**: Support family members of AATD patients (especially children)

**Eligible Expenses**:

- **Genetic testing for family members** ($100-$500)
  - Siblings, children, parents of AATD patient
  - Cascade screening (after one family member diagnosed)
- **Travel for family to support patient** ($200-$800)
  - Family member travels to be with hospitalized AATD patient
  - Transportation, lodging, meals
- **Childcare during patient's illness** ($200-$1,000)
  - Temporary childcare when AATD parent is hospitalized or too ill to care for children

**Examples**:

- $300: Genetic testing for patient's 3 siblings
- $600: Airfare for adult child to visit dying AATD parent in hospice
- $800: Childcare for patient's 2 kids during 2-week hospitalization

**Priority**: Medium (supporting families supports patients)

## Eligibility Criteria

### Patient Eligibility

**Must Meet All Of**:

1. **AATD diagnosis** (or suspected AATD if applying for diagnostic assistance)
   - Documented AAT level <100 mg/dL (or PiZZ, PiSZ, etc. genotype)
   - Or: Clinical suspicion of AATD (unexplained COPD, liver disease, family history)

2. **Financial need**:
   - Household income <300% of Federal Poverty Level (FPL)
     - Example (2026): <$45,000/year for individual, <$93,000 for family of 4
   - Or: Significant medical expenses (>10% of income)
   - Or: Recent financial crisis (job loss, major medical event)

3. **U.S. residency**:
   - U.S. citizen or legal resident
   - Living in United States

4. **Not eligible for other assistance**:
   - Applied for (or not eligible for) other assistance programs:
     - Pharmaceutical patient assistance programs (PAPs)
     - State Medicaid
     - Alpha-1 Foundation assistance (if applicable)
   - Mark Egly Foundation = "last resort" (use other resources first)

**Proof Required**:

- AATD diagnosis (lab report) OR doctor's letter (for suspected AATD)
- Income verification (pay stub, tax return, Social Security award letter, unemployment notice)
- Expense documentation (bill, quote, invoice)

---

### Exclusions (What We Don't Fund)

**We do not fund**:

- ‚ùå Credit card debt (not related to medical expenses)
- ‚ùå Legal fees
- ‚ùå Car payments (except if car needed for medical appointments)
- ‚ùå Non-medical bills (except utilities, rent in crisis situations)
- ‚ùå Cosmetic procedures
- ‚ùå Alternative therapies not supported by evidence
- ‚ùå Expenses already incurred >90 days ago (must be current/upcoming expense)

## Application Process

### Step 1: Inquiry

**How to Start**:

- **Online form**: MarkEglyFoundation.org/Microgrants
- **Phone**: 1-800-XXX-XXXX (toll-free, Monday-Friday 9am-5pm EST)
- **Email**: assistance@markeglyfoundation.org

**Required Info**:

- Name, contact info
- Brief description of need (2-3 sentences)

**Response Time**: 2 business days (Foundation contacts applicant)

---

### Step 2: Application

**How to Apply**:

- Online application (10-15 minutes to complete)
- Or: Phone application (Foundation staff completes form over phone with applicant)

**Required Information**:

1. **Applicant Information**:
   - Name, address, phone, email, date of birth
   - AATD diagnosis date (if applicable)
   - Doctor's name and contact info

2. **Financial Information**:
   - Household size
   - Household income (annual)
   - Source of income (employment, disability, Social Security)
   - Major medical expenses (past year)

3. **Request Details**:
   - Grant type (diagnostic, emergency, treatment, quality of life, family support)
   - Amount requested (specific $ amount)
   - What expense will be paid? (be specific)
   - Why is this needed? (2-3 sentences)
   - Have you applied for other assistance? (yes/no, details)

4. **Supporting Documents** (upload or mail):
   - AATD diagnosis (lab report, doctor's letter) [if applicable]
   - Income verification (pay stub, tax return, award letter)
   - Expense documentation (bill, quote, invoice)
   - Doctor's letter/prescription (for treatment or equipment requests)

**Application Review**: 7-14 days

---

### Step 3: Review & Decision

**Review Process**:

- **Reviewed by**: Patient Services Coordinator
- **Evaluated on**:
  - Eligibility (meets criteria?)
  - Completeness (all documents provided?)
  - Need (is request reasonable and necessary?)
  - Budget (funds available?)

**Decision Criteria**:

- ‚úÖ **Approve**: Meets all criteria, funds available
- ‚è∏Ô∏è **Pending**: Need more information (contact applicant)
- ‚ùå **Deny**: Doesn't meet criteria, funds not available, or duplicate request

**Notification**:

- Email + phone call (within 7-14 days of application)
- If approved: Award letter, payment details
- If denied: Reason provided, resources for other assistance

---

### Step 4: Payment

**Payment Methods**:

- **Direct payment to provider/creditor** (preferred):
  - Foundation pays provider directly (medical bill, utility company, landlord)
  - Ensures funds used for intended purpose
- **Reimbursement to patient** (if already paid):
  - Patient provides proof of payment (receipt, canceled check)
  - Foundation reimburses patient (check or ACH)
- **Check to patient** (for non-bill expenses):
  - Travel, childcare, etc. (where direct payment not feasible)

**Payment Timeline**:

- Direct payment: 7-14 days after approval
- Reimbursement/check to patient: 14-21 days after approval

### Patient Outcomes (Track via Survey)

**Short-Term** (0-3 months post-grant):

- **Access**: Did grant help you access needed service/care? (yes/no)

- **Health**: Has your health improved? (better, same, worse)
- **Stability**: Are you financially stable? (yes, somewhat, no)

**Long-Term** (1-2 years post-grant):

- **Diagnosis**: For diagnostic grants‚Äîwere you diagnosed with AATD? (yes/no)
