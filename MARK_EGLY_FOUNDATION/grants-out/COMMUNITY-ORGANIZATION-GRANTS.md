## Program Overview

### Mission

**The Mark Egly Foundation Community Organization Support Grants Program funds grassroots organizations to**:

- **Build AATD support groups** (local, regional, online)
- **Strengthen patient advocacy** (empower patients to advocate for themselves)
- **Increase community awareness** (educate local communities about AATD)
- **Reduce isolation** (connect patients, caregivers, families)

**Because strong communities save lives.**

---

### Program Philosophy

**Grassroots First**:

- Local communities know their needs best
- Small, volunteer-led organizations can have big impact
- Foundation's role: Enable and amplify (not control)

**Build Capacity**:

- Many support groups are all-volunteer, no budget
- Grants help professionalize and sustain
- Training and technical assistance (not just money)

**Collaboration Over Duplication**:

- Encourage partnerships (local groups + national organizations)
- Share best practices across grantees
- Build AATD community network

**Patient-Led, Patient-Focused**:

- Grants must be led by AATD patients or patient advocates
- Programs must serve AATD patients/families
- Patient voices drive strategy

---

## Grant Categories

### Category 1: Support Group Startup Grants ($3,000-$10,000)

**Purpose**: Launch new AATD support groups

**Eligible Organizations**:

- New support groups (0-2 years old)
- Volunteer-led or fiscally sponsored
- Serve specific geographic area or online community

**Eligible Expenses**:

- **Meeting space** (room rental for in-person meetings)
- **Technology** (Zoom subscription, website hosting, Facebook ads)
- **Marketing** (flyers, social media, local newspaper ads)
- **Materials** (educational materials, name tags, refreshments)
- **Guest speakers** (honorariums for medical experts, patient speakers)
- **Facilitation training** (support group leader training)

**Examples**:

- "$5K to launch AATD support group in Jacksonville, FL" (monthly meetings, 12 months)
- "$8K to start online support group for young adults with AATD" (website, Zoom, marketing)
- "$10K to establish AATD caregiver support group" (6 cities, quarterly meetings)

**Grant Duration**: 1 year (with option to apply for Sustaining Grant in Year 2)

**Expected Outcomes**:

- Support group established (min. 3 meetings in Year 1)
- Attendance (min. 10 participants/meeting average)
- Patient satisfaction (survey: 80%+ find group helpful)

---

### Category 2: Support Group Sustaining Grants ($5,000-$15,000)

**Purpose**: Sustain and grow existing AATD support groups

**Eligible Organizations**:

- Established support groups (2+ years old)
- Demonstrated impact (regular meetings, member satisfaction)
- Plan for growth (expand reach, add services)

**Eligible Expenses**:

- **Ongoing operations** (meeting space, technology, marketing)
- **Program expansion** (add new meeting locations, new programs like exercise class)
- **Events** (annual conference, awareness events, fundraisers)
- **Staff** (part-time coordinator if group has grown significantly)

**Examples**:

- "$10K to sustain AATD support group in Phoenix" (24 monthly meetings + annual conference)
- "$12K to expand online support group" (add Spanish-language meetings + mental health webinars)
- "$15K to hire part-time coordinator for multi-city support group network" (5 cities, 60 meetings/year)

**Grant Duration**: 1 year (renewable annually if outcomes met)

**Expected Outcomes**:

- Group sustained (min. 10 meetings/year)
- Growth (10% increase in attendance or reach)
- Member satisfaction (survey: 85%+ find group helpful)

---

### Category 3: Patient Advocacy Grants ($5,000-$15,000)

**Purpose**: Empower patients to advocate for themselves and the AATD community

**Eligible Organizations**:

- Patient-led advocacy organizations
- State or regional rare disease councils (with AATD focus)
- Coalitions (AATD + other lung/liver disease groups)

**Eligible Expenses**:

- **Advocacy training** (train-the-trainer, advocate skills workshops)
- **Policy campaigns** (grassroots advocacy, state legislation, awareness campaigns)
- **Patient storytelling** (media training, video production, story collection)
- **Coalition building** (partnerships with other rare disease organizations)

**Examples**:

- "$8K for AATD Advocacy Day at State Capitol" (20 advocates, meetings with legislators, media)
- "$12K to train 50 AATD patient advocates" (3-day training, ongoing support)
- "$10K for AATD patient storytelling project" (collect 25 stories, publish book + website)

**Grant Duration**: 1-2 years

**Expected Outcomes**:

- Advocates trained (min. 20)
- Advocacy actions (min. 50‚Äîletters, meetings, media interviews)
- Policy impact (legislation introduced, media coverage, awareness increased)

---

### Category 4: Community Awareness Grants ($5,000-$15,000)

**Purpose**: Raise AATD awareness in local communities

**Eligible Organizations**:

- Community-based organizations (CBOs)
- Local health departments
- Places of worship (churches, mosques, synagogues)
- Community health centers

**Eligible Expenses**:

- **Awareness campaigns** (flyers, posters, social media, local media ads)
- **Community events** (health fairs, screening events, educational seminars)
- **Partnerships** (collaborate with local healthcare providers, community organizations)

**Examples**:

- "$10K for AATD Awareness Week in Miami" (health fair, screening events, media blitz)
- "$8K to integrate AATD education into COPD support program" (community health center partnership)
- "$12K for faith-based AATD awareness campaign" (10 churches, educational seminars + screening)

**Grant Duration**: 6-12 months

**Expected Outcomes**:

- Awareness reached (min. 5,000 people)
- Engagement (min. 500 people attend events or receive materials)
- Testing (min. 50 people tested for AATD)
- Diagnoses (estimated 3-5 AATD cases found)

---

### Category 5: Capacity Building Grants ($3,000-$10,000)

**Purpose**: Strengthen organizational capacity of AATD community organizations

**Eligible Organizations**:

- Any AATD patient organization (support group, advocacy org, CBO)
- Must demonstrate need for capacity building

**Eligible Expenses**:

- **Strategic planning** (facilitated planning retreat)
- **Board development** (board recruitment, training, governance)
- **Fundraising capacity** (donor database, fundraising training, grant writing)
- **Financial management** (accounting software, bookkeeping, audit)
- **Technology** (website redesign, CRM system, social media tools)

**Examples**:

- "$5K for strategic planning retreat for AATD support group" (facilitator, materials, planning)
- "$8K to develop fundraising capacity" (donor database, grant writing training, annual appeal)
- "$10K for board development + governance" (board recruitment, training, bylaws revision)

**Grant Duration**: 6-12 months

**Expected Outcomes**:

- Capacity strengthened (strategic plan, new board members, donor database)
- Sustainability improved (fundraising increased 20%, grants awarded)

## Eligibility Requirements

### Organization Eligibility

**Must Meet All Of**:

1. **Mission Alignment**:
   - Primary focus on AATD (or AATD is significant program area)
   - Mission aligns with Mark Egly Foundation's mission (reduce diagnostic delays, support patients)

2. **Legal Status**:
   - 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization (or fiscally sponsored by 501c3)
   - Registered to operate in state(s) where services provided
   - In good standing (no legal or financial issues)

3. **Leadership**:
   - **Patient-led**: At least 50% of board or leadership are AATD patients, family members, or caregivers
   - **Diverse**: Board/leadership reflects diversity of AATD community (race, ethnicity, age, gender, geography)

4. **Financial Responsibility**:
   - Sound financial management (budget, financial statements if >$50K annual revenue)
   - No outstanding debts to Mark Egly Foundation (if previously funded)

5. **Track Record** (for Sustaining Grants):
   - Established organization (2+ years)
   - Demonstrated impact (testimonials, attendance data, surveys)

---

### Project Eligibility

**Must Meet All Of**:

1. **Clear Need**: Evidence of need for project (community assessment, patient input)
2. **Feasible**: Realistic plan, budget, and timeline
3. **Measurable**: Clear outcomes (how will you measure success?)
4. **Sustainable**: Plan for continuation after grant ends (if ongoing program)
5. **Collaborative**: Partnerships encouraged (not required, but preferred)

---

### Exclusions (What We Don't Fund)

**We do not fund**:

- ‚ùå Organizations without patient leadership
- ‚ùå For-profit organizations
- ‚ùå Political campaigns or lobbying (issue advocacy OK, but not partisan politics)
- ‚ùå Religious activities (faith-based organizations OK, but grant must fund secular services)
- ‚ùå Capital campaigns (building construction, major equipment)
- ‚ùå Endowments
- ‚ùå Individuals (see Patient Assistance Microgrants Program instead)

## Application Process

### Step 1: Letter of Intent (LOI)

**Purpose**: Brief proposal to gauge interest before full application

**Required Information**:

- Organization name, mission, history (1 paragraph)
- Project description (250 words max)
- Amount requested
- Contact information

**Submission**:

- Online form (Foundation website)
- Rolling submissions (reviewed quarterly: Jan 1, Apr 1, Jul 1, Oct 1)
- Decision within 3 weeks (invite to full application or not)

**Outcome**:

- ‚úÖ Invited to full application (~60% of LOIs)
- ‚ùå Not invited (feedback provided, may reapply next quarter)

---

### Step 2: Full Application

**Required Documents**:

1. **Application Form**:
   - Organization information (name, address, EIN, 501c3 status)
   - Contact person (name, title, email, phone)
   - Grant category (startup, sustaining, advocacy, awareness, capacity building)
   - Amount requested

2. **Organization Overview** (2 pages max):
   - Mission and history
   - Programs and services
   - Target population (who do you serve?)
   - Leadership (board, staff, volunteers)
   - Financials (annual budget, revenue sources)

3. **Project Narrative** (4 pages max):
   - **Need**: What problem does this project address? (evidence: data, patient stories)
   - **Project Description**: What will you do? (activities, timeline)
   - **Outcomes**: How will you measure success? (specific, measurable outcomes)
   - **Sustainability**: How will project continue after grant ends?
   - **Collaboration**: Any partnerships? (letters of support encouraged)

4. **Budget** (1 page):
   - Detailed line-item budget (personnel, supplies, marketing, other)
   - Budget narrative (justify each expense)
   - Other funding (confirmed, pending, or in-kind)

5. **Attachments**:
   - 501(c)(3) determination letter (or fiscal sponsor's)
   - Board list (names, affiliations, AATD connection)
   - Financial statements (if annual budget >$50K)
   - Letters of support (optional, 2 max)

**Submission**:

- Online portal (Foundation website)
- Quarterly deadlines: Jan 15, Apr 15, Jul 15, Oct 15
- Review cycle: 60-90 days

---

### Step 3: Review & Decision

**Review Process**:

**Initial Screening**:

- Foundation staff reviews for completeness and eligibility
- Incomplete applications returned (1 week to complete)

**Peer Review Panel**:

- 5-7 reviewers:
  - AATD patients (2-3)
  - Community organization leaders (1-2)
  - Foundation staff (1)
  - Content experts (1‚Äîe.g., support group facilitator, advocacy expert)

**Review Criteria** (scored 1-5, 5 = excellent):

1. **Need** (20%): Clear evidence of need? Addresses gap in AATD community?
2. **Approach** (25%): Sound strategy? Feasible plan? Realistic budget?
3. **Impact** (25%): Clear outcomes? Likely to achieve goals? Benefits many patients?
4. **Organization Capacity** (15%): Capable leadership? Financial stability? Track record?
5. **Sustainability** (10%): Will project continue after grant? Diversified funding?
6. **Collaboration** (5%): Partnerships? Avoids duplication?

**Overall Impact Score**: Average of 6 criteria

**Funding Decision**:

- Score ‚â• 4.0: Fund (if budget allows)
- Score 3.0-3.9: Discuss (may fund with modifications)
- Score < 3.0: Do not fund

**Board Approval**:

- Grants >$15K require Board of Directors approval
- Grants ‚â§$15K approved by Chief Development Officer

**Notification**:

- Within 90 days of deadline
- Funded: Award letter + grant agreement
- Not funded: Reviewer feedback, encouragement to revise and resubmit

## Award Management

### Grant Agreement

**Standard Terms**:

- Award period (start date, end date‚Äîtypically 12 months)
- Approved budget (line-item)
- Reporting requirements (see below)
- Acknowledgment (grantee must acknowledge Foundation in materials)
- Logo usage (Foundation provides logo, usage guidelines)

**Payment Schedule**:

- 50% upfront (upon signed grant agreement)
- 50% at midpoint (after progress report approved)

---

### Reporting Requirements

**Progress Report** (midpoint):

- **Due**: 6 months into grant period
- **Content**:
  - Activities completed vs. planned
  - Outcomes achieved (with data)
  - Challenges encountered (and how addressed)
  - Budget update (spent vs. remaining)
  - Stories (1-2 patient stories‚Äîwith permission)
  - Photos (program activities, participants‚Äîwith permission)
- **Triggers**: Second payment (50% of grant)

**Final Report**:

- **Due**: 60 days after grant period ends
- **Content**:
  - Summary of activities
  - Outcomes achieved (with data‚Äîattendance, surveys, testimonials)
  - Impact stories (patient testimonials, success stories)
  - Financial report (budget vs. actual)
  - Sustainability plan (what happens next?)
  - Lessons learned (what worked, what didn't)

**Optional Site Visit**:

- Foundation may conduct site visit during grant period (with advance notice)
- Informal, supportive (not punitive)

---

### Grant Modifications

**Budget Modifications**:

- <20% reallocation: No approval needed (report in progress report)
- > 20% reallocation: Email request, Foundation approval required

**Timeline Extensions**:

- No-cost extension: Up to 6 months (email request 60 days before grant ends)
- Approval: Chief Development Officer

**Scope Changes**:

- Minor changes: Report in progress report (no prior approval needed)
- Major changes: Email request, Foundation approval required
