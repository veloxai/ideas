### Medical Transport (For Patients Who Can't Fly)

**Scenarios**:

- Patient on continuous oxygen (can't flyâ€”airlines restrict oxygen concentrators)
- Patient too ill to fly (severe lung disease, post-surgery)
- Patient needs wheelchair-accessible transport

**Options**:

**1. Ground Ambulance** (Non-Emergency Medical Transport):

- Wheelchair-accessible van (patient reclines, oxygen available)
- Cost: $2-$5/mile (e.g., 300 miles = $600-$1,500)
- Foundation covers (if financial need)

**2. Medical Air Ambulance** (Extreme Cases):

- Air ambulance with medical team (nurse, respiratory therapist, oxygen)
- Cost: $20K-$50K (very expensiveâ€”reserved for emergencies)
- Foundation may contribute (but typically insurance coversâ€”or patient's out-of-pocket max)

### Oxygen Coordination

**Problem**: Patients on supplemental oxygen (need oxygen at destinationâ€”can't bring own concentrator on plane)

**Solution**: Foundation coordinates oxygen delivery (to hotel or patient's home at destination)

**Process**:

1. Patient notifies Foundation (2 weeks before travel)
2. Foundation contacts local DME supplier (at destination)
3. DME delivers portable oxygen concentrator (to hotel or patient's destination address)
4. Patient uses oxygen during stay (rental $50-$100/week)
5. Patient returns concentrator (before leavingâ€”DME picks up)
6. Foundation covers cost (if not covered by patient's insurance)
