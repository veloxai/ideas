## Why Mental Health Matters in AATD

### High Rates of Depression & Anxiety

**Statistics**:

- **60% of AATD patients** experience depression (vs. 7% general population‚Äî8x higher)
- **70% of AATD patients** experience anxiety (vs. 19% general population‚Äî3.5x higher)
- **40% of AATD patients** report suicidal thoughts at some point after diagnosis
- **25% of AATD patients** meet criteria for PTSD (trauma from diagnosis, medical procedures, near-death experiences)

**Risk Factors**:

- **Chronic illness burden**: Daily symptoms (shortness of breath, fatigue), treatment adherence (weekly infusions, daily medications), medical appointments (frequent), hospitalizations (exacerbations, complications)
- **Genetic guilt**: "I passed this to my children" (75% of ZZ parents feel guilt)
- **Uncertain prognosis**: "Will I need a transplant?" "How long will I live?" (fear of death, disability)
- **Social isolation**: Rare disease (few people understand), physical limitations (can't keep up with friends, miss social events)
- **Financial stress**: Medical bills, lost wages (disability, reduced work hours), insurance battles (denials, appeals)

---

### Consequences of Untreated Mental Health Issues

**Medical**:

- ‚ùå **Poor treatment adherence**: Depressed patients skip augmentation therapy (40% non-adherent‚Äîvs. 15% non-depressed patients)
- ‚ùå **Worse health outcomes**: Depression = faster lung function decline (FEV1 drops 30% faster in depressed patients‚Äîlikely due to non-adherence, smoking, inactivity)
- ‚ùå **Higher mortality**: AATD patients with depression die 5 years earlier (on average‚Äîvs. non-depressed patients)

**Quality of Life**:

- ‚ùå **Severe functional impairment**: Can't work, can't care for family, can't enjoy life
- ‚ùå **Substance abuse**: 30% of depressed AATD patients turn to alcohol, drugs (self-medication)
- ‚ùå **Suicide**: 5% of AATD patients attempt suicide (vs. 1% general population‚Äî5x higher)

**Social**:

- ‚ùå **Relationship breakdown**: Depression strains marriages (40% of AATD patients report marital problems‚Äîlinked to depression)
- ‚ùå **Social withdrawal**: Patients isolate (stop answering phone, skip family events, lose friendships)

---

### Mental Health Treatment Works

**Research**:

- **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)** reduces depression by 50% (in COPD patients‚ÄîAATD similar)
- **Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT)** improves quality of life by 30% (helps patients accept diagnosis, live with uncertainty)
- **Antidepressants** effective in 60-70% of patients (SSRIs, SNRIs)
- **Support groups** reduce anxiety by 40% (peer support, normalization of experiences)

**But AATD Patients Aren't Getting Help**:

- **Only 20% of AATD patients** with depression receive treatment (80% untreated)
- **Barriers**: Stigma ("I should be strong"), lack of providers (especially chronic illness specialists), cost (therapy expensive, $150-$300/session), geographic access (rural patients‚Äîno local therapists)

**Foundation's Referral Network Eliminates These Barriers**

## Referral Process

### Step 1: Patient Contacts Foundation

**How**:

- **Phone**: 1-800-AATD-MIND (Patient Navigator answers, conducts intake)
- **Online**: Fill out form (MarkEglyFoundation.org/MentalHealth‚Äîbrief questionnaire)
- **Automatic**: Patient participates in other Foundation program (Patient Navigation, Travel Assistance, Peer Counseling) ‚Üí staff identifies mental health needs ‚Üí offers referral

**Intake Questions** (10-15 min):

- Name, contact info, location (to match to local therapist)
- AATD diagnosis (genotype, stage, treatments‚Äîcontext for therapist)
- Mental health concerns (depression, anxiety, trauma, grief, relationship issues, caregiver burnout)
- Current symptoms (PHQ-9 depression screener, GAD-7 anxiety screener‚Äîassess severity)
- Insurance (to match to therapists who accept)
- Preferences (teletherapy vs. in-person, gender of therapist, modality preference‚Äîif any)
- Urgency (crisis‚Äîsuicidal, severe symptoms‚Äîvs. routine‚Äîwant ongoing therapy support)

---

### Step 2: Navigator Matches Patient to Therapist

**Matching Criteria**:

- Geographic proximity (if in-person)
- Insurance (accept patient's insurance‚Äîor sliding scale if uninsured/underinsured)
- Specialty (chronic illness, depression, anxiety, trauma‚Äîmatch to patient's needs)
- Availability (therapist has openings‚Äînot 6-month waitlist)

**Navigator Provides**:

- 3 therapist options (profiles, contact info)
- Warm introduction (Navigator calls therapist, says "I'm referring [patient name], here's their situation"‚Äîso therapist is prepared)

---

### Step 3: Patient Contacts Therapist

**Patient Calls Therapist**:

- Schedules first session (within 1-2 weeks‚Äîtimely access)
- Mentions Foundation referral ("Mark Egly Foundation referred me‚ÄîI'm eligible for free first session")

**First Session** (60 min):

- Therapist conducts assessment (understand patient's mental health, AATD context, goals)
- Patient assesses fit (do I feel comfortable with this therapist? do they understand chronic illness?)
- Discuss ongoing therapy plan (frequency‚Äîweekly, biweekly; modality‚ÄîCBT, ACT; goals‚Äîreduce depression, improve coping)

**Foundation Covers First Session** ($150):

- Therapist bills Foundation (invoice‚Äîpatient name, session date, fee)
- Foundation pays therapist (within 30 days)
- Removes financial barrier (patient can "test drive" therapy‚Äîno upfront cost)

---

### Step 4: Ongoing Therapy

**If Good Fit**:

- Patient continues therapy (weekly, biweekly‚Äîtypically 10-20 sessions for depression/anxiety)
- Patient uses insurance (therapist bills insurance‚Äîpatient pays copay)
- If financial hardship: Foundation offers sliding scale assistance (see Financial Assistance section)

**If Not Good Fit**:

- Patient can try another therapist (Navigator helps‚Äîre-match to different provider)
- No penalty (first session still free‚Äîcan use benefit again with new therapist)

### Crisis Referrals (Urgent)

**Red Flags** (During Intake):

## Therapy Modalities

### Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

**How It Works**:

- **Premise**: Thoughts ‚Üí Feelings ‚Üí Behaviors (change negative thoughts ‚Üí improve mood, behavior)
- **Techniques**: Identify negative thoughts ("I'll never get better", "I'm a burden"), challenge them (evidence for/against), replace with realistic thoughts ("I'm managing my illness", "My family loves me")

**Applications in AATD**:

- **Depression**: Challenge hopelessness ("I have no future" ‚Üí "I can still have meaningful life with AATD")
- **Anxiety**: Challenge catastrophizing ("I'm going to die" ‚Üí "I have treatment, many AATD patients live long lives")
- **Non-adherence**: Identify barriers (e.g., "Infusions don't work" ‚Üí examine evidence, reframe)

**Evidence**: 60-70% of patients improve (gold standard for depression, anxiety)

---

### Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT)

**How It Works**:

- **Premise**: Accept what you can't control (having AATD, uncertainty), commit to values (what matters to you‚Äîfamily, career, hobbies)
- **Techniques**: Mindfulness (observe thoughts without judgment), defusion (distance from negative thoughts‚Äî"I'm having the thought that I'm dying" vs. "I'm dying"), values clarification (what do you want your life to be about?), committed action (take steps aligned with values)

**Applications in AATD**:

- **Grief**: Accept diagnosis (can't change it), focus on living according to values (be present parent, contribute to work, enjoy hobbies)
- **Uncertainty**: Accept unknown prognosis (can't predict future), reduce anxiety by focusing on present
- **Chronic pain/dyspnea**: Accept symptoms (can't eliminate shortness of breath), reduce suffering by not fighting it

**Evidence**: 30-40% improvement in quality of life (especially for chronic illness patients)

---

### Eye Movement Desensitization & Reprocessing (EMDR)

**How It Works**:

- **Premise**: Trauma memories "stuck" in brain (not processed), cause flashbacks, nightmares, hypervigilance
- **Technique**: Patient recalls trauma (e.g., ICU intubation, diagnosis moment), while therapist guides eye movements (follow finger side-to-side) ‚Üí brain reprocesses memory ‚Üí memory becomes less distressing

**Applications in AATD**:

- **Medical trauma**: ICU stays, near-death experiences, painful procedures (bronchoscopy, liver biopsy)
- **PTSD**: Flashbacks, nightmares, avoidance (of medical settings, doctors)

**Evidence**: 70-80% reduction in PTSD symptoms (8-12 sessions)

---

### Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

**How It Works**:

- **Premise**: Teach skills (distress tolerance, emotion regulation, interpersonal effectiveness, mindfulness)
- **Techniques**: Breathing exercises (calm anxiety), opposite action (if depressed, do activity even if don't feel like it), DEAR MAN (assertive communication with doctors, family)

**Applications in AATD**:

- **Emotion dysregulation**: Intense anger, sadness (roller coaster emotions)
- **Self-harm, suicidal ideation**: DBT originally for borderline personality disorder‚Äîeffective for high-risk patients
- **Interpersonal conflict**: Strained relationships with caregivers, doctors (teach assertive communication)

**Evidence**: 50-60% reduction in self-harm, suicidal behavior (for high-risk patients)

---

### Grief Counseling

**How It Works**:

- **Premise**: Chronic illness = loss (loss of health, identity, future plans, independence)
- **Techniques**: Normalize grief (it's OK to mourn old life), process emotions (anger, sadness, guilt), find meaning (redefine identity, create new goals)

**Applications in AATD**:

- **Diagnosis grief**: Mourning pre-AATD life ("I used to run marathons, now I can't walk upstairs")
- **Anticipatory grief**: Fear of death, disability (especially advanced disease patients)
- **Caregiver grief**: Watching loved one suffer, decline

**Evidence**: Grief-specific therapy reduces complicated grief by 50% (vs. no treatment)

## Financial Assistance

### First Session Free (All Patients)

**Benefit**: Foundation covers initial consultation ($150 value)

**Eligibility**: Any AATD patient or family member (no income restrictions‚Äîuniversal benefit)

**Rationale**: Remove barrier to starting therapy (many patients hesitate due to cost‚Äîfree first session = test without risk)

### Sliding Scale for Ongoing Therapy

**Who Qualifies**:

- Income < 200% FPL (~$60K for family of 4‚Äîworking class, lower-middle class)

**Benefit**:

- Foundation subsidizes therapy costs (covers portion of copay or full session fee)
- Patient pays reduced rate ($20-$50/session‚Äîvs. $150-$300 full price)

**Structure**:

| Income Level | Patient Pays    | Foundation Subsidizes |
| ------------ | --------------- | --------------------- |
| < 100% FPL   | $0-$20/session  | $130-$150/session     |
| 100-150% FPL | $30-$40/session | $110-$120/session     |
| 150-200% FPL | $50/session     | $100/session          |

**Duration**: Up to 20 sessions per year (covers acute treatment‚Äîmost depression/anxiety treatment is 12-16 sessions)

**Application**: Patient submits income verification (pay stubs, tax return) + insurance info (if any)
