## Service Components

### 1. Dedicated Personal Advocate

**Assignment**:

- Each concierge client assigned senior Patient Navigator (5+ years experience, deep AATD knowledge)
- Advocate-to-client ratio: 1:25 (low caseload‚Äîensures personalized attention)

**Advocate Responsibilities**:

- Serve as single point of contact (client calls/texts Advocate for any need)
- Know client's case intimately (medical history, preferences, goals, family situation)
- Anticipate needs (proactive‚Äîreach out before appointments, after test results, during transitions)
- Coordinate all aspects of care (see detailed services below)

**Relationship-Building**:

- Introductory meeting (60-90 min‚Äîin-person or video, get to know client, assess needs, create personalized care plan)
- Regular check-ins (monthly phone calls‚Äî"How are you feeling? Any concerns? Upcoming appointments?")
- Always available (respond to texts/calls within 1 hour during business hours, 4 hours nights/weekends)

### 3. Comprehensive Care Coordination

**Advocate Manages Entire Care Journey**:

**Appointment Scheduling**:

- Advocate schedules all appointments (pulmonologist, PCP, hepatologist, genetic counselor, etc.)
- Coordinates timing (cluster appointments on same day‚Äîreduce travel)
- Prepares for visits (sends pre-visit summary to doctor‚Äîrecent test results, medication changes, current concerns)

**Medical Records Management**:

- Advocate obtains all records (from all providers‚Äîhospital, PCP, specialists)
- Sends records ahead of appointments (so new doctor has complete history)
- Maintains centralized record (in Foundation Medical Records Portal‚Äîclient has access)

**Test Result Follow-Up**:

- Advocate tracks all pending tests (PFT, CT scan, labs)
- Calls client when results available (explains results, answers questions, schedules follow-up if needed)
- Ensures no results fall through cracks (common problem‚Äîdoctor orders test, forgets to follow up)

**Medication Management**:

- Advocate maintains medication list (current, updated after every visit)
- Coordinates refills (calls pharmacy, ensures prescriptions filled on time)
- Alerts client to potential drug interactions (if new med prescribed‚Äîchecks against current list)

**Provider Communication**:

- Advocate facilitates communication between providers (pulmonologist says "I want hepatologist's opinion on liver meds"‚ÄîAdvocate coordinates, summarizes, ensures both doctors aligned)

### 4. Insurance Advocacy (White-Glove)

**Advocate Handles All Insurance Issues**:

**Prior Authorizations**:

- Advocate submits (gathers documentation, completes forms, submits to insurance)
- Follows up (calls insurance weekly‚Äî"What's status? What else do you need?")
- **Result**: PAs approved faster (2-4 weeks vs. typical 6-12 weeks‚ÄîAdvocate knows how to navigate system)

**Appeals**:

- If insurance denies (augmentation therapy, oxygen, specialist visit), Advocate handles appeal
- Gathers evidence (peer-reviewed studies, doctor's letter, treatment guidelines)
- Writes appeal letter (compelling, medically sound)
- Submits + follows up (escalates to external review if needed)
- **Result**: Higher appeal success rate (80% vs. typical 50%‚ÄîAdvocate is expert)

**Out-of-Network Coverage**:

- If client wants to see specialist out-of-network (e.g., Mayo Clinic, but insurance is UHC‚ÄîMayo not in network), Advocate negotiates with insurance
- Advocate: "Patient needs Mayo's expertise (only 5 AATD experts in US)‚Äîwill you cover as in-network exception?"
- **Result**: Often successful (insurance agrees‚Äîsaves them money vs. denying, then paying for complications from suboptimal care)

**Claims Issues**:

- Advocate monitors claims (ensures insurance pays, resolves billing errors)
- Client never receives surprise bills (Advocate handles‚Äîdisputes, negotiates payment plans if needed)
