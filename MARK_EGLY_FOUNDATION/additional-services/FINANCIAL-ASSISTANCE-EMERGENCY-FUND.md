## Program Overview

### What We Offer

**Mark Egly Foundation Financial Assistance Emergency Fund** provides **one-time grants** to AATD patients experiencing financial crisis:

**Services Include**:

- ‚úÖ **Medical Expense Grants**: Help pay for AATD-related medical bills (testing, treatment, specialist visits, procedures)
- ‚úÖ **Medication Copay Assistance**: Cover copays for augmentation therapy, oxygen, inhalers, other AATD medications
- ‚úÖ **Medical Equipment Grants**: Purchase or rent medical equipment (oxygen concentrators, nebulizers, CPAP machines)
- ‚úÖ **Medical Travel Grants**: Transportation to specialists, transplant centers (flights, gas, hotels)
- ‚úÖ **Utility Assistance**: Help pay electric bills (for patients on home oxygen‚Äîhigh electricity costs)
- ‚úÖ **Emergency Housing**: Temporary housing for transplant patients (must relocate near hospital)
- ‚úÖ **Fast Approval**: 3-5 business days (for emergencies‚Äî24-48 hours)

**Who Can Apply**:

- AATD patients (diagnosed or suspected‚Äîgenetic testing pending)
- Family members (parents of pediatric AATD patients, caregivers)
- Must demonstrate financial need (income < 300% federal poverty level)
- US residents only (Year 1‚Äîmay expand internationally in future)
- **No cost** to apply

## Why Financial Assistance Matters

### Financial Burden of AATD

**Statistics**:

- **Average annual AATD healthcare costs**: $50K-$150K (augmentation therapy, oxygen, medications, doctor visits, procedures)
- **Out-of-pocket costs**: $5K-$15K/year (after insurance‚Äîcopays, deductibles, coinsurance)
- **Lost income**: $20K-$40K/year (due to disability, reduced work hours, missed work for medical appointments)
- **Total financial burden**: $25K-$55K/year (out-of-pocket costs + lost income)

**Financial Hardship Prevalence**:

- **40%** of AATD patients report financial hardship (difficulty paying medical bills)
- **25%** skip or delay medical care (due to cost)
- **20%** have medical debt (collections, bankruptcy)
- **60%** worry about affording future care (even if currently managing)

---

### Consequences of Financial Hardship

**Medical Consequences**:

- ‚ùå **Delayed Diagnosis**: Can't afford genetic testing ($200-$500 out-of-pocket if insurance denies)
- ‚ùå **Non-Adherence**: Skip augmentation therapy infusions (copays $50-$500 per infusion √ó 52 weeks = $2,600-$26K/year)
- ‚ùå **Disease Progression**: Skipping treatment ‚Üí faster lung decline ‚Üí hospitalization, transplant, death
- ‚ùå **Emergency Care**: Delay preventive care (can't afford specialist) ‚Üí end up in ER (more expensive)

**Financial Consequences**:

- ‚ùå **Medical Debt**: Bills go to collections (credit score damage)
- ‚ùå **Bankruptcy**: 60% of bankruptcies in US are due to medical debt
- ‚ùå **Housing Insecurity**: Can't pay rent/mortgage (medical bills take priority)
- ‚ùå **Food Insecurity**: Choose between groceries and medications

**Psychological Consequences**:

- ‚ùå **Stress**: Constant worry about money (worsens health)
- ‚ùå **Depression**: Hopelessness ("I can't afford to stay alive")
- ‚ùå **Family Strain**: Relationships suffer (financial stress)

## Eligible Expenses

### Category 1: AATD Testing & Diagnosis

**Covered Expenses**:

- ‚úÖ **AAT Level Testing**: Serum AAT test ($50-$150)
- ‚úÖ **Genetic Testing**: Genotype confirmation ($200-$500‚Äîif insurance denies)
- ‚úÖ **Phenotype Testing**: AAT protein phenotype analysis ($100-$300)
- ‚úÖ **Family Testing**: Test first-degree relatives ($200-$500 each‚Äîgenetic counseling recommends)
- ‚úÖ **Pulmonary Function Tests**: Spirometry, DLCO, lung volumes ($100-$500‚Äîif insurance denies)
- ‚úÖ **CT Scan**: Chest CT (detect emphysema‚Äî$500-$2,000 out-of-pocket if insurance denies)
- ‚úÖ **Liver Biopsy**: For suspected AATD liver disease ($1,000-$3,000 out-of-pocket)

**Grant Limits**: Up to $1,000 per patient (for testing/diagnosis)

---

### Category 2: Augmentation Therapy

**Covered Expenses**:

- ‚úÖ **Infusion Copays**: Weekly augmentation therapy copays ($50-$500/infusion)
- ‚úÖ **Infusion Center Fees**: Facility fees (if charged separately‚Äî$50-$200/infusion)
- ‚úÖ **Supplies**: Infusion supplies (if not covered‚ÄîIV poles, tubing, etc.‚Äî$50-$100/month)
- ‚úÖ **Home Infusion Setup**: Initial setup costs for home infusion ($200-$500)

**Grant Limits**: Up to $2,000 per patient per year (for augmentation therapy copays)

**Special Consideration**: If patient's insurance denied augmentation therapy (appeal in progress), Foundation may cover out-of-pocket costs during appeal period (up to 3 months‚Äîmax $12,000)

---

### Category 3: Oxygen & Respiratory Equipment

**Covered Expenses**:

- ‚úÖ **Oxygen Concentrator**: Purchase or rental (portable or home‚Äî$500-$3,000)
- ‚úÖ **Oxygen Supplies**: Cannulas, tubing, masks ($50-$200/month)
- ‚úÖ **Nebulizer**: Purchase ($50-$200)
- ‚úÖ **CPAP/BiPAP Machine**: For sleep apnea (common in AATD‚Äî$500-$2,000)
- ‚úÖ **Pulse Oximeter**: Home monitoring ($30-$100)
- ‚úÖ **Spirometer**: Home lung function monitoring ($200-$500)

**Grant Limits**: Up to $3,000 per patient (for equipment‚Äîone-time)

---

### Category 4: Medications

**Covered Expenses**:

- ‚úÖ **Bronchodilators**: Inhalers (albuterol, ipratropium, etc.‚Äî$30-$100/month)
- ‚úÖ **Inhaled Corticosteroids**: Symbicort, Advair, etc. ($50-$300/month)
- ‚úÖ **Antibiotics**: For lung infections ($20-$200 per course)
- ‚úÖ **Immunosuppressants**: Post-transplant medications ($500-$2,000/month‚Äîif copays unaffordable)
- ‚úÖ **Pain Medications**: For chronic pain related to AATD
- ‚úÖ **Mental Health Medications**: Antidepressants, anti-anxiety meds (AATD-related mental health)

**Grant Limits**: Up to $1,000 per patient per year (for medication copays)

**Note**: Foundation encourages patients to apply for manufacturer copay assistance programs first (Grifols, CSL Behring, etc.)‚ÄîFoundation fills gaps if those programs insufficient

---

### Category 5: Medical Travel

**Covered Expenses**:

- ‚úÖ **Transportation**: Gas, flights, train, bus (to specialist, transplant center, AATD center)
- ‚úÖ **Lodging**: Hotel near hospital ($100-$150/night‚Äîup to 7 nights)
- ‚úÖ **Meals**: Per diem for medical travel ($30-$50/day)
- ‚úÖ **Parking**: Hospital parking fees ($10-$30/day)
- ‚úÖ **Tolls**: Road tolls en route to medical facility

**Grant Limits**: Up to $2,500 per patient per year (for medical travel)

**Priority**: Patients traveling for transplant evaluation/surgery, clinical trials, AATD centers of excellence (National Jewish Health, Cleveland Clinic, etc.)

---

### Category 6: Utility Assistance (Oxygen Users)

**Problem**: Home oxygen concentrators use significant electricity (increase electric bill by $50-$150/month)

**Covered Expenses**:

- ‚úÖ **Electric Bill Copay**: Portion of electric bill attributable to oxygen concentrator ($50-$150/month)

**Grant Limits**: Up to $600 per patient per year ($50/month √ó 12 months)

**Eligibility**: Must be on home oxygen 24/7 (prescription required) + demonstrate financial hardship (electric bill shutoff notice, past due balance)

---

### Category 7: Transplant-Related Expenses

**Covered Expenses**:

- ‚úÖ **Caregiver Travel**: Airfare, hotel for family member accompanying transplant patient ($1,000-$2,500)
- ‚úÖ **Temporary Housing**: Extended-stay hotel near transplant center (6-12 weeks pre/post-transplant‚Äî$100-$150/night)
- ‚úÖ **Relocation Assistance**: Moving costs if patient must permanently relocate near transplant center ($500-$2,000)
- ‚úÖ **Post-Transplant Medications**: Immunosuppressants (if unaffordable‚Äîup to 6 months post-transplant)

**Grant Limits**: Up to $5,000 per patient (for transplant-related expenses‚Äîone-time)

**Priority**: Highest priority (transplant is life-saving‚ÄîFoundation ensures financial barriers don't prevent transplant)

---

### Category 8: Emergency Medical Bills

**Covered Expenses**:

- ‚úÖ **ER Visits**: Emergency room copays ($100-$1,000)
- ‚úÖ **Hospitalizations**: Hospital copays, deductibles ($500-$5,000)
- ‚úÖ **Urgent Procedures**: Bronchoscopy, thoracentesis, etc. ($500-$2,000)
- ‚úÖ **Specialist Copays**: Pulmonologist, hepatologist, transplant surgeon ($50-$300/visit)

**Grant Limits**: Up to $2,000 per patient per year (for emergency medical bills)

**Note**: Foundation considers grants for medical debt in collections (if debt prevents patient from accessing care‚Äîe.g., hospital won't schedule elective procedure until outstanding balance paid)

## Application Process

### How to Apply

**Step 1: Submit Application** (Online or Phone):

**Online**:

- Go to MarkEglyFoundation.org/EmergencyFund
- Fill out application form (15-20 min)
- Upload documentation (see below)

**Phone**:

- Call 1-800-AATD-FUND
- Patient Navigator assists with application (phone interview‚Äî20-30 min)
- Email or mail documentation

**Paper Application**:

- Download PDF application (mail or fax to Foundation)
- For patients without internet access

---

### Required Documentation

**1. Proof of AATD Diagnosis**:

- AAT level test results (< 100 mg/dL)
- Genetic test results (genotype‚ÄîZZ, SZ, MZ, etc.)
- Physician's letter (confirming AATD diagnosis or clinical suspicion)
- **Exception**: If applying for genetic testing grant (diagnosis pending), provide low AAT level or physician's referral for testing

**2. Proof of Financial Need**:

- Recent tax return (most recent year) OR
- Recent pay stubs (last 2 months) OR
- Letter from employer (stating income) OR
- Unemployment benefits statement OR
- Disability benefits statement (Social Security, long-term disability)
- **Income Threshold**: < 300% federal poverty level (~$90,000 for family of 4)

**3. Proof of Expense**:

- Medical bill (itemized‚Äîshowing AATD-related charges)
- Prescription (for medication copays‚Äîshowing medication name, dose, copay amount)
- Equipment quote (for oxygen concentrator, nebulizer, etc.‚Äîfrom DME supplier)
- Airline/hotel reservation (for medical travel‚Äîshowing dates, destination, cost)
- Utility bill (for oxygen electricity assistance‚Äîshowing past due balance or shutoff notice)

**4. Insurance Information** (if applicable):

- Insurance card (front/back‚Äîshowing coverage, copays)
- Explanation of Benefits (EOB‚Äîshowing what insurance covered, patient responsibility)
- Denial letter (if insurance denied coverage)

**5. Personal Statement** (Optional but Encouraged):

- 1-2 paragraphs: How will this grant help you? What is your current financial situation? How is AATD impacting your life?
- Helps Foundation understand patient's story (may influence approval, grant amount)

---

### Application Timeline

**Standard Processing**:

- **Submit Application**: Day 1

## Eligibility Criteria

### Who Is Eligible

**Medical Eligibility**:

- ‚úÖ Diagnosed with AATD (confirmed by genetic testing or AAT level < 100 mg/dL)
- ‚úÖ OR Suspected AATD (low AAT level, clinical symptoms‚Äîgenetic testing pending)
- ‚úÖ OR Family member (parent of pediatric AATD patient, caregiver)

**Financial Eligibility**:

- ‚úÖ Household income < 300% federal poverty level:
  - 1 person: < $43,740/year
  - 2 people: < $59,160/year
  - 3 people: < $74,580/year
  - 4 people: < $90,000/year
  - (Add $15,420 for each additional person)

**Residency Eligibility**:

- ‚úÖ US resident (any state, including US territories‚ÄîPuerto Rico, US Virgin Islands, Guam, etc.)
- ‚ùå International patients (not eligible Year 1‚Äîmay expand in future)

**Other Eligibility Requirements**:

- ‚úÖ Expense is AATD-related (medical, travel, utility for oxygen, etc.)
- ‚úÖ Expense is NOT covered by insurance OR patient can't afford copay/deductible
- ‚úÖ Patient explored other assistance options (manufacturer copay programs, hospital financial assistance, Medicaid, etc.)‚ÄîFoundation is last resort
- ‚úÖ Patient has not exceeded annual/lifetime grant limits ($5K/year, $10K lifetime)

---

### Who Is NOT Eligible

**‚ùå Income > 300% federal poverty level**:

- Exception: Extraordinary circumstances (e.g., medical bankruptcy, job loss, recent diagnosis with high medical debt)

**‚ùå Expense is NOT AATD-related**:

- Foundation only covers AATD-related expenses (not general living expenses, non-AATD medical bills)

**‚ùå Patient has not explored other assistance**:

- Must apply to manufacturer copay programs (Grifols, CSL Behring‚Äîfree programs for augmentation therapy copays)
- Must apply to hospital financial assistance (many hospitals offer charity care)
- If eligible for Medicaid, must apply (Foundation can help with application)

**‚ùå Expense already covered**:

- If insurance already paid, Foundation doesn't cover (no double-dipping)

## Review & Approval

### Review Process

**Step 1: Application Screened** (Patient Navigator):

- Check completeness (all required documents submitted?)
- Verify AATD diagnosis (confirmed or suspected?)
- Verify financial need (income < 300% FPL?)
- Calculate grant amount (based on expense, need, available funds)

**Step 2: Application Reviewed** (Emergency Fund Committee):

- **Committee**: 3-5 members (Chief Patient Services Officer, Patient Navigator, Board Member, Volunteer)
- **Frequency**: Weekly meetings (or as-needed for expedited applications)
- **Review Criteria**:
  - Eligibility (meets all criteria?)
  - Need (genuine financial hardship?)
  - Impact (will grant make meaningful difference?)
  - Feasibility (is expense reasonable, appropriate?)
- **Vote**: Approve, deny, or request more information

**Step 3: Notification**:

- Approved: Patient notified via email or phone (same day as decision)
- Denied: Patient notified + explanation (e.g., "Income exceeds threshold," "Expense not AATD-related," "Other assistance available")
- More Info Needed: Patient Navigator contacts patient (request additional documentation)

---

### Disbursement Methods

**Option 1: Check (Mailed)**:

- Payable to patient (or vendor‚Äîe.g., oxygen supplier, hospital)
- Mailed within 1-2 business days of approval
- Delivery: 5-7 days (US Postal Service)

**Option 2: Direct Deposit** (Fastest):

- Funds deposited directly into patient's bank account
- Within 1-2 business days of approval
- Requires patient to provide bank account info (routing number, account number)

**Option 3: Direct Payment to Vendor**:

- Foundation pays vendor directly (e.g., pay electric company, oxygen supplier, hospital)
- Patient provides vendor contact info (account number, invoice #)
- Within 2-3 business days of approval

**Patient Preference**: Patient chooses disbursement method on application
