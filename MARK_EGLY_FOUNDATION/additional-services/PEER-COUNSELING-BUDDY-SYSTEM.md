## Program Overview

### What We Offer

**Mark Egly Foundation Peer Counseling & Buddy System** connects newly diagnosed AATD patients with experienced patients who can provide:

**Services Include**:

- ‚úÖ **1:1 Peer Matching**: Pair new patients with "veteran" AATD patients (similar age, disease stage, location)
- ‚úÖ **Emotional Support**: Someone who understands (lived experience with AATD)
- ‚úÖ **Practical Guidance**: Navigation (doctors, treatments, insurance, lifestyle)
- ‚úÖ **Regular Check-Ins**: Weekly calls, texts, or meetings (first 3 months)

- **Family Members**: Parents, spouses, caregivers (separate buddy matching)
- **No cost** (100% donor-funded)

## Buddy System Structure

### How It Works

**Step 1: New Patient Enrolls**:

- Fills out intake form (demographics, disease stage, interests, needs)
- Consent form (privacy, expectations, boundaries)

**Step 2: Matching** (within 7 days):

- Foundation matches new patient with veteran peer counselor
- Matching criteria:
  - **Disease stage** (similar lung function, liver involvement)
  - **Age** (within 10-15 years)
  - **Gender** (patient preference)
  - **Geographic location** (same state/region preferred‚Äîeasier to meet in person)
  - **Interests** (golf, hiking, parenting, career, etc.)
  - **Language** (for non-English speakers)

**Step 3: Introduction Call** (facilitated by Foundation):

- Program Coordinator calls both parties (3-way call)
- Introductions, set expectations, exchange contact info
- Schedule first 1:1 conversation

**Step 4: Regular Contact** (first 3 months):

- **Week 1-4**: Weekly calls or texts (30-60 min)
- **Month 2-3**: Bi-weekly check-ins
- **Month 4+**: As-needed contact (relationship becomes self-sustaining)

**Step 5: Ongoing Relationship**:

- Buddies often become long-term friends (continue relationship independently)
- Optional: Join peer support group together
- Annual check-in (Foundation surveys both parties‚Äîprogram quality)

---

### Peer Counselor Role

**What Peer Counselors Do**:

- ‚úÖ **Listen**: Active listening (validate feelings, empathize)
- ‚úÖ **Share**: Personal AATD story (diagnosis, treatment, coping)
- ‚úÖ **Guide**: Practical advice (doctors, insurance, lifestyle)
- ‚úÖ **Encourage**: Hope + empowerment ("You've got this")
- ‚úÖ **Connect**: Introduce to resources (Foundation programs, support groups, specialists)
- ‚úÖ **Check In**: Regular contact (weekly ‚Üí monthly ‚Üí as-needed)

**What Peer Counselors DON'T Do**:

- ‚ùå **Medical Advice**: Not doctors‚Äîcan share personal experience, but not medical advice
- ‚ùå **Therapy**: Not therapists‚Äîrefer to mental health professionals if needed
- ‚ùå **Emergency Response**: Not crisis counselors‚Äîrefer to crisis hotline (988, Foundation hotline)
- ‚ùå **Solve Problems**: Empower patient to solve own problems (not do it for them)

**Time Commitment**:

- **Training**: 8 hours (one-time)
- **Active Matching**: 2-4 hours/week (first 3 months per match)
- **Ongoing**: 1-2 hours/month (after initial period)
- **Maximum**: 2-3 active matches at a time (prevent burnout)

## Peer Counselor Training

### Training Curriculum (8 Hours)

**Module 1: AATD 101** (1 hour):

- Genetics, inheritance, genotypes
- Lung disease (emphysema, COPD)
- Liver disease (cirrhosis, hepatitis)
- Treatment options (augmentation therapy, transplant)
- Research updates (gene therapy, new therapies)

**Module 2: Active Listening Skills** (2 hours):

- Reflective listening (paraphrase, validate)
- Open-ended questions ("How are you feeling?" vs. "Are you okay?")
- Empathy vs. sympathy
- Non-judgmental stance
- Cultural competency (LGBTQ+, racial/ethnic minorities, language barriers)

**Module 3: Boundaries & Ethics** (1 hour):

- Confidentiality (what stays private, what to report)
- Boundaries (friendship vs. counseling‚Äîknow limits)
- Mandatory reporting (suicide risk, abuse)
- Self-care (avoid burnout, know when to step back)

**Module 4: Crisis Management** (1 hour):

- Recognizing crisis (suicide ideation, severe depression, panic)
- Crisis response ("I hear you're struggling. I'm here. Let's get you help.")
- Referrals (crisis hotline, 988, therapist, Foundation staff)
- Follow-up (check in after crisis)

**Module 5: Resource Navigation** (1 hour):

- Foundation programs (patient assistance, insurance appeals, research)
- External resources (AlphaNet, Alpha-1 Foundation, patient organizations)
- Finding specialists (AATD centers, top pulmonologists)
- Insurance navigation (tips for fighting denials)

**Module 6: Peer Counseling Best Practices** (1 hour):

- Building rapport (first call tips)
- Pacing (don't overwhelm new patients with info)
- Empowerment (help patients find their own answers)
- Celebrating wins (treatment milestones, advocacy victories)

**Module 7: Role-Playing** (1 hour):

- Practice scenarios (first call, crisis call, insurance question, family guilt)
- Feedback (trainers observe, provide constructive feedback)

**Certification**:

- Complete 8-hour training
- Pass quiz (80%+ score)
- Shadowing (observe 1-2 calls with experienced peer counselor)
- Receive certificate ("Mark Egly Foundation Certified Peer Counselor")

## Matching Process

### Intake Form (New Patients)

**Questions**:

1. **Demographics**: Age, gender, location (state/city)
2. **AATD Info**: Genotype (ZZ, SZ, etc.), AAT level, lung function (FEV1%), liver involvement, date of diagnosis
3. **Treatment**: Current treatments (augmentation therapy, oxygen, transplant list, etc.)
4. **Support Needs**: What are you looking for? (emotional support, practical advice, insurance help, family planning, etc.)
5. **Preferences**: Gender preference for buddy? Language? Interests/hobbies?
6. **Contact**: Phone, email, preferred contact method, best times

---

### Matching Algorithm

**Priority Factors**:

1. **Disease Stage** (most important‚Äîmatch similar lung/liver disease severity)
2. **Age** (within 10-15 years)
3. **Geographic Location** (same state/region‚Äîeasier to meet in person)
4. **Gender** (if patient has preference)
5. **Interests** (shared hobbies, life stage‚Äîe.g., young parents, retirees, golfers)

**Example Match**:

- **New Patient**: 45-year-old male, ZZ genotype, FEV1 55%, on augmentation therapy, Texas, married with young kids, golfer
- **Peer Counselor Match**: 50-year-old male, ZZ genotype, FEV1 50%, on augmentation therapy 5 years, Texas, married with teenage kids, avid golfer

**Why It Works**: Shared experience (similar disease, life stage, interests) ‚Üí instant rapport

---

### Matching Timeline

**Goal**: Match within 7 days of enrollment

**Process**:

1. **Day 1**: New patient enrolls (intake form)
2. **Day 2-3**: Program Coordinator reviews intake, searches database for matches
3. **Day 4-5**: Contact potential peer counselors (confirm availability)
4. **Day 6**: Make match, notify both parties
5. **Day 7**: Introduction call (3-way)

**If No Match Available**:

## Crisis Hotline

### 24/7 AATD Crisis Hotline

**Purpose**: Immediate support for patients in emotional crisis

**Who Should Call**:

- Suicidal thoughts or self-harm ideation
- Severe depression (can't get out of bed, hopeless)
- Panic attacks (difficulty breathing‚Äînot medical emergency, but panic)
- Overwhelming anxiety (new diagnosis, test results, insurance denial)
- Grief (disease progression, end-of-life concerns)
- Family crisis (genetic guilt, relationship strain)

**What Callers Get**:

- **Trained Crisis Counselor**: AATD peer counselor with crisis training OR licensed mental health professional (volunteer)
- **Immediate Support**: Active listening, validation, safety planning
- **Referrals**: Connect to therapist, psychiatrist, emergency services (if needed)
- **Follow-Up**: Wellness check (24 hours after crisis call)

---

### Crisis Hotline Logistics

**Phone Number**: 1-800-AATD-911 (24/7, toll-free)

**Staffing Model** (Year 1):

- **Volunteer-based**: Trained peer counselors + licensed therapists (volunteer shifts)
- **Shift Structure**: 4-hour shifts, 6 shifts/day (cover 24 hours)
- **Backup**: Professional crisis hotline service (contract for overflow‚Äîe.g., National Suicide Prevention Lifeline partner)

**Staffing Model** (Year 3+):

- **Paid staff**: 2 FTE crisis counselors (licensed therapists)
- **Volunteer supplement**: Peer counselors for non-crisis calls (check-ins, questions)

**Training**:

- **Crisis Counseling Certification**: 40-hour training (ASIST, safeTALK, or similar)
- **AATD-Specific**: 4-hour module (AATD + mental health‚Äîgenetic guilt, chronic illness adjustment)

**Technology**:

- **Call routing**: Twilio or similar (route calls to on-call counselor)
- **CRM integration**: Log calls (anonymized data‚Äîcall volume, crisis types, outcomes)

**Cost** (Year 1):

- Volunteer-based (minimal cost‚Äîphone system $2K/year, backup service $5K/year)
- Total: ~$10K/year

---

### Crisis Protocols

**Level 1: Low Risk** (Caller distressed, but safe):

- Active listening, validation
- Coping strategies (breathing exercises, grounding techniques)
- Resource referrals (therapist, support group)
- Follow-up call (24 hours)

**Level 2: Moderate Risk** (Caller has suicidal thoughts, but no immediate plan):

- All Level 1 interventions, plus:
- Safety plan (remove means, identify supports, crisis contacts)
- Encourage caller to contact therapist/psychiatrist (offer to call with them)
- Follow-up call (12 hours, then daily for 1 week)

**Level 3: High Risk** (Caller has suicide plan, imminent danger):

- **Stay on the line** (do not hang up)
- **Call 911** (use second phone line‚Äîdispatch emergency services to caller's location)
- **Keep caller talking** (until help arrives)
- **Document** (crisis report‚Äîshare with Program Coordinator, follow-up team)
