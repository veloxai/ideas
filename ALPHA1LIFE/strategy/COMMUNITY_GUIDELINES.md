## üíô Community Values

### 1. **Compassion First**

We approach every interaction with kindness and empathy. Everyone here is dealing with a chronic condition, and we honor that struggle.

### 2. **Respect Diversity**

Our community includes people of all ages, backgrounds, beliefs, and experiences with AATD. We celebrate this diversity.

### 3. **Support, Not Judge**

We share our experiences to help others, not to criticize their choices. Every person's journey with AATD is unique.

### 4. **Evidence-Based Information**

We value science and medicine while respecting personal experiences. We distinguish between medical facts and personal opinions.

### 5. **Privacy Protection**

What's shared in our community stays in our community. We protect each other's privacy and anonymity.

### 6. **Empowerment**

We help each other become informed, confident advocates for our own health.

## üìú Community Rules

### ‚úÖ DO:

**Be Kind and Supportive**

- Offer encouragement and understanding
- Celebrate others' victories (big and small)
- Acknowledge struggles without minimizing them
- Use "I" statements: "I felt..." not "You should feel..."

**Share Your Experience**

- Your story matters and can help others
- Be honest about challenges and victories
- Share what works for you
- Acknowledge that everyone's experience is different

**Ask Questions**

- There are no "stupid" questions
- Search first to see if your question has been answered
- Provide context to help others understand
- Follow up and share what you learned

**Give Credit**

- If you share information from elsewhere, cite your source
- Tag other members when relevant
- Thank those who help you

**Report Problems**

- Use the "Report" button for guideline violations
- Contact moderators directly for urgent concerns
- Help us keep the community safe

---

### ‚ùå DON'T:

**Do Not Give Medical Advice**

- You may share your personal experience: "My doctor prescribed X and it helped me because..."
- You may NOT tell someone what they should do medically: "You should ask your doctor about X" is okay, "You should take X" is not
- Always include: "I'm not a doctor, but..."
- Encourage consulting with healthcare providers

**Do Not Promote Unproven Treatments**

- No multi-level marketing (MLM) products
- No "miracle cures" or supplements without scientific backing
- No alternative therapies as replacements for proven treatments
- You may discuss complementary therapies (meditation, diet, exercise) if you label them as such

**Do Not Shame or Judge**

- Don't criticize treatment choices
- Don't judge lifestyle decisions
- Don't shame people for their emotions
- Don't gatekeep ("You don't have it as bad as...")

**Do Not Share Others' Information**

- Don't screenshot and share conversations
- Don't mention other members' real names without permission
- Don't share photos of others
- Don't reveal information shared in private messages

**Do Not Use Discriminatory Language**

- No racism, sexism, homophobia, transphobia, ableism
- No religious discrimination
- No political attacks
- No hate speech of any kind

**Do Not Advertise or Spam**

- No unsolicited business promotion
- No fundraising for non-AATD causes without moderator approval
- No repetitive posting
- No posting solely to drive traffic elsewhere

**Do Not Share Graphic Medical Content Without Warning**

- Use content warnings for: surgical photos, graphic descriptions of symptoms, end-of-life discussions
- Mark posts appropriately so members can choose whether to view

## üí¨ What to Share (and What Not to Share)

### ‚úÖ Great Things to Share:

**Personal Experiences**:

- Your diagnosis story
- Treatment experiences (what worked, what didn't)
- Side effects you've experienced
- Coping strategies that help you
- Lifestyle changes that made a difference
- Finding a great specialist
- Insurance tips and tricks
- Emotional struggles and victories

**Questions**:

- Anything about living with AATD
- Seeking recommendations (doctors, resources, products)
- Understanding test results (with identifying info removed)
- Treatment options you're considering
- How to talk to family about AATD
- Workplace accommodations

**Support**:

- Encouragement for others
- Celebrating milestones
- Offering a listening ear
- Sharing helpful resources
- Organizing local meetups (with moderator approval)

**Resources**:

- Articles about AATD research (with links)
- Helpful tools or apps
- Patient assistance programs
- Upcoming events or webinars
- Book or podcast recommendations

---

### ‚ö†Ô∏è Things to Share Carefully:

**Medical Information**:

- Always clarify it's your experience, not medical advice
- Include disclaimers
- Encourage consulting healthcare providers
- Be specific about your genotype/phenotype if relevant (treatment varies)

**Controversial Topics**:

- Politics: Only as directly related to AATD (healthcare policy, research funding)
- Religion: Welcome to share if it helps your coping, but respect others' beliefs
- Alternative medicine: Okay to discuss, but note when not scientifically proven

**Emotional Content**:

- End-of-life discussions: Use content warnings
- Suicide or self-harm: Please use crisis resources (see below), report to moderators
- Severe depression/anxiety: Encourage professional help alongside community support

---

### ‚ùå Do NOT Share:

**Personal Identifying Information** (yours or others'):

- Full names (unless you explicitly choose to)
- Addresses or specific locations
- Phone numbers or email addresses
- Photos with faces (without consent)
- Medical record numbers or insurance info

**Graphic Medical Content** (without warnings):

- Surgical photos
- Photos of severe symptoms
- Detailed descriptions of bodily functions
- End-of-life details

**Harmful Content**:

- Encouragement to avoid medical care
- Conspiracy theories about pharmaceutical companies
- Anti-vaccine rhetoric (augmentation therapy is a medical treatment)
- Promotion of eating disorders or self-harm
- Bullying or harassment

**Illegal Content**:

- Drug seeking behavior
- Sharing prescriptions
- Encouraging illegal activities

## üëÆ Moderation Policies

### Our Moderation Team

**Who They Are**:

- Volunteer AATD patients trained in community moderation
- Mark Egly Foundation staff
- Medical advisor (reviews flagged medical content)

**What They Do**:

- Monitor forums for guideline violations
- Remove inappropriate content
- Issue warnings or suspensions
- Answer questions about guidelines
- Support members in distress
- Facilitate productive discussions

**What They DON'T Do**:

- Provide medical advice
- Take sides in disagreements
- Share private information
- Make decisions based on personal biases

---

### Moderation Process

**Level 1: Automated Filters**

- Catch spam, profanity, known harmful content
- Flag posts for human review

**Level 2: Community Reports**

- Members report concerning content
- Moderators review within 24 hours
- Action taken if guidelines violated

**Level 3: Proactive Monitoring**

- Moderators regularly review posts
- Look for subtle violations
- Intervene in escalating conflicts

---

### Moderator Actions

**Content Removal**:

- Spam: Removed immediately, no warning
- Guideline violation: Removed, warning issued
- Harmful content: Removed immediately, account may be suspended

**Warnings**:

- First offense (minor): Friendly reminder via PM
- Second offense: Official warning on record
- Third offense: Temporary suspension
- Fourth offense: Permanent ban

**Suspensions**:

- 24-hour suspension: Cooling off period
- 7-day suspension: Serious violation
- 30-day suspension: Repeated violations
- Permanent ban: Severe or repeated violations

**Appeals**:

- You may appeal any moderator decision
- Email: community@alpha1life.com
- Include your username and post in question
- Another moderator will review
- Decision within 7 days

## üè• Special Considerations for Health Information

### Medical Disclaimers

**Every member should understand**:

- This is a peer support community, not medical care
- Information shared is based on personal experience, not medical expertise
- Always consult your healthcare provider before making medical decisions
- We are not responsible for outcomes based on information shared here

---

### Sharing Medical Information

**When sharing your experience**:

- Be specific about your situation (genotype, severity, other conditions)
- Note what your doctor told you
- Acknowledge that others' experiences may differ
- Use phrases like: "In my case..." "My doctor said..." "This is just my experience..."

**When asking for advice**:

- Don't ask "What should I do?"
- Instead ask: "Has anyone experienced this? What did your doctor say?"
- Don't expect diagnosis or treatment recommendations
- Do expect: support, shared experiences, suggestions for questions to ask your doctor

---

### Healthcare Provider Participation

**If you're a healthcare provider**:

- Identify yourself as HCP in profile
- Specify your specialty
- Note that you're providing education, not treatment
- Follow HIPAA and professional ethics guidelines
- Don't diagnose or prescribe
- Don't promote your practice

**Designated HCP areas**:

- "Ask a Professional" forum (verified HCPs only)
- Q&A format, moderated by medical advisor
- Providers answer general questions (not individual medical advice)

## üå± Building a Positive Community

### How to Be a Great Community Member

**1. Welcome Newcomers**

- Respond to "New Member Introductions"
- Share your story to help them feel less alone
- Point them to helpful resources

**2. Offer Support**

- Even a "thinking of you" comment matters
- Share what helped you in similar situations
- Listen without trying to "fix"

**3. Stay Positive (Even When It's Hard)**

- It's okay to share struggles
- But also share wins and hope
- Balance realism with optimism

**4. Educate Yourself**

- Learn about AATD from reputable sources
- Understand different genotypes and experiences
- Recognize your own biases

**5. Be Patient with Differences**

- Not everyone has the same resources
- Not everyone has the same support system
- Not everyone has the same health literacy
- Meet people where they are
