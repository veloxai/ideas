## ðŸ”§ Beta Testing Focus Areas

### 1. Technical Functionality

**Test Cases**:

- âœ… Registration and login work on all devices
- âœ… Forum posting, replying, editing work
- âœ… Anonymous posting functions properly
- âœ… Search finds relevant content
- âœ… Specialist directory filters work
- âœ… Donation forms process payments (test mode)
- âœ… Email notifications send properly
- âœ… Password reset works
- âœ… Mobile responsiveness (all pages)
- âœ… Page load times <3 seconds

**Bug Reporting**:

- Beta testers complete bug report form:
  - What happened?
  - What were you trying to do?
  - What did you expect to happen?
  - Device and browser
  - Screenshot (if possible)
- Bugs triaged: Critical (launch blocker), High (fix before public launch), Medium (fix soon), Low (nice to have)

---

### 2. User Experience (UX)

**Evaluation Criteria**:

- **Navigation**: Can users find what they need within 2-3 clicks?
- **Clarity**: Is content language clear and accessible?
- **Visual Design**: Is design clean, modern, accessible?
- **Call-to-Actions**: Are CTAs clear ("Join Forum", "Read Stories", "Donate")?
- **Onboarding**: Do new users understand how to get started?

**UX Testing Activities**:

- **Task-Based Testing**: "Find information about augmentation therapy" (observe where they look)
- **5-Second Test**: Show homepage for 5 seconds, ask what they remember (first impressions)
- **Think-Aloud Protocol**: Ask 5-10 users to narrate their thought process while using site

---

### 3. Content Quality

**Test Areas**:

- **Accuracy**: Is medical information correct? (Physician review)
- **Clarity**: Is content understandable for all education levels?
- **Tone**: Is voice warm, hopeful, supportive (not clinical/scary)?
- **Completeness**: Is anything important missing?
- **Relevance**: Does content match user needs?

**Content Review Process**:

- Beta testers review 5-10 pages each
- Rate each page: Helpful (1-5), Clear (1-5), Accurate (1-5), Suggestions (open text)
- Medical advisory board reviews clinical content

---

### 4. Community Features

**Test Areas**:

- **Forum Engagement**: Do users feel comfortable posting?
- **Moderation**: Are guidelines clear? Do users follow them?
- **Privacy**: Do users understand anonymous posting option?
- **Helpfulness**: Are responses supportive and helpful?
- **Safety**: Any concerning posts or misinformation?

**Community Metrics**:

- Number of posts/replies
- Average response time to questions
- Sentiment analysis (positive/negative/neutral)
- Number of flagged posts (if any)
- User satisfaction with community

## ðŸ”„ Feedback Collection Methods

**Mid-Beta Survey** (April 9, after 1 week):

### 2. Beta Tester Forum

**Private Forum Section**:

- Dedicated "Beta Feedback" forum (not visible to public post-launch)
- Categories:
  - Bugs & Issues
  - Feature Requests
  - General Feedback
  - Praise & What's Working
- Encourages peer discussion (beta testers can build on each other's ideas)

---

### 3. Focus Groups

**Virtual Focus Groups** (Zoom, 60-90 minutes):

- 3 sessions with 5-7 participants each
- Segmented by persona (newly diagnosed, established, family)
- Semi-structured discussion guide
- Incentive: $25 Amazon gift card for participation
- Recorded for later review (with permission)

**Focus Group Questions**:

- What was your first impression of the site?
- What did you love? What frustrated you?
- If you could change one thing, what would it be?
- What would make you come back regularly?
- Would you recommend to a friend? Why or why not?

---

### 4. Direct Observation

**Usability Testing** (5-10 users):

- Screen share session (Zoom, 30 minutes)
- Give specific tasks: "Find information about genetic testing"
- Observe where they click, what they struggle with
- Think-aloud protocol ("Tell me what you're thinking")
- No incentive needed (part of beta commitment)

**Heatmaps** (Hotjar):

- Where users click
- How far they scroll
- What they ignore

## ðŸš¨ Risk Management

### Risk 1: Low Beta Tester Signup (<50)

**Mitigation**:

- Start recruitment 4 weeks before launch (not 2)
- Offer incentives (exclusive founding member status)
- Personal outreach to support group leaders
- Extend recruitment period if needed

**Contingency**:

- If <50, proceed with smaller group (30-40 is still valuable)
- Supplement with internal MEF staff/board testing

---

### Risk 2: Low Engagement (Activation <50%)

**Mitigation**:

- Engaging orientation sessions (not just "go test it")
- Daily prompts and check-ins (keep beta testers engaged)
- Gamification (most active tester gets recognition)

**Contingency**:

- Extend beta period (3 weeks instead of 2)
- Re-engage inactive testers (personal phone call or email)
- Focus on engaged subset for deeper feedback

---

### Risk 3: Critical Bugs Discovered Late

**Mitigation**:

- Extensive pre-beta QA testing
- Test on multiple devices, browsers, screen sizes
- Have developer on-call during beta week 1

**Contingency**:

- If critical bug found Week 2, delay public launch by 2-4 weeks
- Communicate transparently with beta testers
- Use time to fix + re-test thoroughly

---

### Risk 4: Negative Feedback or Concerns

**Mitigation**:

- Set expectations: "Beta means imperfect, we want honest feedback"
- Respond quickly to all concerns (acknowledge, explain, fix)
- Over-communicate: weekly update emails to beta testers

**Contingency**:

- If major concerns (privacy, safety, misinformation), address immediately
- Pause beta if needed to fix fundamental issues
- Re-launch beta after fixes (with same testers)

---

### Risk 5: Beta Testers Share Publicly (Pre-Launch Leak)

**Mitigation**:

- Ask beta testers to keep feedback within group (not NDA, just request)
- Emphasize exclusivity ("you get first look")
- Monitor social media for leaks

**Contingency**:

- If leaked, embrace it (share preview publicly)
- Accelerate public launch timeline if buzz is positive
