## üë§ Persona 1: Sarah - Newly Diagnosed Patient

### Profile

- **Age**: 42
- **Location**: Pittsburgh, PA
- **Occupation**: Elementary school teacher
- **Diagnosis**: 3 months ago (ZZ genotype)
- **Symptoms**: Shortness of breath, fatigue
- **Tech Savviness**: Medium
- **Emotional State**: Scared, overwhelmed, seeking answers

---

### Journey Stage 1: Pre-Awareness (Before Diagnosis)

**Timeline**: Months to years before diagnosis

**Experience**:

- Sarah has been experiencing shortness of breath for 2 years
- Initially attributed to asthma (misdiagnosed)
- Multiple doctor visits, various inhalers tried
- Symptoms worsen, impact daily life
- Finally referred to pulmonologist

**Touchpoints**: None yet (not aware of AATD or Alpha1Life)

**Emotions**: üòü Frustrated, worried, confused

---

### Journey Stage 2: Diagnosis (Day 0)

**Timeline**: The day Sarah learns she has AATD

**Experience**:

- Pulmonologist orders genetic test (blood draw)
- Results come back: ZZ genotype, Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency
- Doctor explains diagnosis (clinical terms, somewhat overwhelming)
- Given printouts and told to "research online"
- Goes home in shock

**Touchpoints**:

- Doctor's office (printout may or may not mention Alpha1Life)
- Google search begins that evening: "What is Alpha-1?"

**Emotions**: üò± Shocked, scared, overwhelmed

**Thoughts**: "What does this mean? Will I die? How do I tell my family?"

---

### Journey Stage 3: Discovery (Days 1-7)

**Timeline**: First week after diagnosis

**Experience**:

- **Day 1**: Google searches dominate ("Alpha-1 life expectancy", "Alpha-1 treatment", "Alpha-1 prognosis")
- **Day 2-3**: Finds Alpha-1 Foundation website (lots of medical info, feels clinical and overwhelming)
- **Day 3**: Joins Facebook group for AATD patients (sees mix of scary and hopeful stories)
- **Day 4**: **Finds Alpha1Life.com** (via Google search: "newly diagnosed Alpha-1 support")

**First Impression of Alpha1Life**:

- **Homepage**: Warm design, patient face smiling (not clinical), headline: "You're not alone with Alpha-1"
- **Relief**: "This feels different. This feels like it's for me."
- **First Click**: "What is AATD?" page (plain language, 6th grade reading level)

**Touchpoints**:

- **Google Search** ‚Üí **Alpha1Life.com homepage**
- **What is AATD page** ‚Üí Video explainer (2 minutes)
- **Patient Stories** ‚Üí Watches Jennifer's story (sees hope)

**Emotions**: üòå Relief, cautious hope, still scared but less alone

**Actions**:

- Spends 15 minutes reading "What is AATD?" and "What to expect"
- Watches 3 patient story videos
- Bookmarks site, doesn't sign up yet (not ready)

---

### Journey Stage 4: Engagement (Weeks 2-4)

**Timeline**: Sarah returns to site multiple times

**Week 2 Experience**:

- Returns to Alpha1Life.com 3-4 times to read more
- **Trigger**: Gets call from pulmonologist about starting augmentation therapy ‚Üí searches "Alpha-1 augmentation therapy" ‚Üí finds Alpha1Life article
- **Deep Dive**: Reads treatment section (augmentation therapy, what to expect, side effects)
- **Emotional Connection**: Reads patient story from someone with similar symptoms ‚Üí feels understood

**Week 3 Experience**:

- Ready to ask questions, but scared to ask doctor (feels dumb)
- **Joins Forum**: Creates account as "SarahP" (not full name, wants privacy)
- **First Post**: "Just diagnosed, starting treatment soon, what should I know?"
- **Within 2 hours**: Gets 4 replies from other patients
- **Response**: "Thank you so much! This is exactly what I needed to hear."
- **Feels**: Connected, supported, less alone

**Week 4 Experience**:

- Now checking forum daily (becomes habit)
- Posts question about telling family
- Shares own experience to help another newly diagnosed member
- **Signs up for newsletter** (wants updates, but not overwhelming)

**Touchpoints**:

- **Google searches** ‚Üí Alpha1Life.com pages
- **Forum** (multiple visits)
- **Email welcome series** (5 emails over 2 weeks)
- **Newsletter signup**

**Emotions**: üòä Hopeful, supported, empowered

**Actions**:

- 10+ site visits
- Forum account created
- 3 forum posts, 5 replies
- Newsletter subscriber

## üó∫Ô∏è Journey Mapping Insights

### Key Friction Points to Remove

1. **Discovery**: Many patients find us late (weeks after diagnosis) ‚Üí Need better SEO, provider partnerships
2. **Forum Signup Hesitation**: Some patients afraid to post publicly ‚Üí Emphasize anonymous posting option
3. **Donation Ask Timing**: Asking too early feels transactional ‚Üí Wait until Month 3-6, after value delivered
4. **Mobile Experience**: 65% traffic is mobile ‚Üí Must optimize every page for smartphone

### Key Moments of Delight to Amplify

1. **First Forum Reply** (within 2 hours): Patients feel heard, supported ‚Üí Train moderators to respond quickly
2. **Personal Thank-You** (after donation): Unexpected personal touch builds loyalty ‚Üí Automate + personalize
3. **Story Published** (patient ambassadors): Pride and purpose ‚Üí Celebrate publicly, recognize contributors
4. **Someone They Helped** (long-term): Seeing impact of their advice ‚Üí Highlight success stories

### Optimal Touchpoint Sequence

**Newly Diagnosed Journey**:

1. Google search ‚Üí Homepage (hope, not clinical)
2. "What is AATD?" page (plain language, video)
3. Patient stories (emotional connection, hope)
4. Forum (community, support)
5. Newsletter signup (stay connected)
6. Virtual support group (deeper connection)
7. Donation ask (after value delivered, Month 3-6)
