## 5. Evaluation Framework Details

**Primary Outcome #1: Earlier Rare Disease Diagnosis**

**Data Collection Method**: Mixed methods (quantitative + qualitative)

1. **User-Reported Case Studies**
   - **Metric**: Number of documented earlier diagnoses
   - **Collection**: Online form (physician reports case where platform helped)
   - **Validation**: Review by Medical Advisory Board (confirm plausibility)
   - **Target**: 500+ Year 1, 5,000+ Year 2

2. **Partner Institution Diagnostic Rates**
   - **Metric**: % change in AATD testing rates before/after platform launch
   - **Collection**: Partner institutions share de-identified EHR data
   - **Analysis**: Interrupted time series analysis (control for temporal trends)
   - **Target**: 25% increase in testing rates

3. **Patient Survey**
   - **Metric**: Time from first symptom to diagnosis (self-reported by patients)
   - **Collection**: Survey via Alpha-1 Foundation (100+ patients/year)
   - **Analysis**: Compare diagnosed before/after platform launch
   - **Target**: Reduce diagnostic delay 50% (5.6 years → <3 years)

## 6. Sample Community Guidelines

**Uniting Doctors Community Guidelines**

_Effective: [Date] | Last Updated: [Date]_

**Our Mission**: Unite healthcare professionals globally to share knowledge, improve patient care, and support each other.

**Our Values**:

1. **Patient First**: Every discussion serves the goal of better patient care
2. **Evidence-Based**: Cite sources, not just opinions
3. **Respectful**: Professional disagreement is welcome; personal attacks are not
4. **Inclusive**: All healthcare roles valued; no hierarchy
5. **Privacy**: HIPAA compliance non-negotiable; protect patient information
6. **Integrity**: Disclose conflicts of interest; no spam or self-promotion

---

**Rules** (Violations may result in warnings, suspensions, or permanent bans):

1. **No Protected Health Information (PHI)**
   - Do NOT post patient names, dates of birth, medical record numbers, or identifiable details
   - De-identify all case discussions (change ages, genders, locations if rare case)
   - When in doubt, remove it

2. **Cite Your Sources**
   - Medical claims should include citations (PubMed links, textbook references)
   - Label evidence levels (A = strong RCT, B = weaker evidence, C = expert opinion)
   - "In my experience" is acceptable but distinguish from proven evidence

3. **Respectful Disagreement**
   - ✅ Acceptable: "Studies show a different approach. Here's the citation..."
   - ❌ Not Acceptable: "That's stupid. You don't know what you're talking about."
   - Attack ideas, not people

4. **No Spam or Self-Promotion**
   - Don't promote your practice, products, or services
   - Disclosing affiliations is acceptable ("I work for Company X") but no advertising

5. **No Discrimination or Harassment**
   - Zero tolerance for racism, sexism, homophobia, ableism, or other discrimination
   - Respect all healthcare roles (physician, nurse, PA, etc.)
   - No bullying or personal attacks

6. **Report, Don't Engage**
   - If you see rule violations, flag them (don't argue publicly)
   - Moderators review all flagged content within 24 hours

## 8. HIPAA Compliance Documentation

**HIPAA Compliance Overview for Uniting Doctors**

**Status**: Platform designed HIPAA-compliant from Day 1

**Key Compliance Elements**:

1. **Business Associate Agreements (BAAs)**
   - Signed with all vendors handling health data (AWS, SendGrid, etc.)
   - Users agree to Terms of Service (includes HIPAA responsibilities)

2. **Technical Safeguards**
   - Encryption at rest (AES-256 for databases)
   - Encryption in transit (TLS 1.3 for all connections)
   - Multi-factor authentication (required for all users)
   - Access controls (role-based, principle of least privilege)
   - Audit logs (track all data access, retained 7 years)

3. **Administrative Safeguards**
   - HIPAA training for all staff (annual requirement)
   - Security risk assessment (annual)
   - Incident response plan (documented, tested quarterly)
   - HIPAA compliance officer designated (staff member responsible)

4. **Physical Safeguards**
   - Cloud infrastructure (AWS HIPAA-eligible services)
   - No on-premise servers (reduces physical security risk)

5. **User Education**
   - Prominent warnings during case posting ("De-identify patient information")
   - HIPAA training module (required at registration)
   - Annual reminders (email campaign)

6. **PHI Detection & Removal**
   - AI scans posts for potential PHI (names, dates, MRNs)
   - Human moderators review flagged content
   - Rapid removal process (within 1 hour of detection)

**Breach Notification Plan**:

- If PHI breached, notify affected individuals within 60 days (HIPAA requirement)
- Notify HHS Office for Civil Rights (reportable breach)
- Incident report published (transparency)

## 9. Sample User Surveys

### Survey #1: Baseline Knowledge Assessment (Pre-Platform)

**Purpose**: Establish baseline knowledge of AATD among healthcare professionals

**Administered**: At user registration

1. Have you heard of Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency (AATD)? (Yes/No/Unsure)
2. Can you describe the main symptoms of AATD? (Open text)
3. Do you know what test is used to diagnose AATD? (Multiple choice: Genetic test / Blood test / Imaging / I don't know)
4. In what patient population would you consider testing for AATD? (Check all that apply: COPD patients / Liver disease patients / Family history / I would not test)
5. How confident are you in diagnosing rare diseases in your practice? (Scale 1-5)

---

### Survey #2: User Engagement & Satisfaction (Quarterly)

**Purpose**: Assess user satisfaction, gather feature feedback

**Administered**: Quarterly email to active users

1. How often do you use Uniting Doctors? (Daily / Weekly / Monthly / Rarely)
2. What features do you use most? (Check all: Browse discussions / Post questions / Answer questions / Read research / Connect with colleagues)
3. Has the platform changed your clinical practice? (Yes / No / Unsure) _If yes, how? (Open text)_
4. How satisfied are you with the platform? (Scale 1-5)
5. What would make the platform more useful? (Open text)
6. Net Promoter Score: How likely are you to recommend Uniting Doctors to a colleague? (Scale 0-10)
