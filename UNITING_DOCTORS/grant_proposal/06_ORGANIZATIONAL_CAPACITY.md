## 4. Organizational Infrastructure

### 4.1 Legal Structure

**Entity Type**: Public Benefit Corporation (PBC) or B Corporation

**Rationale**:

- **Mission protection**: Legal obligation to balance profit and social impact
- **Flexibility**: Can accept grants, investments, and donations
- **Credibility**: Signals commitment to social mission
- **Investor alignment**: Attracts mission-aligned investors

**Alternative Considered**: 501(c)(3) nonprofit

- **Pros**: Tax-deductible donations, grant eligibility
- **Cons**: Restricted revenue models, slower decision-making, less attractive to top tech talent (equity compensation limited)

**Chosen Model**: For-profit PBC initially, with potential conversion to nonprofit in future once sustainable

---

**Composition** (Target: 5-7 members):

## 6. Organizational Risks & Mitigation

### 6.1 Key Person Risk (Founder Dependency)

**Risk**: Project depends heavily on Mark Egly (founder)

**Mitigation**:

1. **Strong #2**: Hire experienced CTO and CMO (distributed leadership)
2. **Medical Advisory Board**: Clinical guidance doesn't depend on founder
3. **Documentation**: Knowledge transfer (architecture docs, decision logs)
4. **Succession plan**: Identified potential interim CEO (Board member)
5. **Key person insurance**: $1M-2M policy on founder (Scenario C)

---

### 6.2 Talent Recruitment Risk

**Risk**: Difficult to recruit top healthcare and tech talent on startup budget

**Mitigation**:

1. **Mission-driven recruitment**: Target purpose-driven candidates (willing to take lower salary for impact)
2. **Equity compensation**: Offer meaningful equity (10-year vision, not 2-year exit)
3. **Remote-first**: Access global talent pool (not limited to expensive cities)
4. **Flexible arrangements**: Part-time options for physicians (maintain clinical practice)
5. **Advisory Board**: Leverage network for recruitment referrals

---

### 6.3 Capacity Constraints

**Risk**: Small team may struggle to deliver ambitious roadmap

**Mitigation**:

1. **Prioritization**: Focus on core features first (MVP approach)
2. **Agile methodology**: Iterative development, adapt to capacity
3. **Contractors**: Use specialized contractors for specific needs (design, security audits)
4. **Partnerships**: Leverage partners' resources (Alpha-1 Foundation provides content)
5. **Community contributions**: Power users help with moderation, content
